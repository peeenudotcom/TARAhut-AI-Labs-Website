## DAY 28: Field Work Day 1 -- Guided Sprint

- Scout the market area: identify busy commercial streets with 50+ businesses

- Print student ID cards or carry TARAhut AI Labs letterhead for credibility

Quality control happens NOW, not after delivery. | This is the most important work session in the entire program. Every deliverable must be client-ready quality. Trainer should personally review every student's work before they send it to their client. |

## DAY 33: Deliver, Close, and Collect

### Learning Objectives
- Complete and hand over the first client deliverables
- Conduct a professional client handover meeting
- Collect first payment (the defining moment of the program)
- Handle client feedback and revision requests gracefully

### Minute-by-Minute Breakdown

| Time | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:05 | Warm-Up | "Aaj ka din tumhari zindagi ka sabse important din ho sakta hai. Aaj tum pehli baar apni mehnat ka paisa kamaoge. AI se. Khud se. Professional ban ke." | Build the moment. This is the emotional peak of the program. |
| 0:05-0:15 | Delivery Check | Trainer reviews every student's deliverables: (1) Quality check: designs professional? Captions error-free? GMB description optimized?, (2) Packaging check: delivery document organized? Easy for client to understand?, (3) Invoice ready: amount correct, payment details clear? Students fix any issues NOW. Nothing goes to a client that isn't professional quality. | Be brutal on quality today. "Yeh post client ko nahi bhej sakte. Color match nahi ho raha. Canva mein fix karo, 5 minute mein." Better to delay 10 minutes than deliver sloppy work. |
| 0:15-0:30 | Instruction | **The Handover Meeting.** Teach the 15-minute client handover structure: (1) "Thank you for choosing us" (30 sec -- gratitude), (2) "Here's what we created" (5 min -- walk through each deliverable), (3) "Here's how to use it" (3 min -- explain posting schedule, how to share content), (4) "Your next month's plan" (2 min -- preview what's coming if it's a retainer), (5) "Feedback?" (2 min -- ask if they like it, any changes needed), (6) "Here's your invoice" (1 min -- share invoice, request payment), (7) "One request" (1 min -- ask for a Google review and 2 referrals). Role-play: trainer does a mock handover with a student acting as client. | The handover is where you earn trust for the NEXT month. A sloppy handover loses the client. | 0:30-0:50 | **HANDOVER EXECUTION** | Students who have in-person meetings: go to the client's business with deliverables ready on phone/laptop. Trainer accompanies the first 2 students for support. Students doing remote handover: WhatsApp video call from class. Trainer listens and coaches. Students without clients: work on proposals for remaining warm contacts OR assist classmates with their handovers (learning by observation). | If a client asks for revisions: "Bilkul, main kal tak fix karke bhej dunga." Never argue with a client about their preferences. Make the change and move on. |
| 0:50-1:00 | **PAYMENT COLLECTION** | Students who completed handover: send invoice and request payment. In-person: "Aapka invoice yeh raha. UPI se kar sakte hain abhi, ya bank transfer bhi chalega." Remote: share invoice on WhatsApp with payment link/UPI ID. **The moment a student receives their first payment: screenshot it.** This is their proof of income for Demo Day. | Some clients will say "kal bhejta hoon." That's okay. Don't pressure. "Ji bilkul, kal tak aa jaaye toh achha hoga kyunki main aapka next month ka plan banane waala hoon." |
| 1:00-1:10 | Break + Celebration | Any payments received? If YES: ring a bell (or clap, or play a song). "₹[amount] earned by [name]! First income!" Even if it's ₹5,000, celebrate. 3 takeaways: (1) Delivery quality = client retention, (2) Always collect payment on delivery day, (3) One client today, five clients next month. **Homework:** Complete all pending revisions. Follow up on all unpaid invoices. Start thinking about Demo Day presentation (Day 35). **Exit ticket:** "Aaj kya deliver kiya, kya payment mili, aur next client kaise laoge?" | |

## DAY 34: Scale and Systematize

### Learning Objectives
- Create a growth plan from 1 client to 5 clients
- Build reusable systems: content templates, onboarding checklists, weekly workflows
- Map the path to INR 50K+/month income
- Prepare for sustainable, long-term freelance business

### Minute-by-Minute Breakdown

| Time | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:05 | Warm-Up | "Ek client mila. Bahut achha. Lekin ek client se ghar nahi chalta. 5 clients chahiye. Aaj hum 1 se 5 ka roadmap banayenge." | Transition from "first sale excitement" to "business building" mindset. |
| 0:05-0:15 | Income Celebration | Updated class scoreboard. Every payment that came in since yesterday. Public acknowledgment of every earning student. For students who haven't earned yet: "Tumhara deal pipeline mein hai. Aaj follow-up karenge." No shaming -- only forward motion. | Privately, trainer should know exactly which students need closing help and have a plan for each. |
| 0:15-0:40 | Instruction | **The 1-to-5 Growth Roadmap.** Teach the math: (1) **1 client at ₹5,000/month = ₹5,000.** That's a start. (2) **3 clients at ₹7,500 average = ₹22,500.** Part-time income. (3) **5 clients at ₹10,000 average = ₹50,000.** Full-time income. (4) **8 clients at ₹10,000 average = ₹80,000.** You're now earning more than most MBA grads in Punjab. **Where do clients come from?** (1) Referrals from existing clients (teach the math: 1 happy client refers 2, those 2 refer 2 each = 7 total in 3 months), (2) Market outreach: continue field work 2 days per week, (3) WhatsApp Business presence: showcase your portfolio on Status daily, (4) Local networking: attend business association meetings, chamber of commerce events, (5) Online presence: LinkedIn profile, Google My Business for YOUR freelance service. **The Upsell Ladder.** Start with ₹5K Starter. After 2 months, pitch Growth at ₹10K. After 4 months, pitch Premium at ₹25K. Never upsell a new client. Earn trust first. | Write the income math on the whiteboard. ₹50K/month in Punjab is a very good income. Make this concrete: "Yeh [city] mein bank manager ki salary hai. Tum 20 saal ki age mein kamaa sakte ho." |
| 0:40-0:55 | Instruction | **Systems for Scale.** You can't serve 5 clients by doing everything from scratch each time. Systems: (1) **Content Template Library:** 50 Canva templates (10 per industry: restaurant, salon, clothing, gym, coaching) that you customize per client. Create once, reuse forever. (2) **Client Onboarding Checklist:** standardized Google Form. Every new client fills the same form. No missed information. (3) **Weekly Workflow:** Monday: content creation (batch all clients). Tuesday: design in Canva. Wednesday: scheduling + delivery. Thursday: client check-ins. Friday: admin + new outreach. (4) **Monthly Reporting Template:** Google Slides template. 5 slides per client: overview, content delivered, engagement stats, recommendations, next month plan. (5) **Price List Document:** professional PDF with all packages, add-ons, terms. Share with prospects. | "System bana lo toh 5 clients manage karna utna hi easy hai jitna 2 clients. Bina system ke 3 clients bhi mushkil lagenge." |
| 0:55-1:10 | Guided Practice | **Build Your Systems Kit.** Students create: (1) Content template folder: duplicate 5 of their best Canva designs and strip the branding (make them client-generic), (2) Master onboarding checklist: finalize the Google Form with all necessary questions, (3) Weekly workflow calendar: block their ideal week in Google Calendar, (4) Monthly reporting template: create a 5-slide Google Slides template. Trainer reviews each system as students build. | These systems are what separate a hustler from a business owner. Students who build good systems will scale faster. |
| 1:10-1:15 | Break | | |
| 1:15-1:40 | Independent Work | **90-Day Income Roadmap.** Each student creates their personal income plan: **Month 1 (next 30 days):** target number of clients, target income, daily action plan (how many businesses to approach per week, how many follow-ups). **Month 2:** target clients (existing + new), target income, upsell plan for Month 1 clients. **Month 3:** target clients, target income, first hire or partnership plan (if income > ₹40K). Include: specific market areas to target, business types to focus on, weekly time commitment, tools budget, learning goals. This document is part of the Demo Day presentation. | Students should think big but plan realistically. "₹50K first month mein nahi hoga. ₹15-20K realistic hai. ₹50K Month 3 mein." Help students set achievable targets. |
| 1:40-1:50 | Group Discussion | **Peer Review of Roadmaps.** Students swap roadmaps with a partner. Each partner asks: (1) Is the income target realistic?, (2) Is the daily action plan specific enough?, (3) What's missing?, (4) What's the best idea in this plan? 5-minute swap, 5-minute share with class: "Sabse interesting plan kiska hai?" | Peer review creates accountability. Students are more likely to follow through on a plan they've shared publicly. |
| 1:50-2:00 | Wrap-Up | "Kal Demo Day hai. Tumhe present karna hai: kya seekha, kya kiya, kya kamaya, aur aage kya karoge. 5-7 minute ka presentation. Professional. Confident. Yeh tumhara graduation hai." 3 takeaways: (1) 1 client to 5 clients = systems + consistency, (2) ₹50K/month is achievable in 90 days, (3) Tomorrow you graduate as AI service professionals. **Homework:** Prepare Demo Day presentation (5-7 min). Include: your journey, your portfolio, your client work, your income (screenshot if earned), your 90-day plan. Practice at least twice. Dress professionally tomorrow. **Exit ticket:** "90 days mein tumhara target income kya hai?" | Demo Day is the last session. Set expectations high: "Kal parents aa sakte hain, guests aa sakte hain. Yeh tumhara stage hai." |
