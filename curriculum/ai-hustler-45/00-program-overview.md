## The Three Phases

### Phase 1: Foundation + Tools (Days 1-15, 15 sessions)
Build the AI skill stack. Students go from zero to producing professional-quality content, designs, and videos using AI tools. By Day 15 every student has a 15+ piece portfolio.

### Phase 2: Applied Skills (Days 16-26, 11 sessions)
Turn skills into services. Students visit real businesses, build service packages with pricing, learn to pitch and close, and master the marketing stack local businesses actually need.

### Phase 3: Earning (Days 27-35, 9 sessions)
Make real money. Students approach 25+ businesses, close their first clients, deliver real work, collect payment, and build a system for INR 50K+/month income.

### Buffer Days (Days 36-45, up to 10 days)
Catch-up sessions for students who fell behind, extra field work for those on track, re-examination for anyone who failed Phase exams, and additional client delivery time.

## Session Format (120 minutes)

Every session follows this exact structure. Trainers must not deviate.

| Block | Duration | Activity |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0:00 - 0:05 | **Warm-Up** -- Energy check, motivational story or win share, today's agenda on whiteboard |
| 2 | 0:05 - 0:15 | **Recap + Homework Review** -- Quick-fire Q&A on yesterday, 2-3 students share homework, trainer corrects common mistakes |
| 3 | 0:15 - 0:40 | **Instruction** -- Core teaching (20% theory). Trainer demonstrates live. Screen shared or projected. Bilingual delivery. |
| 4 | 0:40 - 1:10 | **Guided Practice** -- Students follow along step-by-step. Trainer circulates. Every student must complete the exercise before moving on. |
| 5 | 1:10 - 1:15 | **Break** -- Mandatory 5-minute break. Students stand, stretch, refill water. |
| 6 | 1:15 - 1:50 | **Independent Work** -- Students work on assigned tasks alone or in pairs. Trainer provides 1-on-1 help. This is where real learning happens. |
| 7 | 1:50 - 2:00 | **Wrap-Up** -- 3 key takeaways, homework assignment, preview of tomorrow, exit ticket (1 question students answer before leaving) |

## Assessment Structure

### Continuous Assessment (40% of final grade)
- Daily exit tickets (participation, not graded)
- Homework completion tracking (binary: done/not done)
- Portfolio piece quality (rubric-scored)
- Class participation and peer helping

### Phase 1 Exam -- Day 15 (20% of final grade)

| Component | Weight | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Written Quiz | 30% | 30 MCQs, 45 minutes. Covers AI concepts, tool knowledge, prompt engineering principles. |
| Practical Test | 40% | Timed tasks: write 3 prompts, create 1 Canva design, generate 1 video script. 45 minutes. |
| Portfolio Review | 30% | 15+ pieces reviewed by trainer. Rubric: quality, variety, commercial viability. |

**Pass mark: 60%. Students below 60% get remedial sessions during buffer days.**

### Phase 2 Exam -- Day 26 (20% of final grade)

| Component | Weight | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Client Package | 40% | Complete service package for a real business type: proposal, 3-tier pricing, sample deliverables. |
| Live Pitch | 30% | 5-minute pitch to panel (trainer + 1 guest). Scored on clarity, confidence, value articulation. |
| Marketing Execution | 30% | Set up complete marketing presence: GMB profile, WhatsApp Business, 1 week content calendar. |

**Pass mark: 60%. Students must pass the live pitch component separately (minimum 50%).**

### Demo Day -- Day 35 (20% of final grade)

| Component | Weight | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Client Case Study | 40% | 10-minute presentation: the business, what you did, results, payment received. |
| Income Proof | 30% | Screenshot/receipt of actual payment received during program. Any amount counts. |
| 90-Day Plan | 30% | Written plan: target income, client pipeline, services offered, weekly schedule. |

**Students who have not earned any income present their best pitch + plan. They receive a "Provisional" certificate and have 30 days post-program to convert.**

## Franchise Operating Notes

### Batch Scheduling
- Morning batch: 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM
- Afternoon batch: 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM
- Evening batch: 6:00 PM - 8:00 PM
- Maximum 2 batches per trainer per day

### Required Infrastructure
- Room with projector or large screen
- Stable internet (minimum 10 Mbps)
- Whiteboard + markers
- Power strips for student devices
- Printed handouts for Days 1, 15, 26, 35

### Field Work Safety (Days 17, 28-31)
- Students go in pairs minimum
- Trainer provides market area map with assigned zones
- Students share live location on WhatsApp group
- Debrief mandatory within 2 hours of return
- Emergency contact: Center coordinator phone number on every student's phone

### Weekly Trainer Checklist
- [ ] All session materials reviewed for the week
- [ ] Student attendance updated in system
- [ ] Homework submissions tracked
- [ ] Struggling students identified and support plan made
- [ ] Portfolio tracker updated
- [ ] Weekly progress photo/video for social media
- [ ] Parent/guardian update sent (for students under 21)

## Buffer Days (Days 36-45) Usage Guide

| Student Status | Buffer Day Activities |
|---|---|
| On track, income earned | Advanced topics: automation, upselling, niche specialization. Additional field work. |
| On track, no income yet | Intensive field work with trainer accompaniment. Daily accountability calls. |
| Behind on portfolio | Catch-up sessions: complete missing portfolio pieces with 1-on-1 help. |
| Failed Phase 1 or 2 exam | Remedial teaching + re-examination (one attempt allowed). |
| Needs client delivery help | Trainer co-works with student to deliver first client project. |

Buffer days are NOT optional vacation. Every student must attend minimum 5 of 10 buffer days.
