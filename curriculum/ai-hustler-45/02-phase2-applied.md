## DAY 16: Advanced Prompt Engineering -- CRISP Framework

### Learning Objectives
- Master the CRISP advanced prompt framework
- Use chain-of-thought prompting for complex tasks
- Build mega-prompts that produce complete deliverables in one shot

### Minute-by-Minute Breakdown

| Time | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:05 | Warm-Up | "Phase 2 shuru. Ab skill building se business building pe shift karte hain. Pehle 15 din mein tools seekhe, ab un tools se paise kamaoge." New energy, new phase. | Change the room if possible -- rearrange seats. Signal that something new has begun. |
| 0:05-0:15 | Recap | Phase 1 highlights: show best portfolio pieces from the batch. Quick 5-Block Framework refresher. Announce: "Ab hum advanced level pe jaayenge." | |
| 0:15-0:40 | Instruction | **The CRISP Framework.** An evolution of 5-Block for complex, multi-step outputs: **C = Character** -- Detailed persona with expertise, years of experience, specialization. "You are a 15-year veteran digital marketing consultant who specializes in scaling local businesses in North India from ₹0 to ₹50K monthly revenue using AI tools." **R = Request** -- Multi-layered task with sub-tasks. "Create a complete 30-day launch plan including daily tasks, content calendar, budget allocation, and KPI tracking." **I = Information** -- Rich context: business details, target audience, budget, competitors, constraints. **S = Steps** -- Explicit step-by-step instructions for the AI to follow. "Step 1: Analyze the business type. Step 2: Identify 3 target segments..." **P = Polishing** -- Output format, tone, length, examples to follow, things to avoid. Live demo: build a CRISP mega-prompt that produces a complete social media strategy in one response. Show the 5-Block version vs CRISP version -- quality difference is dramatic. | Write CRISP on the whiteboard in big letters. This framework is for complex, high-value deliverables. 5-Block is for quick tasks. CRISP is for packages worth ₹5000+. |
| 0:40-0:55 | Instruction | **Chain-of-Thought Prompting.** Explain: "AI ko sochne ka mauka do, tabhi accha output aayega." Techniques: (1) "Think step by step before answering", (2) "First analyze, then recommend, then create", (3) "Consider 3 different approaches and pick the best one, explaining why." Demo: same task with and without chain-of-thought. Show quality difference. **Mega-Prompts.** Show a 500-word prompt that produces a complete client deliverable: marketing strategy + content calendar + 5 sample posts + posting schedule + budget recommendation. "Ek prompt, poora package. Yeh hai professional level." | Mega-prompts save massive time. A well-crafted mega-prompt can produce in 2 minutes what takes 30 minutes with multiple small prompts. |
| 0:55-1:10 | Guided Practice | **CRISP Challenge.** Students write 3 CRISP mega-prompts: (1) Complete social media strategy for a new restaurant, (2) Customer re-engagement campaign for a salon losing customers, (3) Festival marketing plan for a clothing store (Diwali). Trainer reviews each prompt before students run them. Focus on prompt quality, not output. | Grade each prompt: does it have all 5 CRISP elements? Is each element detailed enough? Common mistake: vague Information block. Push for specifics. |
| 1:10-1:15 | Break | | |
| 1:15-1:50 | Independent Work | **Mega-Prompt Library.** Students build a "Professional Prompt Library" with 5 CRISP mega-prompts for 5 different service types: (1) Full social media strategy, (2) Business launch plan, (3) Monthly content package, (4) Customer retention campaign, (5) Festival/seasonal marketing plan. Each prompt tested, output saved, and rated. | This library becomes a core business asset. Students will use these prompts with real clients. Emphasize: "Yeh tumhari factory hai. Input = client brief, output = deliverable." |
| 1:50-2:00 | Wrap-Up | Best mega-prompt shared. 3 takeaways: (1) CRISP = professional-grade prompting, (2) Chain-of-thought = smarter AI responses, (3) One great mega-prompt can produce a ₹10,000 deliverable. **Homework:** Create 3 more CRISP mega-prompts for service types not covered. Test and refine. **Exit ticket:** "CRISP ke 5 elements kya hain?" | |

## DAY 17: Field Visit -- Understanding Real Business Needs

### Learning Objectives
- Observe real businesses and identify their marketing gaps
- Practice needs assessment conversations
- Understand what business owners actually want vs what they say they want

### Pre-Session Preparation (Trainer Must Complete 1 Day Before)
- Identify a local market area (bazaar, market complex, commercial street)
- Visit personally and identify 5-6 businesses willing to let students observe
- Prepare printed "Business Assessment Forms" (1 per student)
- Pair students into teams of 2-3
- Brief on safety and conduct rules

### Minute-by-Minute Breakdown

| Time | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:05 | Warm-Up | "Aaj classroom se bahar nikalte hain. Real businesses, real owners, real problems. Observe karna hai, sell nahi karna. Seekhna hai." | Set expectations clearly: this is observation, not selling. Students are NOT pitching today. |
| 0:05-0:20 | Instruction | **The Business Assessment Framework.** Teach what to observe: (1) Does the business have a Google listing? (search on phone before visiting), (2) Do they have social media? How active?, (3) What's their signage like? Professional or amateur?, (4) How do they communicate with customers? (WhatsApp? Phone? Walk-in only?), (5) What's their biggest challenge? (ask if they'll talk). **How to approach:** "Namaste ji, hum TARAhut AI Labs se digital marketing seekh rahe hain. Kya hum aapke business ke baare mein kuch sawaal pooch sakte hain? Sirf 5 minute lagega." Practice this intro 3 times as a class. | Role-play the approach in class first. Common student fear: "Business owner gussa ho jayega." Trainer reassures: "Politely approach karo, zyaada tar log acche se baat karte hain. Na bole toh thank you bolo aur aage badho." |
| 0:20-0:30 | Instruction | **The Assessment Form.** Walk through the printed form: Business name, type, location, owner name, contact (if shared), online presence (GMB: Y/N, Instagram: Y/N, Facebook: Y/N, WhatsApp Business: Y/N), current marketing efforts, observed gaps (list 3-5), estimated budget level (₹0, under ₹5K, ₹5-15K, ₹15K+), opportunity rating (1-5), notes. | Every student gets 3 printed forms (for 3 businesses). They must complete at least 2 during the field visit. |
| 0:30-0:35 | Logistics | Pair assignments. Market area map distribution. Safety rules: (1) Stay in pairs, (2) Share live location on WhatsApp, (3) Be polite always, (4) Don't enter private areas, (5) Return to meeting point by designated time. Emergency contact: trainer's phone. | Check that every student has trainer's phone number saved. Every pair has at least 1 charged phone with data. |
| 0:35-1:35 | **FIELD VISIT** | **60 minutes in the market.** Students in pairs visit 3-4 businesses each. They observe, fill assessment forms, take photos of signage/storefront (with permission), check Google/Instagram for each business on their phones. If business owner is willing to talk, ask the 5 assessment questions. If not, observe and fill what they can. Trainer circulates between pairs, provides guidance, handles any issues. | Trainer should NOT hover over students but should be visible and reachable. Visit each pair at least once. Common issue: students cluster together and don't spread out. Push them to different parts of the market. |
| 1:35-1:45 | Return + Break | All students return to meeting point (classroom or designated area). 5-minute break. Headcount. | Strict return time. If a pair is late, trainer calls immediately. |
| 1:45-1:55 | Debrief | Each pair presents: (1) How many businesses visited, (2) Biggest surprise, (3) Most common gap found, (4) Best opportunity spotted. Quick round: 1 minute per pair. Trainer writes common themes on whiteboard. | Common findings students will report: "Kisi ka bhi accha Instagram nahi hai," "GMB kisi ka nahi tha," "Sab WhatsApp use karte hain but Business wala nahi." These insights are gold. |
| 1:55-2:00 | Wrap-Up | 3 takeaways: (1) Real businesses have real problems you can solve, (2) Most local businesses have terrible online presence -- that's your opportunity, (3) Understanding the client comes before selling to the client. **Homework:** Complete all assessment forms neatly. For your best opportunity business, write a 1-page "What I would do for this business" plan using AI tools. **Exit ticket:** "Aaj ki field visit mein sabse bada seekh kya mila?" | |

### Materials Needed
- Printed Business Assessment Forms (3 per student)
- Market area map (printed or shared digitally)
- Student pair assignments (printed list)
- Safety briefing card (1 per student)

### Portfolio Pieces
- 2-3 completed Business Assessment Forms
- "What I Would Do" plan for 1 business (homework)

| Time | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:05 | Warm-Up | "Kal tumne dekha ki businesses ko kya chahiye. Aaj hum banayenge ki tum kya bechoge." Transition from observation to action. | |
| 0:05-0:15 | Recap | Field visit debrief: 3 students share their "What I Would Do" homework plans. Class discussion: are these realistic? What's missing? Trainer adds professional touches. | |
| 0:15-0:40 | Instruction | **The 3-Tier Service Model.** Explain: every service has 3 levels. **STARTER PACKAGE -- ₹5,000/month:** (1) 12 social media posts/month (3/week), (2) Basic graphic design for each post, (3) Caption writing in Hindi/English, (4) 1 festival greeting video, (5) Monthly content calendar. **GROWTH PACKAGE -- ₹10,000/month:** Everything in Starter PLUS: (1) 20 posts/month (5/week), (2) Google My Business setup + weekly updates, (3) WhatsApp Business setup + broadcast templates, (4) 2 marketing videos/month, (5) Monthly performance review. **PREMIUM PACKAGE -- ₹25,000/month:** Everything in Growth PLUS: (1) 30 posts/month (daily), (2) Meta Business Suite ad management, (3) 4 marketing videos/month, (4) Customer response management, (5) Competitor analysis quarterly, (6) Dedicated WhatsApp support. **Pricing Psychology:** Why 3 tiers work -- anchoring effect. This is the pricing framework they'll use for months. |
| 0:40-0:55 | Instruction | **Writing Professional Proposals.** Show a real proposal template: (1) Cover page with client business name, (2) "About Us" (your services), (3) "Understanding Your Business" (what you learned in assessment), (4) "Our Recommendation" (the 3 tiers), (5) "What You Get" (detailed deliverables per tier), (6) "Investment" (pricing), (7) "Next Steps" (how to start), (8) Terms and conditions. Use ChatGPT with CRISP to generate a proposal in 5 minutes. Live demo. | Show the finished proposal on screen. It should look professional -- clean formatting, no typos, specific to the business. This is what clients see. First impression matters. |
| 0:55-1:10 | Guided Practice | **Proposal Creation.** Using their field visit assessment, each student creates a proposal for 1 real business they visited: (1) ChatGPT CRISP prompt to generate the proposal (10 min), (2) Customize with specific details from assessment form (5 min). | 1:10-1:15 | Break | | |
| 1:15-1:50 | Independent Work | **Complete Service Package Kit.** Students create their complete "business in a box": (1) Service menu (1-page: 3 tiers with pricing), (2) Full proposal template (customizable for any business), (3) Client onboarding checklist (what you need from the client to start: logo, photos, brand colors, target audience, business hours, menu/price list), (4) Sample deliverables folder (3 sample posts, 1 sample video, 1 sample GMB description). All in a Google Doc / Google Drive folder. | This is the student's business identity. From today, they are not students doing homework -- they are professionals building their service business. Language shift matters. |
| 1:50-2:00 | Wrap-Up | 1 student presents their service menu. 3 takeaways: (1) 3 tiers = professional pricing, (2) Proposals win clients, not WhatsApp messages, (3) Your "business in a box" is ready -- you just need clients. **Homework:** Finalize proposal template. Create service menu as a Canva-designed PDF (looks professional). **Exit ticket:** "₹10K package mein kya kya milega client ko?" | |

## DAY 19: Service Packages -- Contracts, Onboarding, and Client Management

### Learning Objectives
- Create simple service contracts
- Build a client onboarding workflow

### Minute-by-Minute Breakdown

| Time | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:05 | Warm-Up | Horror story: "A freelancer did ₹25,000 worth of work. Client said 'mujhe pasand nahi aaya' and refused to pay. Kyun? Because there was no contract." | Real-world cautionary tale. Contracts protect both sides. |

"Simple aur clear rakhna hai, waqeel wali language nahi chahiye." |
| 0:35-0:45 | Instruction | **Client Onboarding Workflow.** Step-by-step: (1) Client says "yes" to a package, (2) Send contract via WhatsApp/email, (3) Collect advance payment (UPI/bank transfer), (4) Send onboarding form: business details, logos, photos, brand preferences, (5) Schedule kick-off call (15 minutes), (6) Deliver first draft within 48 hours, (7) Collect feedback, revise, deliver final. | 1:10-1:15 | Break | | |

Full simulation: (1) Provider presents service menu, (2) Client asks questions/objections, (3) Provider recommends a package, (4) Client says yes, (5) Provider walks through contract, (6) Provider sends onboarding form, (7) Provider explains timeline. | 1:50-2:00 | Wrap-Up | Debrief role-play: what felt hard? What felt natural? 3 takeaways: (1) Contracts are non-negotiable -- always use one, (2) Onboarding forms save time and set expectations, (3) Practice makes confident. **Homework:** Finalize contract template, onboarding form, and client tracker. Unke customers online se aa rahe hain." **2. "Bahut mehnga hai" (Too expensive)** -- Response: Break down per-day cost. "₹10,000/month = ₹333/day. Agar ek bhi extra customer aaye toh recover ho jaata hai." **3. "Mera beta/bhatija kar leta hai" (My nephew does it)** -- Response: "Bilkul, unki help bhi ho jayegi. Main professional level pe le jaaunga. Dekhiye yeh sample..." Show portfolio. **4. "Pehle bhi kisi ne kiya, kaam nahi aaya" (Tried before, didn't work)** -- Response: "Samajh sakta hoon. Kya main pooch sakta hoon kya exactly kiya tha? Main AI tools use karta hoon jo 2024 mein nahi the." **5. "Sochke batata hoon" (Let me think about it)** -- Response: "Zaroor. Main ek free sample bana ke bhej deta hoon -- 1 social media post aapke business ke liye. Kal WhatsApp pe bhejunga." **6. "Abhi budget nahi hai" (No budget right now)** -- Response: "Start karte hain ₹5,000 wale plan se. 1 mahina dekhte hain results. Achha lage toh badhayenge." **7. "Online marketing kaam nahi karta yahan" (Online marketing doesn't work here)** -- Response: "Aapke kitne customers WhatsApp use karte hain? Sab? Toh woh online hain." **8. "Main khud seekh lunga" (I'll learn myself)** -- Response: "Zaroor seekh sakte hain. But kya aapke paas roz 2 ghante hain content banane ke liye? Main yeh kaam karta hoon taaki aap apna business chalayein." **9. "Kya guarantee hai?" (What's the guarantee?)** -- Response: "Guarantee yeh hai ki aapka online presence professional ho jayega. Main pehle mahine ka review karunga. Pasand nahi aaya toh aage nahi badhenge." **10. "Free mein karo, phir dekhunga" (Do it free first)** -- Response: "Main ek free sample de sakta hoon -- 1 post. But professional service ke liye investment chahiye, jaise aap apni shop mein karte hain." | Role-play each objection. Trainer plays the business owner. Call on random students to respond. This MUST be interactive. Students should practice speaking, not just listening. Write all 10 objections on the whiteboard. They stay up until Day 26 exam. |

| 1:10-1:15 | Break | | |
| 1:15-1:50 | Independent Work | **The Perfect Sales Conversation.** Students write out their complete sales script: (1) Introduction (who you are, why you're here), (2) Discovery questions (5 questions to understand the business), (3) Problem identification (what you observed), (4) Solution presentation (show portfolio, explain packages), (5) Pricing presentation (3 tiers), (6) Objection handling (top 3 responses for likely objections), (7) Close (next steps, contract, advance). Then: pair practice. Each pair runs the full conversation 2 times (switching roles). Trainer circulates and coaches. | By end of this session, every student should be able to run a 10-minute sales conversation from memory. Script is a safety net, not a teleprompter. |
| 1:50-2:00 | Wrap-Up | 3 takeaways: (1) Objections are normal -- they mean the person is interested, (2) Always offer a free sample -- it opens doors, (3) Confidence comes from preparation and practice. **Homework:** Practice the sales conversation with a family member or friend.
