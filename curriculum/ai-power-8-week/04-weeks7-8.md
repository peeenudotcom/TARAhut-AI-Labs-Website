## SESSION 25: AI-Optimized Resume & Cover Letter Building

**Date:** Week 7, Day 1
**Theme:** Building a professional resume and cover letter using AI tools
**Tools:** ChatGPT, Claude, Canva (resume templates), Google Docs

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Min | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00 | 5 | Warm-Up | "The Resume Reality Check" -- Show 2 resumes on the projector side-by-side. Resume A: generic, no keywords, objective says "I want to work in a good company." Resume B: AI-optimized, tailored, with quantifiable achievements. Ask: "Which one gets the interview?" Obvious answer. "By the end of today, you'll have Resume B." | Use real (anonymized) examples if possible. The contrast should be dramatic and slightly funny. |
| 0:05 | 10 | Recap | 6-week journey recap: "You've learned AI content creation, design, video, business plans, automations, and chatbots. This week, we package ALL of that into your career toolkit." Show the Week 7-8 roadmap: Resume --> LinkedIn --> Freelancing --> Capstone --> Demo Day. "The finish line is in sight." | Build momentum. Students should feel the programme coming together. |
| 0:15 | 25 | Instruction | **Part 1: Why Most Resumes Get Rejected (8 min).** The truth: recruiters spend 6-10 seconds per resume. ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems) filter 75% of resumes before a human sees them. What kills a resume: generic objectives, no keywords, poor formatting, spelling errors, irrelevant information. What works: tailored to the job, keyword-rich, quantifiable achievements, clean format. **Part 2: AI Resume Building -- Live Demo (10 min).** Step 1: Find a job posting (use Naukri.com or LinkedIn). Step 2: Paste the job description into ChatGPT. Prompt: "Analyze this job description. Extract the top 10 keywords and skills the employer is looking for." Step 3: Prompt: "I am a BBA student from Punjab with the following experiences: [list]. Write a professional resume tailored to this job description. Include a professional summary, education, skills, projects, and achievements. Use the keywords you extracted." Step 4: Show the output. Highlight how AI matched keywords, structured sections, and wrote professional language. **Part 3: Cover Letter in 2 Minutes (7 min).** Prompt: "Write a cover letter for [job title] at [company]. I am [brief background]. Highlight my AI skills, project experience from this 8-week program, and enthusiasm for the role. Keep it under 250 words." Show how to customize for different jobs by changing 2-3 lines. | Emphasize: AI writes the first draft, YOU customize it. Never submit an AI-generated resume without reviewing and personalizing it. This is a life skill, not just a course exercise. |
| 0:40 | 30 | Guided Practice | **Exercise 1: Build YOUR Resume (20 min).** Step 1: Students find a real job posting relevant to their field (BBA --> marketing/operations, B.Com --> accounting/finance, BA --> content/communications). Step 2: Extract keywords using ChatGPT. Step 3: Generate a tailored resume using the prompt taught. Step 4: Copy into a Google Doc. Review and personalize: fix any AI hallucinations, add real details, remove anything untrue. **Exercise 2: Design the Resume (10 min).** Open Canva. Search "resume template." Pick a clean, professional template (trainer recommends 2-3 specific templates). Copy the AI-generated content into the Canva template. Export as PDF. | CRITICAL: Tell students to NEVER lie on a resume. AI can exaggerate -- they must fact-check every line. If AI says "Led a team of 15," but they only worked in a group of 4, fix it. Honesty is non-negotiable. |
| 1:10 | 5 | Break | | |
| 1:15 | 35 | Independent Work | **Resume Refinement + Cover Letter (35 min).** **Phase 1 (15 min):** Resume polish. Use Claude: "Review this resume for a [job title] role. Identify weaknesses, suggest improvements, and check for ATS compatibility." Make improvements based on Claude's feedback. Use Grammarly to check grammar and tone. **Phase 2 (10 min):** Write a cover letter using ChatGPT for the same job. Personalize it -- add one specific thing about the company (research using Perplexity: "What is [company name] known for? What are their recent achievements?"). **Phase 3 (10 min):** Peer review. Swap resumes with a partner. Each person gives 3 pieces of feedback using the prompt: "Review this resume for a [job title]. Give 3 specific suggestions to improve it." Students revise based on peer feedback. | The peer review adds a human layer. Students catch things AI misses -- "This doesn't sound like you" or "You forgot to mention the chatbot project." |
| 1:50 | 10 | Wrap-Up | 3 students share their before (what they had before today) vs. after resume. Class votes on "Most Improved Resume." Save the resume PDF -- it goes into the portfolio website in Week 8. Homework: Write a second cover letter for a different job. Save both cover letters. Preview: "Tomorrow -- LinkedIn. The most important professional platform. And most of your profiles are either empty or terrible. We fix that." | The "most of your profiles are terrible" line should be said with humor and love. It's true and they know it. |

**Date:** Week 7, Day 2
**Theme:** Building a professional LinkedIn presence with AI assistance
**Tools:** LinkedIn, ChatGPT, Claude, Canva (banner design), Leonardo AI (headshot enhancement)

"Which person would YOU hire?" Discussion. | Pick profiles from Indian professionals so students can relate. The outstanding profile should be aspirational but achievable. |

Endorse 3 classmates for skills on LinkedIn. Preview: "Tomorrow -- we turn your skills into MONEY. Freelancing with Fiverr and Upwork." | The endorsement homework builds community and boosts profiles simultaneously. |

## SESSION 27: Freelancing with AI -- Fiverr/Upwork Gigs

**Date:** Week 7, Day 3
**Theme:** Setting up freelancing profiles and creating service offerings using AI skills
**Tools:** Fiverr, Upwork, ChatGPT, Canva, all previously learned tools

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Min | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00 | 5 | Warm-Up | "Money Story" -- Trainer shares a real example: "Meet Anjali from Amritsar. She's 22, did her BBA, and now earns INR 40,000/month from Fiverr doing social media content using Canva and ChatGPT. She works 4 hours a day from home." Show a real Fiverr profile (trainer's own or a public example). "You have the same skills as Anjali. This session, you build your profile." | Use real, verifiable examples. Indian freelancers on Fiverr are easy to find. The income figure should be realistic, not inflated. INR 20,000-50,000/month is achievable for active AI freelancers. |
| 0:05 | 10 | Recap | Resume + LinkedIn recap. "You have a resume and a LinkedIn profile. Those are for jobs. Today we build something for income you control -- freelancing." Freelancing vs. job: no boss, no fixed hours, you choose clients, income scales with effort. Reality check: freelancing is not easy money. It takes effort, consistency, and patience. | Balance hype with reality. Don't oversell freelancing, but don't undersell it either. |
| 0:15 | 25 | Instruction | **Part 1: 5 AI Gigs You Can Offer TODAY (10 min).** Based on what students have already learned: (1) **Content Writing** -- blog posts, articles, product descriptions using ChatGPT + Grammarly. Average price: INR 500-2,000 per article. (2) **Social Media Management** -- content calendars, post creation, caption writing using Canva + ChatGPT. Average price: INR 5,000-15,000/month per client. (3) **Chatbot Building** -- customer service bots using Botpress. Average price: INR 5,000-15,000 per bot. (4) **Presentation Design** -- pitch decks, business presentations using Gamma AI + Canva. Average price: INR 1,000-5,000 per deck. (5) **Data Research & Reports** -- market research, competitor analysis using Perplexity + Claude + Google Sheets. Average price: INR 2,000-8,000 per report. **Part 2: Fiverr Walkthrough (8 min).** Open Fiverr (fiverr.com). Sign up as a seller. Profile anatomy: display name, description, skills, languages, education. Creating a "Gig": title, category, description, pricing tiers (Basic/Standard/Premium), delivery time, gig image. Live demo: create a gig for "I will create social media content using AI tools." **Part 3: Upwork Walkthrough (7 min).** Open Upwork (upwork.com). Sign up as a freelancer. Key difference: on Fiverr, buyers come to you. On Upwork, you apply ("propose") to jobs. Profile overview, skills, portfolio, hourly rate. Show how to search for jobs and write proposals. | Be transparent about platform challenges: Fiverr is competitive, first gigs are hard to get, reviews matter enormously. Teach students to start with low prices to build reviews, then increase. |
| 0:40 | 30 | Guided Practice | **Exercise 1: Fiverr Profile Setup (15 min).** ALL students create a Fiverr seller account together. Trainer walks through every step on projector: profile photo (use the professional one from LinkedIn), description (use ChatGPT: "Write a Fiverr seller description for someone who offers AI-powered content writing, social media management, and chatbot building. I am a trained AI tools specialist from Punjab, India."), skills, languages (English, Hindi, Punjabi -- this is an advantage for local and diaspora clients). **Exercise 2: First Gig Creation (15 min).** Students create their first Fiverr gig. Choose from the 5 gig types taught. Trainer demos on projector: Title: "I will create 30 days of social media content using AI tools." Category: Social Media Marketing. Description: use ChatGPT to write a compelling description. Pricing: Basic (INR 1,500 / 5 posts), Standard (INR 3,500 / 15 posts), Premium (INR 6,000 / 30 posts). Gig image: create in Canva (professional, eye-catching, shows what you offer). | Fiverr signup requires phone verification. Some students may face issues. Have a backup plan: they can complete signup at home and focus on gig content creation in class. |
| 1:10 | 5 | Break | | |
| 1:15 | 35 | Independent Work | **Gig Building + Portfolio Samples (35 min).** **Phase 1 (15 min):** Create 2 more Fiverr gigs (total 3 gigs). Each student should have gigs in at least 2 different categories. Use ChatGPT to write gig titles and descriptions. Use Canva to create gig images. **Phase 2 (20 min):** Create 3 portfolio samples -- one for each gig. Sample 1 (Content Writing): write a 500-word blog post using ChatGPT on a topic relevant to Punjab businesses. Sample 2 (Social Media): create 3 Instagram posts using Canva for a fictional local business. Sample 3 (choose one): a presentation (Gamma), a chatbot screenshot (Botpress), or a research report (Perplexity + Claude). Save all samples as PDFs. These become portfolio pieces for the gig and for the portfolio website in Week 8. | Portfolio samples are CRITICAL. A Fiverr gig without samples gets no orders. Spend time here. Quality matters more than speed. |
| 1:50 | 10 | Wrap-Up | 2 students share their Fiverr gig on projector. Class gives feedback: "Would you buy this gig?" Honest discussion about pricing, presentation, and trust signals. "Your gigs are live. Your portfolio samples exist. Tomorrow, we learn how to actually get clients and make money." Homework: Complete all 3 Fiverr gigs if not done. Sign up on Upwork and complete the profile. Create 1 more portfolio sample. Preview: "Tomorrow -- pricing strategy, landing your first client, and the Upwork proposal game." | |

## SESSION 28: How to Price Your Services & Land Your First Client

**Date:** Week 7, Day 4
**Theme:** Pricing strategy, client acquisition, and deliverable submission
**Tools:** Fiverr, Upwork, ChatGPT, Google Docs

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Min | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00 | 5 | Warm-Up | "The Pricing Game" -- Show 3 Fiverr gigs for the same service (social media content) at different prices: INR 500, INR 3,000, INR 10,000. Ask: "Which one would you buy? Why?" Discussion about perceived value. "The most expensive one has better reviews, better samples, and a more professional profile. Price is about trust, not just money." | This exercise teaches that low price does not equal more sales. Value perception matters. |
| 0:05 | 10 | Recap | Fiverr gigs and portfolio recap. "Who has all 3 gigs live? Who completed their Upwork profile?" Troubleshoot signup issues. Quick check: all students should have at least 1 live Fiverr gig and 2 portfolio samples by now. | 10 minutes of troubleshooting for anyone stuck on verification, gig approval, or profile issues. |
| 0:15 | 25 | Instruction | **Part 1: Pricing Strategy (10 min).** The Beginner's Pricing Framework: **Phase 1 -- Build Reviews (Month 1-2):** Price LOW. Goal is 5-star reviews, not profit. Content writing: INR 500-1,000. Social media pack: INR 1,500-3,000. Chatbot: INR 3,000-5,000. Accept every reasonable order. Over-deliver: give more than promised. **Phase 2 -- Raise Prices (Month 3-4):** After 10+ reviews, increase prices by 50-100%. Add premium tiers. Start saying no to low-value clients. **Phase 3 -- Premium (Month 5+):** Charge market rate. Specialize in a niche. Build repeat client relationships. Key rule: your first 10 clients are not for money, they're for proof. **Part 2: Landing Your First Client (10 min).** Strategy 1: Friends & Family. Tell everyone you know: "I build social media content using AI. Want me to do your business page for free/cheap?" First 3 clients will come from your network. Strategy 2: Fiverr Buyer Requests. Check daily. Write proposals using ChatGPT: "Write a Fiverr buyer request response for [project type]. I have experience with AI tools for content creation. I can deliver in [timeline]." Strategy 3: Upwork Proposals. Search for small jobs ($10-50). Apply with personalized proposals. Key: mention something specific about the client's project. Strategy 4: Local businesses in Punjab. Walk into shops with your portfolio. "Main aapke liye social media content bana sakda haan. Ek free trial dena chahoge?" **Part 3: Client Communication (5 min).** Golden rules: respond within 2 hours, set clear expectations, deliver on time, ask for a review after delivery. Use ChatGPT for professional client messages: "Write a professional message to a client confirming their order for [service] with delivery by [date]." | The local business strategy is the most realistic for Punjab students. Fiverr/Upwork are competitive globally, but local businesses have zero competition and high trust. Encourage students to start local. |
| 0:40 | 30 | Guided Practice | **Exercise 1: Upwork Profile + First Proposal (15 min).** Students who haven't completed Upwork: finish the profile now. Students who have: search for 3 relevant jobs on Upwork. Pick the most realistic one. Write a proposal using ChatGPT: "Write an Upwork proposal for this job: [paste job description]. I have skills in [list AI tools]. I completed a professional AI training program. I can deliver [what] by [when]." Customize the proposal: add one personal touch, mention something specific about the job. **Exercise 2: The Pitch Deck (15 min).** Create a 1-page "Services Overview" using Canva or Gamma: Your name, photo, tagline. 3-5 services you offer with prices. 2-3 portfolio sample previews. Contact info (email, WhatsApp, Fiverr link). This is what you show to local businesses. Think of it as your business card on steroids. | The 1-page services overview is incredibly practical. Students can print it, WhatsApp it, or show it on their phone when talking to local businesses. |
| 1:10 | 5 | Break | | |
| 1:15 | 35 | Independent Work | **Deliverable Finalization + Submission (35 min).** **Phase 1 (10 min):** Finalize all 3 portfolio samples. Ensure quality: proofread content writing samples with Grammarly, check design samples for alignment and consistency, test chatbot screenshots for clarity. **Phase 2 (10 min):** Finalize Fiverr profile and all 3 gigs. Ensure each gig has: compelling title, detailed description, correct pricing tiers, professional gig image, at least 1 portfolio sample attached. Take screenshots of the live Fiverr profile and gigs. **Phase 3 (10 min):** Organize Google Drive folder: "Week 7 -- [Name] -- Career & Freelancing." Include: Resume PDF (from Session 25), cover letter (from Session 25), LinkedIn profile screenshot + URL, Fiverr profile screenshot + URL, 3 Fiverr gig screenshots, 3 portfolio samples (PDFs), Services overview 1-pager, Upwork profile screenshot + URL (if complete). **Phase 4 (5 min):** Submit via Google Form. Update Notion portfolio. | Core requirement: live Fiverr profile + 3 portfolio samples. Resume, LinkedIn, and services overview are strong bonuses. |
| 1:50 | 10 | Wrap-Up | "Freelancer Showcase" -- 3 students present their complete freelancing setup: Fiverr profile, gigs, portfolio samples, and services overview. Class votes on "Most Professional Profile" and "Most Creative Gig." Reflection moment: "6 weeks ago, you didn't know what a prompt was. Today you have a professional resume, a LinkedIn profile, live freelancing gigs, and portfolio samples. Think about that." Preview Week 8: "Next week is the grand finale. You choose a capstone project, build a portfolio website, and present everything to real people -- business owners, parents, and our team. Demo Day is coming. Let's make it legendary." | The emotional reflection is important. Many students won't realize how far they've come unless you explicitly point it out. Let it breathe for a moment. |
