# WEEK 3: AI FOR CONTENT & SOCIAL MEDIA

> Goal: Students become AI-powered content creators. They learn writing, image generation, video editing, and personal branding -- all with AI tools.

**Week 3 Deliverable:** 1-Week Content Calendar + 5 Published Posts (on any platform)

## SESSION 9: AI Writing Tools -- From Blank Page to Viral Post

**Date:** Week 3, Day 1
**Theme:** AI-powered copywriting and content writing
**Tools:** ChatGPT, Claude, Grammarly AI, Canva (text features)

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Min | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00 | 5 | Warm-Up | Show 3 Instagram captions. Students vote: which is AI-written? Twist: ALL 3 are AI-assisted. Lesson: AI writing is already everywhere. | Surprises students. Opens their minds to content creation. |
| 0:05 | 10 | Recap | Quick recap of Weeks 1-2. "We learned to talk to AI and use it for studies. Now we use it to CREATE things people actually see." Ask: "Who has a personal Instagram/YouTube? Who posts regularly?" Gauge the room. | Most will have accounts but post rarely. This week changes that. |
| 0:15 | 25 | Instruction | **Part 1: Types of Content Writing (8 min).** Social media captions, blog posts, ad copy, email newsletters, YouTube scripts, WhatsApp marketing messages. Show one example of each. **Part 2: AI Writing Formulas (10 min).** AIDA formula: Attention-Interest-Desire-Action. PAS formula: Problem-Agitate-Solve. Show prompts: "Write an Instagram caption for a Punjabi restaurant using the AIDA formula. Make it funny and include emojis." Demo on ChatGPT -- show how specifying the formula dramatically improves output. **Part 3: Content Repurposing (7 min).** One idea, 5 formats. Demo: take one topic ("Why Amritsar street food is the best") and turn it into: (1) Instagram caption, (2) Twitter thread, (3) YouTube script intro, (4) WhatsApp status, (5) LinkedIn post. All using AI in under 5 minutes. | The repurposing demo is a jaw-dropper. Students see the power of creating once and publishing everywhere. |
| 0:40 | 30 | Guided Practice | **Exercise 1 (10 min):** Pick any local business in your area (chai shop, boutique, tuition centre). Write 3 different social media captions for it using: (a) AIDA formula, (b) PAS formula, (c) storytelling approach. Use ChatGPT or Claude. **Exercise 2 (10 min):** Content repurposing practice. Take one of your captions and ask AI to convert it into: Instagram post, LinkedIn post, and WhatsApp forward message. **Exercise 3 (10 min):** Tone shifting. Take one caption and ask AI to rewrite it in 4 tones: professional, funny, emotional, Gen-Z slang. Compare the outputs. | Walk around and help students pick local businesses they actually know. Real examples make this more engaging. |
| 1:10 | 5 | Break | | |
| 1:15 | 35 | Independent Work | **Create a Content Calendar Template (35 min):** Using Google Sheets or a simple table, create a 7-day content calendar with columns: Day, Platform, Content Type, Topic, Caption (AI-assisted), Hashtags. Fill in at least 3 days (finish remaining days as homework). **For each day:** write the actual caption using AI (not just the topic). Must include at least 2 different platforms and 3 different content types across the week. | This starts the Week 3 deliverable. Provide a template on screen for students to copy. |
| 1:50 | 10 | Wrap-Up | 2 students share their content calendar concept (which business or personal brand they're creating for). Best concept gets "Content Creator of the Day" shoutout. Homework: Complete all 7 days of the content calendar. Preview Session 10: "Tomorrow we make the posts LOOK amazing -- AI image generation!" | |

## SESSION 10: AI Image Generation -- Canva AI, Leonardo AI, Ideogram

**Date:** Week 3, Day 2
**Theme:** Creating professional visuals with AI
**Tools:** Canva AI, Leonardo AI, Ideogram

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Min | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00 | 5 | Warm-Up | Show 4 images: 2 real photos, 2 AI-generated. Students guess which is which. Most will get it wrong. "AI can now create images that look THIS real." | Use impressive AI images. Landscapes or product photos work best. |
| 0:05 | 10 | Recap | Review content writing formulas (AIDA, PAS). Check: "Who completed their 7-day content calendar?" Quick scan. Reminder: it's due end of this week with 5 actual posts. | |
| 0:15 | 25 | Instruction | **Part 1: Canva AI (10 min).** Sign up for Canva (canva.com). Show free plan features. Demo: (a) Magic Design -- describe what you want, Canva creates it. (b) Text to Image -- generate custom images. (c) Magic Write -- generate text inside designs. (d) Background remover. Live demo: Create an Instagram post for a fictional "Amritsar Food Festival" in 3 minutes using Canva AI. **Part 2: Leonardo AI (8 min).** Open Leonardo AI (leonardo.ai). Sign up (free credits). Demo: Generate a professional product photo, a logo concept, and an artistic image. Show how prompt specificity matters ("A modern minimalist logo for a chai brand called 'Sip Punjab', warm orange and brown colors, clean white background"). **Part 3: Ideogram (7 min).** Open Ideogram (ideogram.ai). Its specialty: TEXT IN IMAGES (other AIs struggle with this). Demo: Create a poster with readable text -- "Grand Opening -- Sip Punjab -- 20% Off." Show how Ideogram handles text better than others. | Have all accounts pre-created and logged in to save time. Free tiers are sufficient for all demos. |
| 0:40 | 30 | Guided Practice | **Exercise 1: Canva AI (12 min).** Students create an Instagram post for their content calendar using Canva AI. Must include: custom image or background, text overlay, brand colors. Trainer walks through step-by-step on projector. **Exercise 2: Leonardo AI (10 min).** Students generate 3 different images using Leonardo AI with specific, detailed prompts. Save the best one. **Exercise 3: Ideogram (8 min).** Students create 1 poster or banner with text using Ideogram. Use case: event announcement, sale poster, or motivational quote. | Common issue: students get lost in Canva's interface. Keep instructions tight: "Click here, then here, then here." |
| 1:10 | 5 | Break | | |
| 1:15 | 35 | Independent Work | **Visual Content Sprint (35 min):** Create 3 complete social media posts for your content calendar using the tools learned today. Requirements: Post 1: Instagram post using Canva AI (with text + image). Post 2: A product or concept image using Leonardo AI, placed into a Canva template. Post 3: An event/announcement poster using Ideogram. Save all as PNG/JPG. These count toward the 5-post deliverable. | Students should be creating for their actual content calendar topic (local business or personal brand). Consistency matters. |
| 1:50 | 10 | Wrap-Up | Gallery walk: 4-5 students project their best design. Class votes on "Best Design." Quick tips: "Always check that AI-generated images don't have weird hands or text errors." Homework: Create 1 more post (total 4 of 5 done). Preview: "Tomorrow -- AI VIDEO. You'll make a video without a camera." | |

## SESSION 11: AI Video Creation -- CapCut AI & InVideo AI

**Date:** Week 3, Day 3
**Theme:** Creating videos with AI tools
**Tools:** CapCut AI, InVideo AI

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Min | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00 | 5 | Warm-Up | Play a 30-second AI-generated video (pre-made with InVideo). Ask: "How long do you think it took to make this?" Reveal: "5 minutes with AI." | Sets the expectation for what's possible. |
| 0:05 | 10 | Recap | Review image tools from Session 10. Quick show: 3 students display their best image creation on projector. Celebrate quality. | Keep it brief -- today is packed. |
| 0:15 | 25 | Instruction | **Part 1: CapCut AI (12 min).** Open CapCut (capcut.com or app). Free features: auto captions, text-to-speech, templates, AI effects, background removal for video. Live demo: Take a simple product photo, use CapCut's templates to turn it into a 15-second Instagram Reel with music, text, and transitions. Show auto-caption feature (critical for Reels). **Part 2: InVideo AI (13 min).** Open InVideo AI (invideo.io). Its magic: describe a video in text and it creates a full video with stock footage, voiceover, and music. Live demo: Type "Create a 60-second promotional video for a new clothing boutique in Ludhiana. Show fashionable Indian women, modern store interiors, and festive collections. Use an upbeat Bollywood-style background music. Add text: Grand Opening This Saturday." Watch InVideo generate the entire video. | InVideo AI demo is the showstopper. Give students time to react. Then show how to edit/customize the generated video. |
| 0:40 | 30 | Guided Practice | **Exercise 1: CapCut (15 min).** Students download/open CapCut. Take one of their AI-generated images from Session 10 and create a 15-second animated post/reel. Add: text overlay, music (from CapCut library), transition effect, auto-captions if speaking. Trainer walks through each step. **Exercise 2: InVideo AI (15 min).** Students write a text prompt for a 30-60 second video related to their content calendar topic. Generate the video. Preview it. Make 2 edits (change a clip, edit text, adjust music). | CapCut may need app download -- have students download in advance if possible. InVideo AI free tier gives limited exports -- plan accordingly. |
| 1:10 | 5 | Break | | |
| 1:15 | 35 | Independent Work | **Video Creation Challenge (35 min):** Create 1 complete video (30-60 seconds) for their content calendar. Can use either CapCut or InVideo AI (or combine both). Must include: relevant visuals, text/captions, background music, a clear message/CTA. This is post #5 for the deliverable (if they have the previous 4 image posts done). Export and save. Upload to WhatsApp group for peer viewing. | Help students who struggle with export/save. Some may need to reduce quality for faster export on slow connections. |
| 1:50 | 10 | Wrap-Up | Play 3 student videos on projector. Class reacts. Discuss: "Which video would you actually watch on Instagram? Why?" Honest feedback culture. Homework: Post at least 1 of your 5 created posts on your actual social media. Screenshot it. Preview: "Tomorrow -- we put it all together. Personal branding + your deliverable gets finalized." | Encourage actual posting. The confidence of putting AI work into the real world is transformative. |

For students: it's your Instagram bio, LinkedIn profile, the content you post. Before: "Student. Loves food. Ludhiana." After: "BBA Student | AI Content Creator | Turning Punjab's stories into stunning visuals | DM for collaborations." Do the same for LinkedIn headline. **Part 3: Gamma AI Introduction (7 min).** Open Gamma AI (gamma.app). Demo: create a quick personal portfolio presentation by typing a description. Check: Are all 7 days filled? Are captions written (not just topics)? Are the 5 posts created (3 images + 1 video minimum)? Peer fills out a simple checklist for each other. | LinkedIn signup may be new for many students. Walk through it. Even a basic profile with AI-written headline is valuable. |
| 1:10 | 5 | Break | | |
| 1:15 | 35 | Independent Work | **Deliverable Finalization (35 min):** **Phase 1 (15 min):** Complete any missing content calendar days or posts. Ensure exactly: 7-day content calendar (filled) + 5 created posts (at least 3 images + 1 video + 1 any format). **Phase 2 (10 min):** Compile deliverable into a Google Drive folder: Folder name: "Week 3 -- [Name] -- Content Portfolio." Contents: content calendar (sheet or doc), 5 posts (images + video files), screenshot of at least 1 post published on real social media. **Phase 3 (10 min):** Submit folder link via Google Form. Post your best work in WhatsApp group with #Week3Done. | Google Form must be ready. Have a sample folder structure on screen for students to copy. |
| 1:50 | 10 | Wrap-Up | Celebration: show 3-4 best posts on projector. "You're now content creators. Some of you posted for the first time ever this week." Share stats: "As a class, we created [X] posts and [X] videos this week." Preview Week 4: "Next week: AI for MONEY. We help real businesses in your area. You'll walk into a real shop and offer them AI services." | The money/business angle is the biggest motivator for many students. Build the hype. |

## SESSION 13: AI for Local Businesses in Punjab

**Date:** Week 4, Day 1
**Theme:** Understanding how AI helps small businesses
**Tools:** ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google Sheets

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Min | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00 | 5 | Warm-Up | Story time: "Raju runs a sweet shop in Jalandhar. He spends 2 hours daily replying to WhatsApp orders, writing bills by hand, and doesn't have time to post on Instagram. Let's fix Raju's life with AI." Students suggest ideas. | Relatable local story. Engages business thinking. |
| 0:05 | 10 | Recap | Week 3 highlights: content creation skills. Key question: "How many of you got likes/comments on your posted content?" Celebrate real engagement. Connect: "Those content skills? Businesses will PAY you for them." | Bridge from content creation to business application. |
| 0:15 | 25 | Instruction | **Part 1: Local Business Landscape (8 min).** Types of small businesses in Punjab: restaurants/dhabas, clothing boutiques, coaching centres, sweet shops, salons, gyms, real estate agents, wedding planners. Common problems: no online presence, manual billing, poor customer follow-up, no social media. AI can help with ALL of these. **Part 2: The AI Business Audit (10 min).** How to assess a business: 5-Question Quick Audit: (1) Do you have social media? (2) How do you handle orders/bookings? (3) Do you send follow-ups to customers? (4) How do you make bills? (5) What's your biggest daily headache? Demo: Run through the audit for "Raju's Sweet Shop" and identify 5 AI improvements. **Part 3: AI Solutions Menu (7 min).** Show the "AI Services Menu" -- a one-page list of things students can offer: social media content (Week 3 skills), WhatsApp auto-replies, billing templates, customer follow-up automation, market research, Google Business profile. | Print the "AI Services Menu" as a card for each student. They'll use this when approaching businesses for the capstone. |
| 0:40 | 30 | Guided Practice | **Business Selection (10 min):** Students choose a REAL local business they know personally (family business, neighbourhood shop, friend's business). Write down: business name, type, location, owner's first name. If they can't think of one, trainer assigns from a pre-made list. **AI Audit Exercise (20 min):** Students use the 5-question audit template and ANSWER for their chosen business (based on what they know). Then use ChatGPT: "I'm helping a [type] business in [city]. They currently [describe current state]. Suggest 5 specific ways AI tools can improve their business. Be practical and low-cost." Review AI suggestions and highlight the top 3 most realistic ones. | This is the foundation of their Week 4 deliverable. The business they choose here is the one they'll work with all week. Encourage students to WhatsApp the business owner tonight to confirm participation. |
| 1:10 | 5 | Break | | |
| 1:15 | 35 | Independent Work | **Business Improvement Plan -- Section 1 (35 min):** Start a Google Doc: "AI Business Improvement Plan -- [Business Name]." Write Section 1: Business Overview (1 paragraph -- what the business does, where it's located, current challenges). Write Section 2: AI Audit Results (the 5-question audit + findings). Write Section 3: Recommended AI Solutions (top 3 with brief explanation of each). Use AI to help structure and refine, but content must be based on the REAL business. | Students who picked a family business often write the most authentic plans. Encourage this. |
| 1:50 | 10 | Wrap-Up | 3 students share their chosen business and top recommendation. Trainer gives quick feedback on feasibility. Homework: Contact the business owner (call or WhatsApp). Ask them the 5 audit questions directly. Update your plan with real answers. Preview: "Tomorrow -- AI for billing and invoicing. You'll build something the business owner can actually USE." | The homework to contact the real business owner is CRITICAL. Follow up on this in Session 14. |

## SESSION 14: AI + Google Sheets for Invoicing & Billing

**Date:** Week 4, Day 2
**Theme:** Practical business tools -- billing, invoicing, data management
**Tools:** Google Sheets, ChatGPT (for formulas), Canva (for invoice design)

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Min | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00 | 5 | Warm-Up | Show a handwritten bill from a local shop (photo) vs a professional digital invoice. "Which one makes a business look more trustworthy?" Obvious answer -- but most local businesses still use handwritten bills. "Today, you fix that." | Visual contrast is powerful. |
| 0:05 | 10 | Recap | Check homework: "Who contacted their business owner?" Hands up. Those who did: share one insight. Those who didn't: "Call them at break. You need real data for your plan." Quick recap of AI audit findings. | Accountability moment. Gently pressure those who didn't follow through. |
| 0:15 | 25 | Instruction | **Part 1: Google Sheets + AI (15 min).** Open Google Sheets. Demo: Create a simple invoice template with: business name, date, item list, quantity, price, subtotal, GST calculation, grand total. The AI trick: use ChatGPT to generate Google Sheets formulas. Demo prompt: "Write a Google Sheets formula to calculate 18% GST on the value in cell D10 and show the total including GST in cell D12." Paste formula into Sheets. It works. Show: auto-sum for multiple items, conditional formatting for paid/unpaid, dropdown menus for item names. **Part 2: Invoice Design with Canva (5 min).** Quick demo: use Canva invoice templates to make a professional-looking invoice. Add the business logo (or create one with AI). **Part 3: Simple Inventory Tracker (5 min).** Demo: a basic inventory sheet in Google Sheets. Columns: Item, Stock, Reorder Level. Use conditional formatting: red when stock < reorder level. | For non-IT students, Google Sheets formulas feel like magic. Go slowly. The ChatGPT-for-formulas trick is a genuine life skill. |
| 0:40 | 30 | Guided Practice | **Build an Invoice (15 min):** Students create an invoice template in Google Sheets for their chosen business. Must include: business name, customer name, date, at least 5 sample items, quantity, price, subtotal, GST (18%), grand total. Use ChatGPT for any formula they don't know. **Build an Inventory Tracker (15 min):** Create a simple inventory sheet with 10 items from the business. Add conditional formatting (trainer shows step-by-step). Add a "Total Stock Value" formula at the bottom. | Students will ask "What items should I list?" Tell them to check the business's actual menu/product list (from their audit) or make realistic ones. |
| 1:10 | 5 | Break | Reminder: students who haven't called their business owner -- do it NOW during break. | |
| 1:15 | 35 | Independent Work | **Business Tool Creation (35 min):** For your chosen business, create at least 2 of these: (1) Professional invoice template (Google Sheets + Canva design version), (2) Inventory tracker, (3) Customer list/CRM sheet (Name, Phone, Last Visit, Notes), (4) Daily sales tracker (Date, Items Sold, Revenue). Add these to your Business Improvement Plan document as "Section 4: AI Tools Created." Include screenshots or links to the Sheets. | These are real deliverables the student can SHOW to the business owner. Tangible value. |
| 1:50 | 10 | Wrap-Up | 2 students demo their invoice template on projector. Quick feedback. "Imagine walking into a shop and saying 'I made this for your business, for free.' That's how you get your first client." Homework: Share the invoice/tracker with the business owner on WhatsApp. Ask: "Would this be useful?" Screenshot their response. Preview: "Tomorrow -- WhatsApp automation. The business owner will love you." | |

Find: (1) Industry trends, (2) Customer preferences, (3) Competitor analysis (what are similar businesses doing online?). Summarize findings in 5 bullet points. | Some students won't have a second phone for WhatsApp Business. Solution: they document everything in Google Docs as a "WhatsApp Setup Guide" the business owner can follow. |

| 1:15 | 35 | Independent Work | **Business Plan Expansion (35 min):** Add to the Business Improvement Plan: Section 5: WhatsApp Business Setup Guide (screenshots or setup instructions, message templates). Section 6: Market Research Summary (industry trends, competitor analysis, 3 opportunities). Section 7: 30-Day Action Plan (week-by-week: what the business should do first, second, third, fourth). Use AI to help structure the action plan but customize it for the real business. | The plan is now substantial: 7 sections. Students should feel the weight of what they've created. |
| 1:50 | 10 | Wrap-Up | 1 student presents their 30-day action plan. Class gives feedback: "Would this convince a business owner?" Honest critique. Homework: Finalize the entire Business Improvement Plan for submission tomorrow. If possible, meet the business owner in person and walk through the plan. Preview: "Tomorrow -- you present your plans. Best ones get featured. And we submit." | |

## SESSION 16: Business Plan Presentations + Week 4 Deliverable Submission

**Date:** Week 4, Day 4
**Theme:** Presentation skills + deliverable submission
**Tools:** Google Docs, Gamma AI (for quick presentations)

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Min | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00 | 5 | Warm-Up | "Shark Tank Punjab" announcement: "Today, 5 of you will present your business improvement plans to the class. The class is the 'investor panel.' Best pitch wins bragging rights." | Competitive energy. Even shy students get excited by Shark Tank format. |
| 0:05 | 10 | Recap | Quick review of all Week 4 content: AI business audit, invoicing, WhatsApp Business, market research. "You've learned skills that most small business consultants charge lakhs for. And you can do it in a week." | Build confidence before presentations. |
| 0:15 | 25 | Instruction | **Quick Presentation Tips (10 min).** Structure: Problem --> Solution --> Proof --> Ask. Speak to the business owner, not to "an audience." Keep it under 5 minutes. Watch it generate a presentation in 30 seconds. "This is your shortcut for making your plan look professional." **Grading Rubric Review (10 min).** Show on screen: Relevance to the real business (3 marks), Quality of AI tools used (3 marks), Feasibility of recommendations (2 marks), Presentation clarity (2 marks) = 10 marks total. | Gamma AI is being introduced here for quick presentations. Class gives structured feedback: 1 thing they'd buy, 1 question they'd ask if they were the business owner. | Pick diverse businesses for the 5 presenters. | 1:10 | 5 | Break | | |
| 1:15 | 35 | Independent Work | **Final Submission Sprint (35 min):** **Phase 1 (15 min):** Final edits to the Business Improvement Plan. Ensure all 7 sections are complete. Run through Grammarly. Check that all links to Sheets/tools work. **Phase 2 (10 min):** Create a Google Drive folder: "Week 4 -- [Name] -- Business Plan." Include: the plan document, invoice/tracker Sheets, WhatsApp setup guide, Gamma presentation, any screenshots of business owner feedback. **Phase 3 (10 min):** Submit via Google Form. Bonus: if the business owner sent a WhatsApp response or feedback, include screenshot in the folder. | Extra credit for business owner feedback reinforces real-world application. |
| 1:50 | 10 | Wrap-Up | Announce top 3 presentations based on class vote. "You're halfway through the program. In 4 weeks, you've gone from 'What is AI?' to creating business plans for real companies. Soch badal gayi?" Milestone celebration. Preview Weeks 5-6: "Next week -- AUTOMATION. You'll build systems that work while you sleep. Week 6 -- you build CHATBOTS. Zero coding." Half-time motivational close. | This is the halfway point. Make it feel like an achievement. Consider a small celebration (chai, samosas) if centre budget allows. |
