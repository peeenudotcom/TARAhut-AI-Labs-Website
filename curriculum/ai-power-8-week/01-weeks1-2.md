**Tools:** ChatGPT, Gemini (demo only)

|---|---|---|---|---|

Show 3 real examples: Google Translate, Instagram filters, Spotify recommendations. **Part 2 (5 min):** Myth-busting -- show 5 myths on slides (AI is sentient, AI will replace all jobs, AI is always right, AI is only for coders, AI is too expensive). Bust each one with one sentence. **Part 3 (10 min):** LIVE DEMO -- "10 Tasks in 10 Minutes." On projector, open ChatGPT and do: (1) Write a birthday wish in Punjabi, (2) Summarize a news article, (3) Create a study plan, (4) Draft an email to a professor, (5) Explain a BBA concept, (6) Write an Instagram caption, (7) Create a quiz, (8) Translate English to Hindi, (9) Write a complaint letter, (10) Generate business name ideas. Have backup: use Gemini if ChatGPT signup fails. |

Screenshot each. **Task 2 (15 min):** Ask the same prompt to both ChatGPT and Gemini. Write 3 differences you notice. **Task 3 (5 min):** Post your best AI output in the batch WhatsApp group with caption "My first AI output!" | Circulate. Praise creative prompts publicly. Help stuck students with prompt starters: "Try asking AI to..." |
| 1:50 | 10 | Wrap-Up | 3 students share their best prompt + output on projector. Re-do the "Haath Uthao" poll: "Who STILL thinks AI will replace humans?" Compare numbers. Assign homework: Try 5 more prompts at home, screenshot them. Preview Session 2: "Tomorrow we learn the SECRET to making AI give you exactly what you want." | End on a cliffhanger. Builds curiosity for prompting session. |

## SESSION 2: Prompting 101 -- The Art of Talking to AI

**Date:** Week 1, Day 2
**Theme:** Prompt engineering fundamentals
**Tools:** ChatGPT, Gemini

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Min | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00 | 5 | Warm-Up | "AI or Human?" game. Show 3 short paragraphs on screen. Students guess which was written by AI and which by a human. Reveal answers. | Pick examples where AI output is surprisingly good AND where it's obviously robotic. |
| 0:05 | 10 | Recap | Ask 3 students: "What was the most interesting thing you asked AI since yesterday?" Quick discussion: "Did anyone get a bad or weird answer?" (Sets up prompting lesson.) | Connect bad outputs to bad prompts -- "Garbage in, garbage out." |
| 0:15 | 25 | Instruction | **The Prompting Framework -- RICEE:** R = Role ("Act as a..."), I = Instruction (what to do), C = Context (background info), E = Examples (show what you want), E = Extras (format, tone, length). **Live demo of BAD vs GOOD prompts:** Bad: "Write about business." Good: "Act as a BBA professor. Write a 200-word explanation of break-even analysis for a first-year student. Use a chai stall example. Keep language simple." Show the dramatic difference in output quality. **5 prompt types:** (1) Question prompts, (2) Task prompts, (3) Role-play prompts, (4) Chain prompts (multi-step), (5) Rewrite/improve prompts. Demo each with one example. | Write RICEE on the board/whiteboard. Students copy it. This framework is used for the rest of the program. |
| 0:40 | 30 | Guided Practice | **Exercise 1 (10 min):** Trainer gives a bad prompt. Students rewrite it using RICEE framework. 3 students share on projector. Class votes on best one. **Exercise 2 (10 min):** Trainer gives a topic (e.g., "Write about Amritsar for tourists"). Students write 3 versions: (a) basic prompt, (b) with role + context, (c) with full RICEE. Compare outputs. **Exercise 3 (10 min):** Chain prompting: First prompt: "List 5 business ideas for a college student in Jalandhar." Second prompt: "Take idea #3 and write a 100-word business plan." Third prompt: "Now write an Instagram post promoting this business." | Show how each level of RICEE improves output. Make the improvement visible. |
| 1:10 | 5 | Break | Quick stretch. Trivia: "The word 'prompt' comes from Latin 'promptus' meaning 'brought forth.' You're literally bringing forth AI's intelligence!" | Fun fact builds vocabulary. |
| 1:15 | 35 | Independent Work | **Prompt Challenge:** Write 10 prompts (2 of each type: question, task, role-play, chain, rewrite). Each prompt must use at least 3 elements of RICEE. Save all in a Google Doc titled "[Name] -- Prompt Portfolio." Share link with trainer. **Bonus challenge:** Find a prompt that makes ChatGPT and Gemini give VERY different answers. Screenshot both. | This starts their deliverable. They need 20 total prompts by end of Week 1. This gives them 10 + 5 from homework + 5 from Session 1. |
| 1:50 | 10 | Wrap-Up | "Prompt of the Day" award -- trainer picks the most creative prompt and puts it on the WhatsApp group. Preview Session 3: "Tomorrow you meet 2 more AI friends -- Claude and Perplexity. And we learn about what AI gets WRONG." Homework: Write 5 more RICEE prompts (now they have 20 total for the deliverable). | |

## SESSION 3: Meet the AI Family -- Claude, Perplexity + AI Safety & Hallucinations

**Date:** Week 1, Day 3
**Theme:** Expanding the toolkit + critical AI literacy
**Tools:** Claude, Perplexity, ChatGPT, Gemini

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Min | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00 | 5 | Warm-Up | "2 Truths 1 AI Lie" -- Trainer shows 3 facts. Students identify which one was made up by AI (hallucination). Reveal: it was the most confident-sounding one. Lesson: AI lies with full confidence. | Sets up the hallucination topic perfectly. |
| 0:05 | 10 | Recap | Quick recap of RICEE framework. Pop quiz: "What does the first E stand for?" Random student answers. Review homework: anyone find a prompt where ChatGPT and Gemini disagreed? | 2-3 students share briefly. |
| 0:15 | 25 | Instruction | **Part 1 (12 min):** Introduce Claude and Perplexity. Claude: "The careful, thoughtful one. Great for long writing, analysis, being honest about what it doesn't know." Perplexity: "The researcher. It searches the internet and gives you sources." Live demo: Ask all 4 tools the same question: "What are the top colleges in Punjab for BBA?" Compare: ChatGPT (general), Gemini (Google-integrated), Claude (nuanced), Perplexity (with citations). **Part 2 (13 min):** AI Safety & Hallucinations. What are hallucinations? Live demo: ask AI a very specific fake question ("Who won the 2025 Jalandhar Chess Championship?") -- show it confidently makes up an answer. Privacy: never put Aadhaar numbers, passwords, personal health info. Show what NOT to type. The 3-check rule: (1) Does this sound too specific? (2) Can I verify this? (3) Would I bet money on this? | When showing hallucination demo, use a local reference so students relate. The "bet money" test resonates with students. |
| 0:40 | 30 | Guided Practice | **Setup (10 min):** Sign up for Claude (claude.ai) and Perplexity (perplexity.ai). Trainer walks through on projector. **Exercise 1 (10 min):** "Tool Matchmaker" -- Trainer gives 5 tasks. Students decide which AI tool is best for each and explain why: (1) Write a poem (ChatGPT), (2) Research latest scholarship deadlines (Perplexity), (3) Analyze a long article (Claude), (4) Quick translation (Gemini), (5) Fact-check a claim (Perplexity). **Exercise 2 (10 min):** Hallucination Hunt -- Give students 3 AI-generated paragraphs (pre-prepared). They must find the hallucinated facts and verify using Perplexity or Google. | Have pre-prepared paragraphs ready with subtle hallucinations (wrong dates, fake statistics, invented book titles). |
| 1:10 | 5 | Break | Stretch. Fun: show a meme about AI hallucinations. | |
| 1:15 | 35 | Independent Work | **Task 1 (15 min):** Write the same prompt to all 4 AI tools. Create a comparison table in Google Docs: Tool | Speed | Quality | Sources | Best For. **Task 2 (10 min):** Write 3 prompts specifically designed for Perplexity (research-type) and 3 for Claude (analysis-type). **Task 3 (10 min):** Create an "AI Safety Poster" -- write 5 rules for safe AI use in your own words (they can use Canva or just write in Docs). Post in WhatsApp group. | This comparison table is a great portfolio piece -- encourage them to make it neat. |
| 1:50 | 10 | Wrap-Up | 2 students share their comparison tables. Trainer asks: "After 3 days with AI, what has changed in how you think about it?" Brief discussion. Preview Session 4: "Tomorrow is Prompt Showdown day -- you'll compete, and we'll finalize your 20-prompt portfolio." Homework: Finalize all 20 prompts in Google Doc. | |

Best persuasion wins. **Round 2 (7 min):** "Create a 1-day Amritsar travel itinerary for a foreign tourist." Most detailed and useful wins. **Round 3 (6 min):** "Explain GST to a 10-year-old." | 1:15 | 35 | Independent Work | **Final Polish + Submission (25 min):** Students finalize their 20-prompt portfolio. Submit by sharing Google Doc link in a Google Form (trainer prepares form beforehand). **Peer Review (10 min):** Each student exchanges Doc link with a partner. They read 3 prompts and write 1 compliment + 1 suggestion as a comment in the Doc. | Have the Google Form ready. Collect all submissions. Grade using rubric from HQ. |
| 1:50 | 10 | Wrap-Up | Announce Prompt Showdown winners. Week 1 reflection: "Write one sentence: what did AI teach you this week?" Read a few aloud. Preview Week 2: "Next week, AI helps you with your actual studies -- assignments, research, IELTS, SOPs. Bring a real assignment you're working on to Session 5." | The "bring a real assignment" instruction is critical. Week 2 needs real student work. |

## SESSION 5: AI for Assignments, Essays & Summaries

**Date:** Week 2, Day 1
**Theme:** Using AI ethically for academic work
**Tools:** ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grammarly AI

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Min | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00 | 5 | Warm-Up | Show a "before and after" -- a badly written student paragraph and the AI-improved version. Ask: "Is this cheating?" Quick show of hands. Promise to discuss the ethics. | Starts an important conversation. No judgment. |
| 0:05 | 10 | Recap | Week 1 highlights. Ask: "Who used AI this weekend for something outside class?" 3 students share. Reinforce: "You're already building AI habits." | Celebrate real-world usage. |
| 0:15 | 25 | Instruction | **Part 1: The AI Study Partner Model (10 min).** AI is NOT for copy-paste. AI is for: (1) Understanding concepts you don't get, (2) Getting a first draft to improve, (3) Summarizing long readings, (4) Getting feedback on YOUR writing. The 3-step method: Write yourself first --> Ask AI to improve --> Edit the AI version in YOUR voice. **Part 2: Live Demo (15 min).** Take a real BBA assignment topic: "Discuss the importance of marketing mix in Indian FMCG companies." Demo: (a) Ask ChatGPT to explain the concept simply, (b) Ask Claude to create an outline, (c) Write a paragraph yourself, then ask Gemini to improve it, (d) Use Grammarly AI to fix grammar. Show the progression from blank page to polished paragraph. | Emphasize: the goal is BETTER student work, not AI-written work. The 3-step method is the ethical standard for the program. |
| 0:40 | 30 | Guided Practice | **Setup (5 min):** Students open the real assignment they brought (per Session 4 homework). Those who forgot: trainer provides 3 generic topics. **Exercise 1 (10 min):** Ask AI to explain your assignment topic "like I'm a first-year student." Read the explanation. Highlight 2 things you learned. **Exercise 2 (10 min):** Ask Claude to create an outline for a 500-word answer. Students modify the outline based on their own ideas. **Exercise 3 (5 min):** Sign up for Grammarly (grammarly.com). Install the Chrome extension. Test on one paragraph. | Grammarly free version is sufficient. Walk through installation step by step. |
| 1:10 | 5 | Break | | |
| 1:15 | 35 | Independent Work | **Write a 300-word AI-assisted essay (35 min):** Step 1 (10 min): Write a rough first draft yourself -- no AI. Step 2 (10 min): Ask AI to suggest improvements (don't replace; suggest). Step 3 (10 min): Rewrite incorporating suggestions. Step 4 (5 min): Run through Grammarly. Save all 3 versions (rough, AI suggestions, final) in one Google Doc. | The 3-version requirement proves the student did the thinking. This is key for academic integrity. |
| 1:50 | 10 | Wrap-Up | 2 students share before/after (rough vs final). Class appreciates the improvement. Homework: Write a summary of any 1 chapter from their current textbook using the 3-step method. Preview: "Tomorrow -- AI for research. Bring a topic you're curious about." | |

## SESSION 6: AI Research Tools -- Perplexity + Google Scholar

**Date:** Week 2, Day 2
**Theme:** Research skills using AI
**Tools:** Perplexity, Google Scholar, ChatGPT, Claude

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Min | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00 | 5 | Warm-Up | "Fact or Fake?" -- Show 3 statistics about India's economy. Students use Perplexity to verify in 2 minutes. First to find the fake one wins. | Builds research instincts. |
| 0:05 | 10 | Recap | Review the 3-step method from Session 5. One student shares their homework (textbook summary). Quick feedback. | Reinforce the ethical AI use pattern. |
| 0:15 | 25 | Instruction | **Part 1: Perplexity Deep Dive (12 min).** Perplexity as a research engine. Features: citations, follow-up questions, focus modes (Academic, Writing, Math). Live demo: Research "Impact of GST on small businesses in Punjab." Show how Perplexity gives sources. Click through to original articles. Show the "Related" questions feature for deeper exploration. **Part 2: Google Scholar + AI (8 min).** Open Google Scholar (scholar.google.com). Search the same topic. Show how to read abstracts. Use Claude to summarize a research paper abstract in simple language. **Part 3: Research Workflow (5 min).** The 4-step AI research process: (1) Perplexity for overview + sources, (2) Google Scholar for academic papers, (3) Claude to summarize complex papers, (4) ChatGPT to help structure your findings. | Show students that Perplexity replaces hours of googling. The "Related questions" feature is a goldmine for expanding research. |
| 0:40 | 30 | Guided Practice | **Research Sprint (30 min):** All students research the same topic: "Digital India initiative -- successes and failures." **Step 1 (8 min):** Use Perplexity to find 5 key facts. Note the sources. **Step 2 (7 min):** Use Google Scholar to find 1 academic paper or report on the topic. Copy the abstract. **Step 3 (7 min):** Use Claude to summarize the abstract in 5 bullet points a non-expert can understand. **Step 4 (8 min):** Use ChatGPT to create a "Research Summary Template" -- 1 paragraph intro, key findings (bullet points), sources list. | Walk through each step on projector. Students follow along. Pause between steps to make sure everyone is keeping up. |
| 1:10 | 5 | Break | | |
| 1:15 | 35 | Independent Work | **Individual Research Project (35 min):** Students pick their OWN topic (related to their course or interest). Complete the 4-step research process. Produce a 1-page "Research Brief" in Google Docs with: Title, 3-sentence introduction, 5 key findings with sources, 2-sentence conclusion. Minimum 3 cited sources. | Students doing SOP (study abroad) track: research universities/programs. Students doing research summary track: research their chosen topic. This feeds directly into the Week 2 deliverable. |
| 1:50 | 10 | Wrap-Up | 2 students share their Research Brief topics and one surprising finding. Trainer: "You just did in 35 minutes what used to take 3 days in a library." Homework: Expand the Research Brief to 2 pages (add more findings). Preview: "Tomorrow -- IELTS/PTE students, your day has come. Everyone else: AI for exam prep." | |

## SESSION 7: AI for IELTS/PTE Prep + Study Abroad SOPs & LORs

**Date:** Week 2, Day 3
**Theme:** AI for test prep and study-abroad applications
**Tools:** ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Grammarly AI

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Min | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00 | 5 | Warm-Up | "English Challenge" -- Show a sentence with 3 grammar errors. First to identify all 3 wins. Then show how Grammarly catches them instantly. | Connects to IELTS prep. Fun and humbling. |
| 0:05 | 10 | Recap | Review research skills from Session 6. Quick: "Who found an interesting source using Perplexity?" 2 students share. | |
| 0:15 | 25 | Instruction | **Track A: IELTS/PTE Prep (15 min).** How AI helps with each section: Writing Task 1 (describe a graph): "Describe this graph in IELTS format" + AI feedback. Writing Task 2 (essay): AI as practice partner -- write, get AI feedback on band score. Speaking: practice with ChatGPT voice mode or type responses and get evaluation. Reading: use AI to explain difficult passages. Listening: AI generates practice questions. Live demo: Write an IELTS Task 2 essay response, paste into Claude, ask: "Evaluate this IELTS Writing Task 2 response. Give a band score estimate and 3 specific improvements." **Track B: SOP & LOR (10 min).** What is an SOP? What is an LOR? Show a BAD SOP (generic, no personality) vs a GOOD SOP (specific, personal, compelling). Demo: Use ChatGPT with RICEE to draft an SOP section: "Act as an admissions consultant. Help me write the 'motivation' paragraph of my SOP for an MBA in Canada. I'm from Jalandhar, studied BBA at [college], passionate about entrepreneurship because my family runs a small textile business." | Split screen time is okay -- IELTS students engage more with Track A, study-abroad with Track B. But EVERYONE learns both because SOPs use writing skills. |
| 0:40 | 30 | Guided Practice | **IELTS Track (15 min):** Students write a response to: "Some people think that AI will replace human teachers. To what extent do you agree?" (150-200 words). Then ask Claude to evaluate it: "Rate this IELTS Task 2 response on a scale of 1-9 for Task Achievement, Coherence, Lexical Resource, and Grammar." Students note their scores and AI's improvement suggestions. **SOP Track (15 min):** Students write the opening paragraph of an SOP (even if not applying abroad -- it's a skill). Use the structure: Hook (personal story) --> Academic background --> Why this program --> Why this country. AI helps refine, not write from scratch. | Students choose ONE track for guided practice. The other track is available as independent work or homework. |
| 1:10 | 5 | Break | | |
| 1:15 | 35 | Independent Work | **Choose your deliverable track:** **Option A -- IELTS/PTE students:** Write 2 full IELTS Writing Task 2 practice essays (250 words each). Get AI evaluation for each. Create a "My IELTS Improvement Plan" document showing: practice essay, AI score, AI feedback, revised version, new score. **Option B -- SOP track:** Write a complete first draft SOP (500-700 words) using the structure taught. Use AI for refinement at each paragraph, not for generation. Include: personal story, academic background, why this program, why this university, future goals. **Option C -- Research Summary track (for students not doing IELTS or study abroad):** Expand the research brief from Session 6 into a 2-page research summary with proper structure. | Most students will choose B or C. All three are valid Week 2 deliverables. Trainer should guide undecided students based on their goals. |
| 1:50 | 10 | Wrap-Up | 1 student from each track shares progress. Encourage peer support: "IELTS students -- practice with each other using AI feedback." Homework: Complete your chosen deliverable to 80% -- we finalize tomorrow. Preview Session 8: "Tomorrow we polish, peer review, and submit. Bring your best work." | |

## SESSION 8: Deliverable Polish + Peer Review + Week 2 Submission

**Date:** Week 2, Day 4
**Theme:** Finalization, peer feedback, and submission
**Tools:** All tools as needed, Grammarly AI

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Min | Block | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00 | 5 | Warm-Up | "2-Week AI Journey" -- Quick poll on WhatsApp or hands: "On a scale of 1-10, how confident are you using AI now vs Day 1?" Celebrate the growth. | Builds confidence. Most will say 7-9 vs 2-4 on Day 1. |
| 0:05 | 10 | Recap | Recap all Week 2 skills: AI for assignments (3-step method), Perplexity research, Google Scholar, IELTS prep, SOP writing. Quick-fire: trainer names a task, students name the best tool. | Fast and energetic. Builds tool-selection instincts. |
| 0:15 | 25 | Instruction | **Deliverable Quality Standards (10 min):** Show the grading rubric on screen. For SOP: Clarity (3), Personalization (3), AI Usage (evidence of human+AI collaboration) (2), Grammar (2) = 10 marks. For Research Summary: Depth (3), Sources (3), Structure (2), Clarity (2) = 10 marks. For IELTS Practice: Number of attempts (2), AI Feedback Integration (3), Improvement shown (3), Reflection quality (2) = 10 marks. **Peer Review Process (5 min):** Explain how to give constructive feedback: 1 strength, 1 question, 1 suggestion. Model it with a sample document on screen. **Power Editing Tips (10 min):** Demo quick improvements: (a) Ask Claude to "make this more personal and less generic," (b) Use Grammarly for final grammar pass, (c) Ask Perplexity to verify any facts/statistics in the document, (d) Ask ChatGPT to "suggest a stronger opening line." | Show the rubric clearly. Students should know exactly what gets full marks. |
| 0:40 | 30 | Guided Practice | **Peer Review Round (30 min):** Students pair up (trainer assigns pairs -- mix strong with developing students). **Round 1 (12 min):** Student A shares screen or Doc link. Student B reads and gives verbal feedback using the 1-1-1 format (1 strength, 1 question, 1 suggestion). Student A takes notes. **Round 2 (12 min):** Switch roles. **Group Share (6 min):** 3 pairs share the best feedback they received with the class. | Walk around and listen to peer reviews. Coach students who give vague feedback ("It's good") to be specific ("The second paragraph is strong because..."). |
| 1:10 | 5 | Break | | |
| 1:15 | 35 | Independent Work | **Final Polish + Submission (35 min):** **Phase 1 -- Revise (15 min):** Incorporate peer feedback. Make at least 3 specific improvements. **Phase 2 -- AI Final Pass (10 min):** Run through Grammarly. Ask Claude: "Read this and identify the weakest paragraph. Suggest how to make it stronger." Make final edits. **Phase 3 -- Submit (10 min):** Format neatly in Google Docs. Add a cover line: "Week 2 Deliverable -- [Name] -- [Track: SOP/Research/IELTS]." Submit link via Google Form. | Have the Google Form ready. Deadline: submit before leaving class. Late submissions lose 1 mark per day. |
| 1:50 | 10 | Wrap-Up | Celebrate Week 2 completion. "You've now used AI for academics, research, test prep, and professional writing. You're ahead of 95% of students your age." Read 2-3 strong opening lines from submitted SOPs/research summaries (anonymous). **Big Preview:** "Weeks 3-4 change everything. We go from academics to MONEY. You'll learn to create content, build social media, and help real businesses. The fun stuff starts now." | End on high energy. The transition from academic to creative/business AI is a major motivator. |
