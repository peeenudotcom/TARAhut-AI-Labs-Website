# Week 4: Content Creation & Career Exploration

## Week Overview

**Theme:** Build your digital presence, create AI content, and explore AI careers
**Sessions:** 4 (Sessions 13-16)
**Total Hours:** 8 hours
**Key Outcome:** Students have a LinkedIn profile, can create AI-powered content, and present their work at Showcase Day

## Session 13: LinkedIn Profile Setup (for Internships/Scholarships)

**Date:** Week 4, Day 1
**Duration:** 120 minutes
**Topic:** Building Your Professional Digital Identity with LinkedIn
**Learning Objectives:**
1. Understand why a LinkedIn profile matters even for school students
2. Create a complete LinkedIn profile using AI-assisted writing
3. Write a compelling headline and summary using prompt engineering
4. Connect with relevant people and join educational communities

### Materials Needed
- Projector with internet access
- Student laptops with ChatGPT and LinkedIn access
- Whiteboard and markers
- Handout: "LinkedIn Profile Blueprint for Students" (print 1 per student)
- Students should bring: a decent headshot photo (phone selfie is fine)
- Parental consent forms (if required for students under 16)

### Pre-Session Setup (Trainer)
- Create a demo LinkedIn profile (or use your own) to show as example
- Prepare prompts for AI-assisted profile writing
- Print blueprints
- Check: LinkedIn requires minimum age 16 in India; for students under 16, focus on "preparing" the profile content to publish later OR get parental consent
- Have sample student LinkedIn profiles (screenshots) ready to show

### Minute-by-Minute Session Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "Who Has LinkedIn?"** Quick show of hands. Probably very few. Show a stat: "85% of jobs are filled through networking. LinkedIn has 100+ million users in India." Show an example of a Class 10 student who got an internship through LinkedIn. "You are never too young to start building your professional identity." | Use a real example if possible. If not, create a hypothetical that is realistic. The goal is to make LinkedIn feel relevant, not like "something adults do." |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap Week 3.** Quick check: "Is your bot ready for Showcase Day?" Address any last-minute bot issues. Transition: "Your bot shows what you can BUILD. Your LinkedIn shows WHO you are. Together, they make you stand out." | Keep the Week 3 recap brief -- the focus is on Week 4 content. If students have urgent bot issues, address them during independent practice time. |
| 0:15-0:25 | 10 min | **Instruction: LinkedIn for Students -- Why It Matters.** Explain 5 benefits: (1) Scholarship applications look for digital presence (2) Internship applications in Class 11-12 (3) Competition and science fair documentation (4) Building a professional network early (5) Showcasing projects and AI skills. Show 2-3 sample student profiles on the projector. | Address the "but I am too young" objection directly: "The best time to plant a tree was 10 years ago. The second best time is today. The best time to make a LinkedIn profile is right now, while you have AI skills most adults do not have." |
| 0:25-0:35 | 10 min | **Instruction: Anatomy of a Great Student Profile.** Walk through each section: (1) Profile photo (clear, professional-ish, no filters) (2) Headline (not just "student" -- something like "Class 10 Student | AI Enthusiast | Custom GPT Builder") (3) About section (4) Education (5) Skills (6) Projects. Show good vs bad examples for each. | The headline is the most important part. Live-demo using ChatGPT: "Write 5 LinkedIn headline options for a Class 10 student in Punjab who has learned AI prompt engineering and built a custom chatbot." Show the options. Let students see AI writing about THEM. |
| 0:35-0:40 | 5 min | **Live Demo: AI-Powered Profile Writing.** Open ChatGPT. Prompt: "Write a LinkedIn About section for a 15-year-old Class 10 CBSE student from Punjab who recently completed an AI course at TARAhut AI Labs. They can do prompt engineering, built a custom chatbot, and are interested in technology careers. Keep it under 150 words, professional but friendly, showing personality." Show the result. Edit it together with class input. | Students see that AI can write ABOUT them. This is meta: using AI skills (from Week 1) to build their professional identity. Point this out explicitly. |
| 0:40-1:00 | 20 min | **Guided Practice: Create Your Profile.** Students follow the blueprint handout step by step: (1) Sign up or log in to LinkedIn (2) Upload profile photo (3) Use ChatGPT to generate headline options -- pick the best one (4) Use ChatGPT to draft their About section -- edit to make it personal (5) Add Education details. Trainer circulates helping with setup. | Age verification: LinkedIn requires users to be 16+. For students under 16, have them create the profile content in a Google Doc to publish later. Do NOT encourage lying about age. Discuss digital honesty. For students 16+, proceed with account creation. |
| 1:00-1:15 | 15 min | **Guided Practice: Skills and Projects.** Students add: (1) Skills: Prompt Engineering, ChatGPT, AI Tools, Critical Thinking (2) Projects: their Custom GPT (with description) (3) Certifications: TARAhut AI Labs AI Explorer (upcoming). They use ChatGPT to write project descriptions. | Help students write project descriptions that sound professional but honest. "Do not say 'I built a world-class chatbot.' Say 'Built a custom GPT study buddy trained on Class 10 CBSE Science content that provides chapter-specific explanations and quiz questions.'" |
| 1:15-1:20 | 5 min | **Break.** | Quick stretch. |
| 1:20-1:40 | 20 min | **Independent Practice: Network Building.** Students connect with: (1) Their trainer (2) TARAhut AI Labs page (3) 5 classmates (4) 2-3 educational pages (CBSE, NCERT, etc.). They join 2 relevant LinkedIn groups (AI for Students, Education Technology). Discuss LinkedIn etiquette: personalized connection requests, no spam, professional behavior. | Supervise carefully. Discuss online safety: do not accept connection requests from strangers, do not share personal information (home address, phone number), do not click suspicious links. This is a digital safety lesson too. |
| 1:40-1:55 | 15 min | **Independent Practice: First LinkedIn Post.** Students draft their FIRST LinkedIn post using AI. Prompt for ChatGPT: "Write a LinkedIn post for a Class 10 student announcing they are learning AI and have built their first chatbot. Keep it authentic, not braggy. Under 100 words. Include relevant hashtags." Students edit the draft to make it genuinely their own voice, then post (or save as draft). | The first post is always the hardest. Make it a class event: "Everyone post at the same time!" This removes individual fear. After posting, everyone likes and comments on each other's posts -- immediate engagement. |
| 1:55-2:00 | 5 min | **Wrap-Up: Digital Identity Check.** Quick review: "Your LinkedIn is now live. You have a professional profile that most adults in India do not have. You are ahead." Preview Session 14: "Tomorrow, you become a video creator. AI will help you make professional videos without a camera team." | End on a confidence-building note. Students should feel proud of their professional profiles. |

### Homework / Take-Home Challenge
Show your LinkedIn profile to a parent or older sibling. Ask them: "Would you connect with this person?" If they say yes, you did it right. Get 3 more connections tonight.

## Session 14: AI Video Creation Basics

**Date:** Week 4, Day 2
**Duration:** 120 minutes
**Topic:** Creating Professional Videos Using AI Tools (HeyGen + CapCut)
**Learning Objectives:**
1. Create an AI avatar video using HeyGen
2. Edit videos using AI-powered features in CapCut
3. Write video scripts using ChatGPT
4. Produce a 60-second educational or personal intro video

### Materials Needed
- Projector with internet access
- Student laptops with HeyGen (free trial), CapCut (free), and ChatGPT access
- Whiteboard and markers
- Handout: "Video Script Template" (print 1 per student)
- Headphones (optional, for video editing without disturbing others)

### Pre-Session Setup (Trainer)
- Create a HeyGen account and test the free tier (limited to a few videos)
- Create a demo AI avatar video (30-60 seconds) to show students
- Install/access CapCut on the demo machine
- Prepare backup: if HeyGen free credits run out, use Canva's AI video features or simple screen recording
- Print script templates

### Minute-by-Minute Session Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "Is This Person Real?"** Show 3 videos on the projector -- 2 are AI-generated avatar videos, 1 is a real person. Students vote which is real. Reveal answers. "AI can now create realistic videos of people who do not exist. Today, YOU learn how to do this." | The "deepfake" reveal is always mind-blowing for teens. Use this as a teaching moment about both power and responsibility. "This technology can be used for good (education, content creation) or bad (fake news). We learn to use it for GOOD." |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap Session 13.** Quick LinkedIn check: "How many connections do you have now?" Celebrate the student with the most connections. Any issues with profiles? Quick fixes. Transition: "LinkedIn is your professional text presence. Today, we add VIDEO to your toolkit." | Keep recap brief -- students are excited about video creation. |
| 0:15-0:25 | 10 min | **Instruction: AI Video Landscape.** Overview of AI video tools: (1) HeyGen -- avatar videos (a digital person speaks your script) (2) CapCut -- AI-powered video editing (auto captions, effects, templates) (3) Other tools: Synthesia, Runway, Canva video. Show the demo AI avatar video (pre-made). Discuss use cases: educational content, introductions, school project presentations, social media. | Keep the tool overview brief. Focus on HeyGen and CapCut as the primary tools for today. Show the demo video -- it should be impressive but also clearly educational/professional, not gimmicky. |
| 0:25-0:35 | 10 min | **Instruction: Script Writing with AI.** Before any video, you need a script. Demo: "Write a 60-second video script for a Class 10 student introducing themselves and their AI chatbot project. Tone: confident and friendly. Include: who you are, what you built, why it matters, and a call to action." Show the ChatGPT output. Edit it together: cut filler words, make it punchy. | Teach the script formula: Hook (5 sec) + Introduction (10 sec) + Main Content (35 sec) + Call to Action (10 sec) = 60 seconds. Students should time themselves reading the script aloud to check length. |
| 0:35-0:40 | 5 min | **Live Demo: HeyGen Walkthrough.** Open HeyGen on the projector. Show: (1) Choose an avatar (2) Select a voice (3) Paste the script (4) Select background (5) Generate video. Start generating a video (it takes 1-3 minutes). While it processes, move to guided practice. | HeyGen free tier allows 1-2 videos. Plan accordingly: each student may only get 1 free video. Decide whether to have students share accounts or use individual accounts with limited output. |
| 0:40-1:00 | 20 min | **Guided Practice: Write Your Script.** Students use ChatGPT to write a 60-second script. They can choose from: (1) Personal introduction video for LinkedIn (2) Educational video explaining a topic (3) Chatbot demo introduction (4) "Why I love AI" short video. They draft, edit, and time themselves reading aloud. Script must be under 150 words (approximately 60 seconds spoken). | Walk around helping students refine scripts. Common issue: scripts that are too long. Help them cut ruthlessly. "Every word must earn its place." Read scripts aloud with students and time them. |
| 1:00-1:15 | 15 min | **Guided Practice: Create Your AI Video.** Students go to HeyGen and create their video. Steps: (1) Pick avatar and voice (2) Paste script (3) Choose background (4) Generate. While videos generate, students who are waiting explore CapCut features. | Manage the queue: if all students hit HeyGen at once, the free tier may throttle. Stagger creation or have half the class start with CapCut exploration while the other half does HeyGen, then swap. |
| 1:15-1:20 | 5 min | **Break.** | Quick break while HeyGen videos render. |
| 1:20-1:40 | 20 min | **Independent Practice: CapCut Editing.** Students open CapCut. Explore AI features: (1) Auto-captions (add subtitles automatically) (2) AI background removal (3) Templates (ready-made video templates) (4) Text effects and transitions. Students either: (a) Edit their HeyGen video in CapCut (add captions, transitions) or (b) Create a new short video in CapCut using templates and AI features. | CapCut is intuitive and teens often already know it from social media. Let the experienced users help beginners. Focus on 2-3 key features rather than trying to cover everything. Auto-captions is the most universally useful feature. |
| 1:40-1:55 | 15 min | **Independent Practice: Final Video Polish + Share.** Students finalize their videos. They export and save. They post their video on LinkedIn (optional) or save for Showcase Day. Students who finish early help others or create a second video. | Some students will create amazing content; others will struggle with the tools. Both are fine. The goal is exposure to AI video creation, not professional-quality output. |
| 1:55-2:00 | 5 min | **Wrap-Up: Content Creator Unlocked.** Show 2-3 best student videos on the projector. Class applause. Discuss: "You can now create professional videos without a camera, studio, or editing team. That is a real-world skill." Preview Session 15: "Tomorrow, AI helps you with school projects and science fair reports." | The video showcase is always a crowd-pleaser. Students are proud of their videos. Save all videos for potential Showcase Day use. |

### Homework / Take-Home Challenge
Create one more 60-second AI video on ANY topic you want. It could be educational, creative, or fun. Bring it to class tomorrow.

## Session 15: AI for School Projects & Science Fair Reports

**Date:** Week 4, Day 3
**Duration:** 120 minutes
**Topic:** Using AI to Create Outstanding School Projects and Reports
**Learning Objectives:**
1. Use AI to research, structure, and write science fair reports
2. Create AI-powered presentations for school projects
3. Generate data visualizations and diagrams using AI
4. Apply AI tools to any school assignment efficiently and ethically

### Materials Needed
- Projector with internet access
- Student laptops with ChatGPT, Claude, Canva, and Google Slides access
- Whiteboard and markers
- Handout: "AI Project Workflow Checklist" (print 1 per student)
- Handout: "Science Fair Report Template" (print 1 per student)

### Pre-Session Setup (Trainer)
- Prepare a demo science fair report (AI-assisted) to show as example
- Create a demo Canva presentation using AI features
- Print both handouts
- Have a list of sample project topics ready

### Minute-by-Minute Session Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "The Night Before the Project Is Due."** Humorous scenario: "It is 10 PM, your science fair project is due tomorrow, and you have nothing. What do you do?" Students share horror stories. "What if AI could turn that 10 PM panic into a structured project in 30 minutes? Let us see how." | Every student relates to last-minute project panic. This warm-up creates immediate relevance and engagement. |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap Session 14 + Homework Videos.** Show 2-3 homework videos on the projector. Quick applause. Transition: "You have made AI videos, chatbots, and professional profiles. Today, we tackle the thing that affects your marks the most: school projects." | Keep video sharing brief but celebratory. Transition to the most academically relevant session of the week. |
| 0:15-0:25 | 10 min | **Instruction: The AI Project Workflow.** Teach the 6-step workflow: (1) **Research** -- Use AI to gather information and explain concepts (2) **Structure** -- Use AI to create an outline (3) **Draft** -- Use AI to write sections (you edit heavily) (4) **Design** -- Use AI for visuals and presentations (5) **Review** -- Use AI to proofread and improve (6) **Present** -- Use AI to create speaking notes. | Draw the 6 steps on the whiteboard as a flowchart. Emphasize step 3: "AI writes a DRAFT. YOU make it yours. Copy-pasting AI output is not a project -- it is plagiarism. Using AI as a starting point is smart." |
| 0:25-0:35 | 10 min | **Instruction: Research with AI (Ethically).** Demo: research a topic using ChatGPT AND cross-reference with Google. Show: "ChatGPT says the Taj Mahal was built in 1632. Let us verify on Google. Yes, confirmed." Teach the verify-everything rule. Also show how to ask AI for sources: "Provide sources for each fact" (and verify those sources exist). | This is a critical ethical discussion. Be direct: "AI can make up facts and fake sources. ALWAYS verify. Your teacher will know if you did not." Show a live example of AI making up a source -- this is powerful and memorable. |
| 0:35-0:40 | 5 min | **Live Demo: Science Fair Report in 5 Minutes.** Speed demo: (1) Topic: "Effect of music on plant growth" (2) Ask ChatGPT for outline (3) Ask for introduction paragraph (4) Ask for methodology section (5) Ask for conclusion template. Show the full report structure generated in under 5 minutes. Then: "This is your starting point. Now YOU add your actual experiment data, your observations, and your personal analysis." | The speed demo impresses students but the follow-up caveat is essential: "This is a skeleton. Without your actual work, it is empty. AI gives structure; you give substance." |
| 0:40-1:00 | 20 min | **Guided Practice: Build Your Project.** Students choose a project topic (from a list OR their own upcoming school project). Using the AI Project Workflow Checklist, they: (1) Research the topic with AI (2) Generate an outline (3) Draft the introduction and one body section (4) Identify 3 facts that need verification. Trainer circulates and helps. | Encourage students working on REAL upcoming school projects -- this gives them immediate value. Students without a current project can choose from the sample list: renewable energy, water conservation, effects of social media on teens, space exploration, Indian freedom fighters. |
| 1:00-1:15 | 15 min | **Guided Practice: AI-Powered Presentations.** Students open Canva or Google Slides. Use AI features: (1) Canva's AI presentation generator: "Create a 10-slide presentation on [topic]" (2) OR use ChatGPT: "Create a Google Slides outline with 10 slides for a presentation on [topic]. For each slide, give the title and 3 bullet points." Students start building their presentation. | Canva's AI features are impressive for presentation creation. If students do not have Canva accounts, Google Slides + ChatGPT for content works well too. Focus on structure over design -- they can make it pretty later. |
| 1:15-1:20 | 5 min | **Break.** | Quick break. |
| 1:20-1:40 | 20 min | **Independent Practice: Complete Project Package.** Students work on creating a complete project package: (1) Written report (at least introduction + outline of remaining sections) (2) Presentation (at least 5 slides) (3) Speaking notes for presentation (generated with AI: "Write speaker notes for each slide, 30 seconds per slide, conversational tone"). | Walk around checking quality. Push students to personalize AI output: "Change this sentence to sound like YOU." "Add your opinion here." "This fact needs a source -- verify it." |
| 1:40-1:55 | 15 min | **Independent Practice: Peer Review.** Students swap projects with a partner. Using a simple rubric, they evaluate: (1) Is it clear? (2) Is it accurate? (3) Does it sound like the student or like AI? (4) Are sources provided? (5) Is the presentation visually appealing? They give 3 specific improvement suggestions. | The "does it sound like AI?" question is critical. Teach students to identify AI-sounding text: "In conclusion," "It is important to note that," "Furthermore," etc. Help them develop their own voice. |
| 1:55-2:00 | 5 min | **Wrap-Up: Tomorrow Is Showcase Day.** Remind students what to prepare: (1) Chatbot demo ready (2) LinkedIn profile live (3) One project/video to show (4) 1-minute presentation practiced (5) Dress nicely -- parents and teachers will be there. "You have 4 weeks of incredible work to show. Be proud. Be confident. Be yourselves." | Send a final reminder message to parents about Showcase Day timing and what to expect. Prepare the classroom setup for tomorrow (chairs for guests, projector tested, schedule ready). |

### Homework / Take-Home Challenge
FINAL PREPARATION for Showcase Day: (1) Practice your 1-minute chatbot presentation 3 times (2) Make sure your bot is working and shareable (3) Charge your laptop fully (4) Prepare for any questions about your work.

## Session 16: Future Careers in AI + Showcase Day

**Date:** Week 4, Day 4
**Duration:** 120 minutes
**Topic:** AI Career Exploration + Final Student Showcase
**Learning Objectives:**
1. Identify 10+ career paths in AI and technology
2. Present their chatbot and course work to an audience
3. Demonstrate prompt engineering and AI skills under pressure
4. Receive feedback from parents, teachers, and peers

### Materials Needed
- Projector with internet access and HDMI adapter
- Student laptops fully charged
- Whiteboard and markers
- Chairs arranged for audience (parents/teachers)
- Printed certificates (from TARAhut AI Labs)
- Printed feedback forms for audience (parents/teachers)
- Timer for presentations
- Camera/phone for documentation (photos and video of showcase)
- Small refreshments (optional)

### Pre-Session Setup (Trainer)
- Arrange room: presentation area with projector, audience seating, demo stations
- Test projector and internet connection
- Print certificates with each student's name
- Print audience feedback forms
- Set up a "demo walk" area where parents can try students' chatbots
- Have a running order (student presentation sequence)
- Welcome sign: "TARAhut AI Labs -- AI Explorer Senior Showcase Day"

### Minute-by-Minute Session Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "4 Weeks Ago vs Today."** Trainer addresses the class (before audience arrives): "4 weeks ago, most of you had never written a prompt. Today, you have built chatbots, created AI videos, set up LinkedIn profiles, and developed AI study systems. That is incredible. Own it today." | This is a confidence boost before the pressure of presenting. Speak from the heart. Name specific accomplishments by specific students if appropriate. |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Quick Prep: Final Rehearsal.** Students do a final 1-minute rehearsal with a partner. Check: bot is working, presentation is ready, laptop is charged. Address any last-minute panic. "You are ready. Trust yourself." | Walk around doing quick tech checks. Fix any broken links, low batteries (have chargers ready), or missing files. This is backstage preparation time. |
| 0:15-0:25 | 10 min | **Instruction: AI Careers Overview.** Before the audience arrives, deliver a quick lesson on AI careers. Show 10 career paths on the projector: (1) Prompt Engineer (2) AI Product Manager (3) Data Scientist (4) AI Ethics Officer (5) Machine Learning Engineer (6) AI Content Creator (7) Chatbot Designer (8) AI Researcher (9) AI Startup Founder (10) AI Trainer/Teacher. For each: one sentence about what they do + average salary range in India. | This gives students future vision. Connect each career to something they learned in the course: "You already have prompt engineering skills. Companies pay 8-15 LPA for prompt engineers in India." Make salaries real and motivating. |
| 0:25-0:35 | 10 min | **Instruction: Your AI Career Roadmap.** Quick exercise: students write down (1) Their #1 AI career interest (2) One step they can take this year to move toward it (3) One skill they want to learn next. Collect these -- they go in the portfolio. Discuss: "You are 14-16 years old with AI skills. By the time you are 20, AI will be in EVERY job. You are ahead of the curve." | This is aspirational and forward-looking. End the instruction portion on a high note: "The future belongs to people who can work WITH AI. That is now you." |
| 0:35-0:40 | 5 min | **Audience Welcome.** Parents and teachers arrive. Trainer welcomes them: brief explanation of the course, what students learned over 4 weeks, and what they will see today. "Your children have done remarkable work. Prepare to be impressed." | Be professional and warm. Parents should feel that their INR 3,999 was well spent. Set expectations: each student presents for ~2-3 minutes, then there is a demo walk, then certificates. |
| 0:40-1:10 | 30 min | **Showcase: Student Presentations.** Each student presents (2-3 minutes): (1) Their name and what they learned (2) Live demo of their chatbot (3) One other thing they are proud of (video, project, LinkedIn). Audience can ask 1 question per student. Timer on screen. Applause after each. | Manage time strictly: 2-3 minutes max per student. If 15 students, this takes 30-45 minutes. For larger classes, do half individual presentations and half group demos. Clap loudly after each student -- model the supportive behavior you want from the audience. Help shy students: stand near them, ask leading questions if they freeze. |
| 1:10-1:15 | 5 min | **Break / Transition.** Quick transition from presentation to demo walk format. Students set up at "demo stations" (their desks with laptops open to their chatbots). | Rearrange quickly. Students at their desks with bots open. Parents circulate. |
| 1:15-1:35 | 20 min | **Demo Walk: Parents Try the Bots.** Parents and teachers walk around, interact with students' chatbots, and ask questions. Students demo their bots, explain how they built them, and answer questions. Trainer circulates, facilitating conversations. | This is the most powerful part for parents. Seeing their child's creation and hearing them explain AI concepts is impactful. Take photos/videos. Help parents who are not tech-savvy interact with the bots. |
| 1:35-1:40 | 5 min | **Audience Feedback.** Parents fill out feedback forms: (1) Was the course valuable? (2) What impressed you most? (3) Would you recommend this to other parents? (4) Interest in advanced courses? | Collect ALL feedback forms. This is invaluable for franchise operations and marketing. If parents are willing, get video testimonials on phone. |
| 1:40-1:50 | 10 min | **Certificate Ceremony.** Trainer calls each student by name. Student walks up, receives certificate, handshake, and a brief personal compliment from the trainer. Photo with each student. Group photo at the end. | Make it feel special and formal. Call each name clearly. The personal compliment should reference something specific: "Priya built the best school reception bot I have ever seen." Parents love hearing their child praised specifically. |
| 1:50-1:55 | 5 min | **Trainer Closing Speech.** "These students have not just learned AI tools. They have learned to think critically, build solutions, and present their work with confidence. They are ready for the AI future. Thank you to every parent for supporting them." Mention next steps: advanced courses at TARAhut AI Labs, alumni community access, continued learning. | This is also a soft pitch for advanced courses. Have brochures or QR codes ready for parents interested in the Generative AI & Prompt Engineering course or other offerings. |
| 1:55-2:00 | 5 min | **Final Goodbye.** Students and parents mingle. Trainer is available for individual conversations. Exchange contact information for the alumni group. Take final group photos. | Be present and available. Parents often want to discuss their child's potential or ask about advanced learning paths. Have your course catalog and pricing ready for these conversations. |

### Post-Session Tasks (Trainer)

- Compile parent feedback forms into a summary report
- Upload showcase photos/videos to the TARAhut AI Labs social media (with parent consent)
- Send thank-you messages to all parents via WhatsApp
- Add all students to the alumni community
- Identify students to invite for advanced courses
- Complete the franchise session report
