# Week 2: Board Exam Prep with AI

## Week Overview

**Theme:** AI as your ultimate study companion for CBSE/ICSE board exams
**Sessions:** 4 (Sessions 5-8)
**Total Hours:** 8 hours
**Key Outcome:** Students have a complete AI-powered study system for board exam preparation

## Session 5: AI Study Plans for Board Exams (CBSE/ICSE)

**Date:** Week 2, Day 1
**Duration:** 120 minutes
**Topic:** Creating Personalized AI-Powered Study Plans
**Learning Objectives:**
1. Generate a personalized study plan for any subject using AI
2. Identify weak areas and create focused revision schedules
3. Use AI to break down syllabus into manageable daily chunks
4. Create a 30-day board exam study plan

### Materials Needed
- Projector with internet access
- Student laptops with ChatGPT/Claude access
- Whiteboard and markers
- Students should bring: their syllabus copy or textbook index
- Handout: "My Study Plan Template" (print 1 per student)
- Calendar printout (blank monthly calendar, 1 per student)

### Pre-Session Setup (Trainer)
- Download CBSE and ICSE syllabus for Class 9 and 10 (key subjects)
- Prepare a demo study plan prompt tested in ChatGPT
- Print blank calendar sheets and study plan templates
- Write on whiteboard: "Fail to plan = Plan to fail"

### Minute-by-Minute Session Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "Study Habits Confession."** Anonymous quick poll on screen (use Google Forms or hand-raise): "How many days before an exam do you start studying?" Options: 1 day, 3 days, 1 week, 2 weeks, "I study regularly." Show results live. Laugh together. "AI is about to fix this." | This is relatable and funny. Most teens will admit to last-minute cramming. No judgment -- frame AI as the solution to their procrastination. |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap Week 1.** Quick framework refresher: show 2 prompts on screen, students identify which framework was used. Then ask: "What was your biggest takeaway from the debate?" 2-3 students share. Transition: "Now we put those prompt skills to REAL use -- your exams." | Bridge Week 1 skills to Week 2 application. "Everything you learned last week becomes your weapon this week." |
| 0:15-0:25 | 10 min | **Instruction: Why Study Plans Fail (and How AI Fixes It).** Common problems: (1) Too ambitious (2) No subject rotation (3) Ignoring weak areas (4) No breaks planned (5) No revision slots. Show how AI can account for ALL of these. Live demo: paste a Class 10 CBSE science syllabus into ChatGPT with the prompt: "Create a 30-day study plan for Class 10 CBSE Science. I am weak in Chemistry, okay in Physics, strong in Biology. I can study 3 hours daily. Include revision days and breaks." | Walk through the AI-generated plan. Point out: "See how it gave more days to Chemistry? See how it added revision? You could never plan this well on your own in 5 minutes, but AI did it instantly." |
| 0:25-0:35 | 10 min | **Instruction: The Perfect Study Plan Prompt.** Teach the formula: Subject + Board + Class + Weak areas + Strong areas + Available time per day + Exam date + Preferred study style (visual/reading/practice) + Special requests. Build one together on the whiteboard with student input. | Have students call out their own details. "Raj, what is your weakest subject? Math? Okay, let us add that." This makes the demo personal and engaging. |
| 0:35-0:40 | 5 min | **Live Demo: Compare Plans.** Show the same study plan request to both ChatGPT and Claude. Compare the two plans. Ask: "Which one is more realistic? Which one accounts for breaks? Which one would you actually follow?" | Students learn that different AI tools have different strengths. Claude often gives more structured plans; ChatGPT may be more creative with study techniques. |
| 0:40-1:00 | 20 min | **Guided Practice: Build Your Personal Study Plan.** Each student writes a prompt for their own study plan using their real subjects, real weak/strong areas, and real available time. They generate the plan in ChatGPT. Trainer circulates and helps refine prompts. Students then refine: "This plan has too much Math on Monday. Adjust to spread Math across the week." | Help students who are unsure of their weak areas: "Think about your last exam. Which subject did you score lowest in?" Push students to iterate: the first AI plan is a draft, not final. Teach them to refine. |
| 1:00-1:15 | 15 min | **Guided Practice: Plan Refinement Workshop.** Students pair up and review each other's plans. Each partner must find 2 things to improve. They then ask AI to adjust: "My study partner thinks I need more revision days. Add a revision day after every 5 days of new topics." | Peer review builds accountability. Students take the plan more seriously when a classmate has reviewed it. |
| 1:15-1:20 | 5 min | **Break.** | Quick stretch. Students often keep working through break on this one -- gently remind them to rest their eyes. |
| 1:20-1:45 | 25 min | **Independent Practice: 30-Day Battle Plan.** Students transfer their AI-generated study plan to the blank calendar printout. They customize it with colors (red = weak subjects, green = strong subjects, blue = revision). They add personal touches: motivational quotes, reward milestones ("After completing Chemistry Chapter 4, treat = ice cream"). | This is arts-and-crafts meets exam prep. Teens love color-coding. The physical calendar is intentional -- it goes on their bedroom wall. Provide colored pens/markers if possible. |
| 1:45-1:55 | 10 min | **Independent Practice: Subject-Specific Deep Dive.** Students pick their weakest subject and ask AI: "Break down [subject] [chapter] into 5 study sessions of 45 minutes each. For each session, list exactly which pages to read, which questions to solve, and what to revise." They add this detail to their calendar. | This adds granularity to the plan. A plan that says "Study Chemistry" is useless. A plan that says "Read pages 45-52, solve Q1-Q5, revise formulas on page 50" is actionable. |
| 1:55-2:00 | 5 min | **Wrap-Up: Plan Commitment.** Each student holds up their calendar and says: "I commit to following this plan for the next 30 days." Take a group photo with calendars. Preview Session 6: "Tomorrow, AI becomes your personal note-taker. Bring a textbook chapter you find boring -- AI will make it interesting." | The public commitment increases follow-through. The photo can be shared with parents. End with encouragement: "You now have a better study plan than most college students." |

### Homework / Take-Home Challenge
Follow your AI study plan for Day 1 tonight. Tomorrow, report: Did you follow it? What worked? What needs adjustment?

## Session 6: AI for Note Summarization

**Date:** Week 2, Day 2
**Duration:** 120 minutes
**Topic:** Turning Textbook Chapters into Smart, AI-Powered Notes
**Learning Objectives:**
1. Use AI to summarize long textbook chapters into concise notes
2. Generate notes in multiple formats (bullet points, mind maps, tables)
3. Upload/paste textbook content and get structured summaries
4. Create "one-page cheat sheets" for any chapter

### Materials Needed
- Projector with internet access
- Student laptops with ChatGPT/Claude access
- Students MUST bring: one textbook (any subject)
- Whiteboard and markers
- Handout: "Note Formats Menu" (print 1 per student)

### Pre-Session Setup (Trainer)
- Prepare a sample textbook paragraph (typed out) for demo
- Test: paste a long paragraph into ChatGPT and get a summary
- Prepare examples of different note formats (bullet, table, mind map text, Q&A, flowchart)
- Print "Note Formats Menu" handout

### Minute-by-Minute Session Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "How Long Would It Take?"** Show a dense 2-page textbook excerpt on screen. Ask: "How long would it take you to summarize this into neat notes?" Students guess (usually 30-60 min). Then paste it into ChatGPT with a summary prompt. Result appears in 10 seconds. Dramatic pause. "That is why we are here today." | The speed contrast is powerful. Students immediately see the value. Use a genuinely difficult/boring textbook passage for maximum impact. |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap Session 5.** Ask 3 students: "Did you follow Day 1 of your study plan last night? What happened?" Quick discussion. Then transition: "A study plan is useless if your notes are bad. Today, we fix your notes forever." | Celebrate students who followed through. Do not shame those who did not -- just encourage them. |
| 0:15-0:25 | 10 min | **Instruction: The 6 Note Formats AI Can Create.** Present on screen/whiteboard: (1) Bullet point summary (2) Table/comparison chart (3) Text-based mind map (4) Q&A format (flashcard-ready) (5) Timeline/sequence (6) One-page cheat sheet. Show a quick example of each using the same textbook paragraph. | Distribute the "Note Formats Menu" handout. Students should see that the SAME content can be presented 6 different ways. Different formats suit different subjects: tables for history comparisons, timelines for events, Q&A for revision. |
| 0:25-0:35 | 10 min | **Instruction: The Perfect Summarization Prompt.** Teach the formula: "Summarize the following [subject] chapter for a Class [X] [CBSE/ICSE] student. Format: [chosen format]. Length: [one page / 200 words / 10 bullet points]. Focus on: [key concepts / exam-important points / formulas]. Difficulty: [simple language / technical terms included]." Demo live with a real chapter excerpt. | Stress the importance of specifying "exam-important" -- this makes AI focus on what will be tested, not just everything in the chapter. Also show the difference between "simple language" and "technical terms" outputs. |
| 0:35-0:40 | 5 min | **Live Demo: The Power of Iteration.** Take the AI summary and refine: "This summary is too long. Cut it to 10 bullet points, each under 15 words." Then: "Now add the key formula for each point." Then: "Highlight which points are most likely to appear in board exams." | Show 3 rounds of refinement in real-time. Students see that the first output is a draft -- iteration makes it exam-ready. |
| 0:40-1:00 | 20 min | **Guided Practice: Summarize Your Textbook.** Students open their textbooks to ANY chapter. They type or photograph a key section (1-2 pages). They paste/describe it to ChatGPT and request a summary in their preferred format. Trainer circulates, helping with prompt refinement. | Some students will try to type entire chapters -- redirect them to focus on 1-2 pages. If students have phone cameras, they can photograph the page and type key points rather than the entire text. Help students whose textbooks are in Hindi to use appropriate prompts. |
| 1:00-1:15 | 15 min | **Guided Practice: Format Comparison.** Students take the SAME chapter section and request summaries in 3 different formats. They compare and choose: "Which format helps ME understand this best?" They write their preference and reasoning in their Prompt Portfolio. | This builds metacognitive skills. Students learn HOW they learn best. Some will prefer tables, others bullet points. There is no wrong answer. |
| 1:15-1:20 | 5 min | **Break.** | Remind students to rest eyes. Quick stretch. |
| 1:20-1:40 | 20 min | **Independent Practice: One-Page Cheat Sheet Challenge.** Students create a complete one-page cheat sheet for their most difficult chapter using AI. Rules: (1) Must fit on one page (2) Must include key formulas/dates/facts (3) Must include 3 potential exam questions (4) Must be in a format they can actually use for revision. | This is the most practical output of the session. Students should save/print this cheat sheet and actually use it. Walk around and check quality. Push students: "Would this ACTUALLY help you during revision? What is missing?" |
| 1:40-1:55 | 15 min | **Independent Practice: Cross-Subject Notes.** Students create AI summaries for 2 more subjects (different from the first one). They experiment with different formats for different subjects. Goal: by end of session, each student has AI-generated notes for 3 different chapters across 3 subjects. | Students who finish early can help others. Encourage them to share their best prompts with classmates. Build a collaborative culture. |
| 1:55-2:00 | 5 min | **Wrap-Up: Note Revolution.** Ask 2-3 students to show their best cheat sheet on the projector. Class votes for the best one. Preview Session 7: "Tomorrow, AI becomes your exam paper setter. It will create mock test questions specifically for YOUR weak areas." | The competitive element drives quality. Students will try harder when they know their work might be shown. |

### Homework / Take-Home Challenge
Create AI-powered notes for 2 chapters you need to revise. Use different formats for each. Bring them to share tomorrow.

## Session 7: Practice Q&A with AI (Mock Exam Questions)

**Date:** Week 2, Day 3
**Duration:** 120 minutes
**Topic:** AI as Your Personal Exam Paper Setter
**Learning Objectives:**
1. Generate subject-specific mock exam questions using AI
2. Create questions at different difficulty levels (easy, medium, hard)
3. Use AI to check answers and get detailed explanations
4. Build a personal question bank for board exam practice

### Materials Needed
- Projector with internet access
- Student laptops with ChatGPT/Claude access
- Whiteboard and markers
- Handout: "Mock Exam Generator Prompt Templates" (print 1 per student)
- Blank A4 sheets for mock test simulation
- Timer for mock test activity

### Pre-Session Setup (Trainer)
- Prepare demo prompts for 3 subjects (Math, Science, Social Studies)
- Test question generation quality in ChatGPT
- Print prompt template handouts
- Set up timer on projector screen
- Have sample CBSE/ICSE question paper format ready

### Minute-by-Minute Session Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "Exam Anxiety Poll."** Quick show of hands: "How many of you feel nervous about board exams?" (Most will raise hands.) "What if you could practice with unlimited exam papers, customized exactly to YOUR weak areas, with instant answers? That is what we are building today." | Normalize exam anxiety. Then immediately position AI as the solution. This creates strong motivation for the session. |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap Session 6.** Ask 2 students to show their homework notes (AI-generated summaries). Quick discussion: "Did the notes help you understand the chapter better?" Transition: "Good notes are step 1. Step 2 is PRACTICE. Today, AI creates unlimited practice questions for you." | Connect the learning chain: study plan (Session 5) + notes (Session 6) + practice questions (today) = complete exam prep system. |
| 0:15-0:25 | 10 min | **Instruction: Types of Exam Questions.** Review board exam question types: (1) MCQ (1 mark) (2) Short answer (2-3 marks) (3) Long answer (5 marks) (4) Case study / passage-based (5) Assertion-Reasoning. Show how AI can generate ALL of these. Demo: generate 5 MCQs for Class 10 Science Chapter 1 using ChatGPT. | Use actual CBSE/ICSE question formats. Students should see that AI-generated questions look EXACTLY like real exam questions. If possible, show a real past paper side by side with AI-generated questions. |
| 0:25-0:35 | 10 min | **Instruction: The Ultimate Question Generator Prompt.** Teach the formula: "Generate [number] [question type] questions for [subject] [chapter/topic] for Class [X] [CBSE/ICSE]. Difficulty: [easy/medium/hard/mixed]. Include: answer key with step-by-step explanations. Format: number each question, put answers at the end." Demo with 2 different subjects. | Emphasize "step-by-step explanations" in the prompt -- this is what makes AI-generated questions better than a question bank book. Students get instant, detailed answers. |
| 0:35-0:40 | 5 min | **Live Demo: Difficulty Scaling.** Same topic, 3 prompts: "Generate 3 EASY questions about photosynthesis," "Generate 3 MEDIUM questions," "Generate 3 HARD board-exam-level questions." Show how difficulty changes. | Students see that they can start easy and work up. This is a studying superpower -- adaptive difficulty based on their comfort level. |
| 0:40-1:00 | 20 min | **Guided Practice: Generate Your Mock Exam.** Each student picks their weakest subject and chapter. Using the prompt template handout, they generate: (1) 5 MCQs (2) 3 short answer questions (3) 2 long answer questions (4) 1 case study question. Trainer circulates and helps refine prompts. Students review the generated questions for quality. | Walk around checking that questions are relevant and accurate. Occasionally AI generates questions outside the syllabus -- teach students to refine: "Only ask questions from NCERT Chapter 3, not general knowledge." |
| 1:00-1:15 | 15 min | **Guided Practice: Answer and Self-Check.** Students attempt to answer 5 of their generated questions WITHOUT looking at the AI answer key. Then they check their answers against AI explanations. For wrong answers, they ask AI: "I got this wrong. Explain why the correct answer is [X] in simple terms." | This builds genuine learning. Students are not just generating questions -- they are actively testing themselves and getting feedback. The "explain why I was wrong" prompt is gold for learning. |
| 1:15-1:20 | 5 min | **Break.** | Quick rest. Some students will want to keep practicing -- encourage a proper break. |
| 1:20-1:40 | 20 min | **Independent Practice: Mini Mock Test.** Trainer picks ONE subject (most popular request). Students generate a 15-minute mini mock test: 10 MCQs from a specific chapter. They take the test on paper (no AI help), timed on the projector. After 15 minutes, they use AI to check their answers and calculate their score. | This simulates a real exam environment. Timer on screen adds pressure. Students get an immediate score and can see exactly where they need to improve. This is often the most impactful activity of the session. |
| 1:40-1:50 | 10 min | **Independent Practice: Question Bank Builder.** Students create a "Question Bank" document. They generate and save: 10 questions each for their 3 weakest chapters across any subjects. Total: 30 questions with answers saved in a Google Doc or notebook. | This becomes a reusable resource. Students should be told: "You now have 30 practice questions you can use tonight, tomorrow, before any test. And you can generate 30 more in 10 minutes anytime." |
| 1:50-1:55 | 5 min | **Independent Practice: Score Tracking.** Students create a simple score tracker: Chapter name, Number of questions attempted, Number correct, Percentage, Date. They fill in today's mini mock test score. | This tracker will be used throughout their exam prep. It shows progress over time and identifies persistent weak areas. |
| 1:55-2:00 | 5 min | **Wrap-Up: The Exam Prep Superpower.** Quick share: "What was your mini mock test score?" Celebrate high scores, encourage low scores ("Now you know exactly what to study!"). Preview Session 8: "Tomorrow, we learn AI flashcards -- the fastest way to memorize anything for exams." | Frame low scores positively: "A low score NOW means a higher score LATER because now you know what to focus on." End with confidence-building. |

### Homework / Take-Home Challenge
Generate a 20-question mock test for your most feared subject/chapter. Take the test. Score yourself. Bring your score and the questions you got wrong.

## Session 8: Revision Using AI Flashcards

**Date:** Week 2, Day 4
**Duration:** 120 minutes
**Topic:** Mastering Revision with AI-Powered Flashcards
**Learning Objectives:**
1. Create AI-generated flashcards for any subject using ChatGPT
2. Use Quizlet to organize and practice flashcards digitally
3. Apply spaced repetition principles for effective memorization
4. Build a complete flashcard deck for board exam revision

### Materials Needed
- Projector with internet access
- Student laptops with ChatGPT/Claude access and Quizlet accounts
- Whiteboard and markers
- Index cards (blank, 50 per student) for physical flashcards (optional)
- Handout: "Spaced Repetition Schedule" (print 1 per student)

### Pre-Session Setup (Trainer)
- Create a demo Quizlet account and a sample deck
- Prepare a demo flashcard generation prompt
- Test Quizlet import feature (bulk import from text)
- Print spaced repetition schedule handouts
- Have index cards ready if using physical option

### Minute-by-Minute Session Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "Memory Test."** Show 15 random items on screen for 30 seconds (mix of words, numbers, images). Cover them. Students write down as many as they remember. Most remember 5-7 items. "What if I told you there is a scientifically proven way to remember almost everything? It is called spaced repetition, and AI makes it 10x easier." | The memory test proves the point: our brains forget fast. This creates urgency for the flashcard solution. Use items related to board exam content for bonus relevance. |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap Session 7 + Homework Check.** Ask 3 students: "What was your mock test score? Which questions did you get wrong?" Brief discussion. Then: "Getting questions wrong is the BEST thing that can happen -- because now you know exactly what to put on flashcards." | Connect yesterday's weak areas to today's flashcard creation. The learning chain continues: plan > notes > questions > flashcards. |
| 0:15-0:25 | 10 min | **Instruction: The Science of Flashcards.** Explain spaced repetition in simple terms: "Study a flashcard today. If you remember it, study it again in 3 days. If you still remember it, study it again in 7 days. If you forget, go back to Day 1." Draw the forgetting curve on the whiteboard. Explain: AI + Quizlet automates this entire process. | Keep the science simple. The forgetting curve visual is powerful -- show how we forget 70% within 24 hours without review. Then show how spaced repetition flattens the curve. |
| 0:25-0:35 | 10 min | **Instruction: AI Flashcard Generation.** Demo the prompt: "Create 20 flashcards for Class 10 CBSE Chemistry Chapter 2: Acids, Bases, and Salts. Format: Front: [question or term], Back: [answer or definition]. Make them exam-focused. Include formulas, reactions, and key definitions." Show the output. Then show how to generate different types: definition cards, formula cards, diagram description cards, date/event cards. | Show at least 3 different flashcard types. For Science: formula cards. For History: date/event cards. For English: vocabulary/literary device cards. Students should see the versatility. |
| 0:35-0:45 | 10 min | **Instruction: Quizlet Setup + Import.** Live demo: (1) Create Quizlet account (2) Create a new study set (3) Show bulk import: copy AI-generated flashcards, paste into Quizlet using the import feature. Show Quizlet's study modes: flashcards, learn, test, match. | Walk through Quizlet slowly. Some students may already know it -- use them as helpers. The bulk import feature saves massive time: generate 50 flashcards in ChatGPT, import all at once into Quizlet. |
| 0:45-1:00 | 15 min | **Guided Practice: Generate and Import.** Students pick ONE chapter from their weakest subject. They generate 20 flashcards using ChatGPT. Then they create a Quizlet account (if they do not have one) and import the flashcards. Trainer circulates helping with setup and import issues. | Common issues: Quizlet import format needs "term, definition" separated by tabs or specific delimiters. Help students format the ChatGPT output correctly. If Quizlet is blocked on school devices, use Google Sheets as alternative (Column A = question, Column B = answer). |
| 1:00-1:15 | 15 min | **Guided Practice: Flashcard Quality Check.** Students review their 20 flashcards in Quizlet. They test themselves on all 20. For any card where the answer is unclear or incorrect, they go back to ChatGPT: "This flashcard answer seems wrong. The correct answer for [topic] is [X]. Please verify and fix." They update their deck. | Quality control is crucial. AI sometimes generates inaccurate flashcards, especially for specific CBSE/ICSE content. Teach students to verify: "Trust but verify. Check AI flashcards against your textbook." |
| 1:15-1:20 | 5 min | **Break.** | Quick break. Hand out the "Spaced Repetition Schedule" printout during break. |
| 1:20-1:40 | 20 min | **Independent Practice: Mega Deck Builder.** Students create flashcard decks for 3 different subjects/chapters. Target: 60 total flashcards (20 per subject). They use ChatGPT to generate, then import into Quizlet or save in Google Docs. Students who finish early can try different flashcard types: "Create flashcards with hints" or "Create flashcards with mnemonics." | Push for quantity AND quality. 60 flashcards across 3 subjects is a solid revision base. Students who work fast can aim for 100. Walk around checking for accuracy and variety. |
| 1:40-1:50 | 10 min | **Independent Practice: Spaced Repetition Calendar.** Using the handout, students plan their flashcard review schedule for the next 2 weeks. Day 1: Study all new cards. Day 2: Review cards you got wrong. Day 4: Review all. Day 7: Review all. Day 14: Final review. They mark these dates on their study plan calendar from Session 5. | This connects Session 5 (study plan) with Session 8 (flashcards). The study plan calendar now has specific flashcard review dates. The system is becoming comprehensive. |
| 1:50-1:55 | 5 min | **Independent Practice: Quizlet Compete.** Quick 3-minute Quizlet Live game (if enough students and internet allows). Students compete using one of the class decks. | Quizlet Live is fun and competitive. If technical issues prevent it, do a verbal rapid-fire: trainer reads the "front" of a card, first student to shout the "back" correctly gets a point. |
| 1:55-2:00 | 5 min | **Wrap-Up: Week 2 Complete Study System.** Recap the entire Week 2 system on the whiteboard: Study Plan + AI Notes + Mock Questions + Flashcards = Board Exam Ready. "You now have a complete exam prep system that no tuition teacher can match." Preview Week 3: "Next week, we build something cool -- your own AI chatbot. No coding required." | Draw the complete system diagram on the whiteboard. Students should feel the power of having all 4 tools. Take a photo of the whiteboard for students to save. |

### Homework / Take-Home Challenge
Use your complete AI study system tonight: (1) Follow your study plan (2) Generate AI notes for one chapter (3) Create 10 practice questions (4) Review your flashcards. Report back on what worked best for you.
