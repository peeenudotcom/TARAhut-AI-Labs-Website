# Phase 2: Content & Creative -- Sessions 13-24 (Weeks 4-6)

## Phase 2 Overview

**Duration:** 3 Weeks, 12 Sessions
**Goal:** Students master AI-powered content creation across three pillars: copywriting, visual design, and video. Every output is created for their REAL client business. By end of Phase 2, students have a complete content portfolio ready for deployment.
**Key Milestone:** By end of Week 6, every student has produced professional-quality written copy, 20+ designed graphics, and 5+ video pieces for their client.

# WEEK 4: AI-Powered Copywriting

## Week 4 Learning Objectives
- Write compelling marketing copy for all major formats using AI
- Master brand voice creation and consistency
- Create ad copy, social media captions, email campaigns, and website copy
- Understand copywriting frameworks (AIDA, PAS, BAB, 4Ps)

## Week 4 Deliverable
**Copy Portfolio** -- A complete collection for their client business: 10 Instagram captions, 5 Facebook ad copies, 1 email campaign (5 emails), 10 WhatsApp messages, 1 Google Business description, 5 Google ad headlines+descriptions, website homepage copy. Each piece must use an appropriate copywriting framework.

### Session 13: Copywriting Fundamentals + Brand Voice (Day 1, Week 4)

**Session Goal:** Students learn core copywriting principles and create a brand voice guide for their client business.

| Time | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|------|----------|---------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | **Warm-Up:** Show 3 real ads (Zomato, Amul, Nike). Ask: "Which one makes you want to buy? Why?" Discuss what makes copy persuasive -- emotion, clarity, urgency, benefit-focus. | Pick ads students will recognize. Zomato's witty billboards are great examples. Print or screenshot them. |
| 0:05-0:15 | **Recap:** Phase 1 summary. Prompt Library status check. Phase 2 overview: "For the next 3 weeks, everything you create is FOR your client." | 0:15-0:22 | **Instruction:** Copywriting 101 -- what is copywriting (writing that sells), difference between copywriting and content writing. The AIDA framework: Attention (hook), Interest (problem/relevance), Desire (solution/benefits), Action (CTA). Walk through AIDA with a local business example. | Write AIDA on the board. Use a real example: salon running a Diwali offer. Attention: "Diwali pe glow chahiye?" Interest: "Dull skin, busy schedule, no time?" Desire: "Our 60-min facial gives you party-ready skin." Action: "Book now on WhatsApp." |
| 0:22-0:29 | **Instruction:** More frameworks -- PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution), BAB (Before-After-Bridge), 4Ps (Promise-Picture-Proof-Push). | 0:29-0:36 | **Instruction:** Brand voice -- what it is (personality of the brand in words), why it matters (consistency builds trust). | 0:36-0:40 | **Instruction:** Using AI for copywriting -- the prompt structure: framework + brand voice + audience + format + constraints. Show: "Using the AIDA framework, write an Instagram caption for [business]. Brand voice: casual, Hinglish, emoji-friendly. Target: women 25-35 in [city]. Max 150 words. End with a WhatsApp CTA." | This combines prompt engineering (Week 3) with copywriting frameworks (today). The two skills together = professional-quality output. |
| 0:40-0:52 | **Guided Practice:** Brand voice guide creation -- pick one student's client business. | 1:02-1:10 | **Guided Practice:** Copy comparison -- take one caption and rewrite it using PAS instead of AIDA. Compare the two versions. Vote on which is better. | 1:10-1:15 | **Break** | |
| 1:15-1:28 | **Independent Work:** Students create a Brand Voice Guide for THEIR client business. Use the template. Some businesses may need a more serious voice (healthcare), others playful (food). Guide students to match the business. |
| 1:28-1:40 | **Independent Work:** Write 5 Instagram captions for their client using AIDA framework + their brand voice guide. Each caption for a different content pillar. Use AI to draft, then manually edit for local relevance. | Quality check: read captions aloud. Do they sound natural? Would a real person in Punjab actually talk like this? If not, edit. |
| 1:40-1:50 | **Independent Work:** Write 3 WhatsApp promotional messages for their client using PAS framework. Short, punchy, emoji-rich, clear CTA (call, visit, order). | WhatsApp messages should be under 100 words. Every word counts. AI tends to be too long for WhatsApp -- students must learn to trim. |
| 1:50-1:55 | **Wrap-Up:** 2 students read their best caption aloud. Class gives feedback: "Would you engage with this post?" | Feedback should be honest but constructive. "The hook is great but the CTA is weak" is better than "it's fine." |
| 1:55-2:00 | **Preview:** Tomorrow -- ad copy mastery. Facebook ads, Google ads, the copy that actually makes money. | "Tomorrow's copy will eventually have real money behind it in Week 9." |

### Week 4 Assessment Rubric: Copy Portfolio

| Criteria | Excellent (5) | Good (3-4) | Needs Work (1-2) |
|----------|--------------|------------|-------------------|
| Completeness | All required pieces present (captions, ads, emails, web copy) | Most pieces present, 1-2 missing | Many pieces missing |
| Framework Usage | Clear use of AIDA/PAS/BAB/4Ps with appropriate selection | Frameworks used but not always the right fit | No clear framework usage |
| Brand Voice | Consistent brand voice across all pieces matching the guide | Mostly consistent with occasional drift | Inconsistent or generic voice |
| Quality | Copy is compelling, clear, and actionable with strong hooks/CTAs | Copy is functional but lacks polish | Copy is generic, weak hooks, unclear CTAs |
| Client Relevance | All copy is customized for the real client business | Most copy is customized, some generic | Copy feels template-y, not customized |
| Presentation | Well-organized portfolio with rationale and professional layout | Organized but missing rationale or polish | Disorganized, hard to review |
