### Session 29: How Meta Advertising Works (Day 1, Week 8)

**Session Goal:** Students understand the Meta advertising ecosystem, campaign structure, and how to think about paid advertising strategically.

| Time | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|------|----------|---------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | **Warm-Up:** "Kitne logon ne paise lagake Instagram pe post boost kiya hai?" (How many of you have ever boosted a post on Instagram?) Ask what happened. Most will say: "Kuch nahi hua" or "Likes aaye par customers nahi." This is the difference between boosting and proper advertising. | This frames WHY they need to learn Meta Ads properly. Boosting is lazy marketing. Proper campaign management is skilled marketing. "After today, you will never just 'boost' a post again." |
| 0:05-0:15 | **Recap:** Week 7 highlights. GBP and WhatsApp are the DESTINATION -- now we learn to drive TRAFFIC to those destinations with ads. The ads pipeline: Ad (attention) --> Landing page/WhatsApp/GBP (conversion) --> Follow-up (retention). | Draw the pipeline on the board. Every ad needs a destination. Students already built the destinations. Now they build the traffic engine. |
| 0:15-0:22 | **Instruction:** How Meta Ads work -- the auction system. Every time someone opens Facebook/Instagram, an auction happens in milliseconds: advertisers bid, Meta shows the winning ad. Winner is determined by: (1) Bid amount, (2) Estimated action rates (will the user click/convert?), (3) Ad quality (relevance, engagement). Low budget + great ad can beat high budget + bad ad. | This is critical conceptual knowledge. Students think "more money = more reach." Reality: better ad creative = lower cost per result. This means their AI-powered creative skills from Phase 2 are a COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE in the ad auction. |
| 0:22-0:29 | **Instruction:** Campaign structure -- 3 levels: (1) Campaign (objective: what do you want?), (2) Ad Set (audience: who do you want to reach? + budget + schedule), (3) Ad (creative: what do they see?). Take a group photo with the Ads Manager "In Review" or "Active" status visible on screens. | This is the EMOTIONAL PEAK of the course. Make it ceremonial. ### Session 34: A/B Testing + Campaign Optimization (Day 2, Week 9)

**Session Goal:** Students analyze their live campaign data, learn A/B testing principles, and make data-driven optimization decisions.

| Time | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|------|----------|---------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | **Warm-Up:** "RESULTS TIME! Kaun pehle batayega -- kitne log ne aapka ad dekha?" (Who wants to share first -- how many people saw your ad?) Let 3-4 students share their initial metrics. Celebrate ALL numbers -- even small ones. | This is exciting. Even 500 impressions and 5 clicks is a win for a first campaign. The energy should be "we're learning from data" not "who spent the least." |
| 0:05-0:15 | **Recap:** What we launched yesterday, key metrics to track (Impressions, Reach, Clicks, CTR, CPC, Amount Spent). Everyone opens Ads Manager and updates their monitoring spreadsheet with overnight data. | Give 5 minutes for everyone to log their numbers. Walk around and help students navigate to the right report. Some will have great numbers, some won't. Both are learning opportunities. |
| 0:15-0:22 | **Instruction:** Reading your data -- what the numbers mean: (1) High impressions but low clicks = ad is being shown but not compelling (creative problem), (2) High CTR but no messages/leads = destination page problem, (3) High CPC = audience too competitive or ad not relevant, (4) Low impressions = audience too narrow or budget too low. Diagnosis framework. | Write the diagnosis framework on the board. Students should be able to look at their numbers and diagnose the problem. This is the transition from "I ran an ad" to "I'm a media buyer." |
| 0:22-0:29 | **Instruction:** A/B testing explained -- what it is (testing two versions to see which performs better), what to test: (1) Different creatives (same audience), (2) Different audiences (same creative), (3) Different copy (same image and audience). Rules: change ONLY one variable at a time. Need at least 500-1,000 impressions per variation for meaningful data. | A/B testing is the foundation of professional campaign management. "Never guess what works. TEST what works. Let data decide." |
| 0:29-0:36 | **Instruction:** Optimization actions -- based on 24-hour data: (1) CPC too high? Pause and create new creative or adjust audience. (2) CTR above 2%? Scale by increasing budget 20-30%. (3) Ad rejected? Fix and resubmit. (4) No results at all? Check if ad is actually delivering (status = Active, not In Review or Paused). | Practical, actionable optimization steps. Not theory -- real actions they take RIGHT NOW based on their real data. |
| 0:36-0:40 | **Instruction:** Retargeting concept -- what it is (showing ads to people who already interacted with your business: visited website, engaged with Instagram, watched video). Why it works: warm audiences convert 3-5x better than cold. How to set up: Custom Audiences based on engagement. "You can't do this today because you need data first. But in 2-3 weeks, you'll have enough data to retarget." | Plant the retargeting seed. Students won't set this up in class but should understand the concept. When they work with clients post-graduation, retargeting is a powerful tool. |
| 0:40-0:55 | **Guided Practice:** Class data review -- display 3-4 students' campaign data on projector (with permission). As a class, analyze each: (1) What's working? (2) What's not? (3) What should they change? Use the diagnosis framework. Trainer facilitates, class discusses. | This is collaborative learning at its best. Students learn from each other's campaigns. Different businesses, different audiences, different results -- but the analysis framework is the same. |
| 0:55-1:05 | **Guided Practice:** A/B test creation -- for students with enough budget: duplicate the existing ad and change ONE element (different image or different primary text). Name it "[Business Name] - Test 2." This runs alongside the original. In 24-48 hours, compare performance. | Not all students will have enough budget for a true A/B test. Those with INR 300+ budget can split: INR 150 on original, INR 150 on variation. Others observe and learn the process. |
| 1:05-1:10 | **Guided Practice:** Lookalike audiences concept -- briefly show how to create a lookalike audience from a Custom Audience (e.g., people similar to your Instagram engagers). Explain: "Once you have 100+ conversions, lookalike audiences become your best targeting tool." | Future skill. Students should know this exists even if they can't use it yet. It's a selling point: "I can help you reach people similar to your best customers." |
| 1:10-1:15 | **Break** | |
| 1:15-1:28 | **Independent Work:** Students analyze their own campaign data. Write a brief report: (1) Campaign status (active, in review, rejected), (2) Key metrics (impressions, reach, clicks, CTR, CPC), (3) Diagnosis (what's working, what's not), (4) Action plan (what they will change or let run). | Walk around with the diagnosis framework. Help students interpret their numbers. Some will be discouraged if CPC is high -- help them see it as data, not failure. "Now you know creative A doesn't work. That IS the learning." |
| 1:28-1:40 | **Independent Work:** Students who can afford to A/B test -- create and launch their test variation. Students who can't -- use AI to generate 3 alternative creatives/copy options they would test if they had more budget. Document the hypotheses: "I would test [X] because [reason]." | Both groups are learning the A/B testing mindset. Having the budget to actually test is ideal, but the THINKING is what matters most. |
| 1:40-1:50 | **Independent Work:** Update monitoring spreadsheet with latest data. Set a reminder to check again tonight and tomorrow morning. Calculate running totals: total spent so far, total clicks, average CPC. | Data discipline. Students should be in the habit of checking and recording numbers twice daily during an active campaign. |
| 1:50-1:55 | **Wrap-Up:** "Sabse important seekh aaj ki kya thi?" (What was today's most important learning?) Quick round. Most will say "data tells you what to fix." | Reinforce the data-driven mindset. "Feelings don't matter in advertising. Only numbers." |
| 1:55-2:00 | **Preview:** Tomorrow -- landing pages and funnels. "Your ad drives traffic. But where does that traffic GO? Tomorrow we build the destination that turns clicks into customers." | "An ad without a great landing page is like a shop with no door. Tomorrow we build the door." |

### Session 35: Landing Page Creation + Funnel Building (Day 3, Week 9)

**Session Goal:** Students build a landing page using Systeme.io or Framer and construct a complete marketing funnel.

| Time | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|------|----------|---------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | **Warm-Up:** Quick ad update -- "How are your campaigns doing? Any surprises?" Let 2-3 students share notable results (good or bad). Celebrate wins, troubleshoot problems quickly. | Keep this brief -- 5 minutes max. The focus today is landing pages, not ad analysis. But the check-in keeps engagement high. |

Every section uses the copy written in Week 4. | Draw the landing page wireframe on the board. Label each section. | 0:29-0:36 | **Instruction:** Systeme.io overview -- why we use it: free plan allows landing pages, funnels, email, and automation all in one platform. Framer is an alternative for students who want a more design-forward option. Show both briefly, then focus on Systeme.io for the guided practice. |

This is their first time building a web page. GO SLOW. The drag-and-drop editor is intuitive but students will have questions.
