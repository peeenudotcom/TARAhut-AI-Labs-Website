# AI Explorer for School Kids -- Junior (Class 5-7)

## TARAhut AI Labs, Punjab, India

---

## Program Summary

AI Explorer Junior is a fun, hands-on introduction to Artificial Intelligence designed for students in Class 5-7 (ages 10-13). Students will discover what AI is, how it works, and how to use AI tools safely and creatively for school projects, storytelling, art, and problem-solving. The course emphasizes learning through play, group activities, competitions, and creative projects.

**Price:** INR 2,999
**Duration:** 4 weeks (16 sessions)
**Schedule:** 4 days/week, 2 hours per session (120 minutes)
**Total Contact Hours:** 32 hours
**Languages:** English / Hindi / Punjabi (trainer adapts to student comfort)
**Max Batch Size:** 15 students
**Age Group:** 10-13 years (Class 5-7)

---

## Pedagogy: 20-40-40 Model (Age-Adapted)

| Component | Standard Split | Junior Adaptation |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Theory / Instruction | 20% | 15% -- shorter lectures, more visual/video-based |
| Guided Practice | 40% | 50% -- more hand-holding, pair work, trainer-led demos |
| Independent Work | 40% | 35% -- scaffolded tasks, buddy system encouraged |

### Age-Appropriate Adaptations

- **More fun, less lecture:** Every concept introduced through a game, story, or video first
- **More guided, less independent:** Trainer walks through every step before students try alone
- **Buddy system:** Students paired up for all activities (stronger + weaker pairing)
- **Visual instructions:** Step-by-step screenshots on screen at all times
- **Celebration culture:** Stickers, points, leaderboard, weekly champions
- **Multilingual support:** Key terms explained in English, Hindi, and Punjabi
- **No jargon:** Technical terms always paired with a kid-friendly explanation

## Safety Guidelines

### AI Ethics for Kids (The 5 AI Rules)

1. **AI is a helper, not a cheater** -- Always tell your teacher when you use AI for homework
2. **AI can be wrong** -- Always check AI answers with a book or adult
3. **Never share personal info** -- No real names, addresses, phone numbers, or photos with AI
4. **Be kind with AI** -- Don't ask AI to make mean or hurtful content
5. **AI is not your friend** -- AI is a tool, talk to real people for real problems

### Online Safety Protocol

- All AI tool accounts created using parent/guardian email addresses
- Students use supervised school/lab accounts only
- No personal devices required (all work done on lab computers)
- Browser history and AI chat logs monitored by trainer
- No social media integration or public sharing without parental approval
- Students never share login credentials with others

### Parental Consent

- **Mandatory consent form** signed by parent/guardian before Session 1
- Consent form covers: AI tool usage, screenshot/photo policy, project sharing
- Parent orientation session (30 min) recommended before Week 1
- Parents receive weekly progress updates via WhatsApp group
- Parents invited to Showcase Day (Session 16)

### Data Privacy

- No student photos uploaded to AI tools
- AI-generated content saved locally, not on public platforms
- Student projects shared only within class unless parent approves wider sharing
- All accounts deactivated after course completion

## Trainer Guidelines

### Teaching Style for Junior Batch

- **Energy level: HIGH.** Be animated, use gestures, move around the room
- **Voice:** Vary tone, use dramatic pauses, whisper for "secrets," loud for excitement
- **Language:** Mix English with Hindi/Punjabi naturally. Key terms in English, explanations in local language
- **Patience:** Repeat instructions 2-3 times without showing frustration
- **Praise:** Specific praise ("Great prompt, Riya!" not just "Good job")
- **Names:** Use every student's name at least 3 times per session
- **Humor:** Dad jokes, funny AI examples, memes -- keep it light
- **Movement:** Never sit for more than 5 minutes. Walk among students during practice

### Classroom Management

- **Seating:** U-shape or pairs facing screen
- **Signal:** Hand-raise signal for attention (trainer raises hand, students mirror)
- **Timer:** Visible countdown timer on screen for all activities
- **Music:** Background music during independent work (lo-fi or instrumental)
- **Noise level:** Use a visual noise meter (green/yellow/red)
