## SESSION 13: Using AI to Solve Real Problems

### Session Objectives
- Students identify real problems in their school/community
- Students use AI tools to brainstorm and prototype solutions
- Students understand that AI is most powerful when applied to real needs

### Materials Needed
- Projector, speakers, whiteboard
- Lab computers with ChatGPT + Canva
- "Problem Solver" worksheet
- Sticky notes (for brainstorming wall)

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|------|----------|----------|---------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "What Bugs You?"** -- Each student writes on a sticky note: ONE thing that bugs them about school, home, or their neighborhood. Stick all notes on the "Problem Wall" (whiteboard). Read a few aloud. "Aaj hum in problems ko AI se SOLVE karenge!" | This grounds the session in real life. Problems might include: "Too much homework," "Dirty streets," "Boring lunch," "Traffic near school." All valid! Group similar problems together on the wall. |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap: "Week 3 Design Skills Review"** -- Quick-fire: "Name a Canva AI feature. What's the Art Prompt Formula? How do you make a good poster? What presentation tool did we use?" 5 questions, award stars. Show 2-3 best posters from last week. | Keep this fast. Week 4 is about APPLICATION -- using everything learned. "Ab tak hum tools seekh rahe the. Ab hum KAAM karenge in tools se!" |
| 0:15-0:40 | 25 min | **Instruction: "AI Problem-Solving Method"** -- Teach the 5-step method: **(1) IDENTIFY** the problem clearly. **(2) ASK AI** for ideas: "I'm a Class 6 student and I noticed [problem]. Give me 10 creative ideas to help solve this." **(3) PICK** the best 2-3 ideas. **(4) PLAN** using AI: "Help me make a simple action plan for [idea]." **(5) CREATE** something: a poster, presentation, letter, or proposal. Demo live: Problem: "Students waste food at lunch." Ask ChatGPT for solutions. Pick best idea. Ask AI for an action plan. Create a quick poster in Canva. | Walk through the ENTIRE 5-step process in one demo. The problem should be relatable. ChatGPT might suggest: awareness campaign, smaller portions option, food sharing system, compost bin. Pick "awareness campaign" and show how to create a poster in Canva with an AI-generated image. "Dekha? Problem se solution tak, AI ne har step mein help ki. But IDEAS tumhari hain!" |
| 0:40-1:10 | 30 min | **Guided Practice: "Problem Solver Teams"** -- Teams of 3-4. Each team picks a problem from the Problem Wall (or their own). Follow the 5-step method: Step 1: Write the problem clearly (5 min). Step 2: Ask AI for 10 ideas (5 min). Step 3: Pick top 2 ideas, discuss why (5 min). Step 4: Ask AI for an action plan (5 min). Step 5: Start creating their output -- poster, presentation slide, or letter (10 min). | Circulate actively. Help teams write clear problem statements -- this is where most get stuck. "Don't say 'school is boring.' Say 'Students in our class feel bored during the 45-minute math period because there are no interactive activities.'" The more specific the problem, the better AI's solutions. Check that teams are DISCUSSING AI suggestions, not just accepting the first one. "AI ne 10 ideas diye -- tum kaunse 2 choose kar rahe ho? KYU?" |
| 1:10-1:15 | 5 min | **Break** | Screen shows: "The biggest problems in the world need the smartest solutions. Maybe YOUR idea will change something!" |
| 1:15-1:50 | 35 min | **Independent Work: "Complete Your Solution"** -- Teams complete their output (poster, presentation, or letter). Must include: (1) Problem statement. (2) Their solution idea. (3) Action plan (3-5 steps). (4) A visual element (AI-generated image, poster, or slide). Last 10 min: Each team presents their problem + solution in 2 minutes. Class votes on "Most Impactful Solution." | The 2-minute presentations are critical practice for Showcase Day. Coach teams: "Problem clearly batao, solution explain karo, aur dikhao ki AI ne kaise help ki." Help teams that are behind -- they can simplify their output to just one poster or one slide. The vote should be for IMPACT, not prettiness. "Kiska solution sabse zyada fark la sakta hai?" |
| 1:50-2:00 | 10 min | **Wrap-Up: "Problem Solver" Award + Preview** -- Announce the Most Impactful Solution team. Award "Problem Solver" badges. Stars of the day. Preview: "Kal hum science projects banayenge AI se -- REAL science fair level projects!" | Save all problem-solution outputs for Showcase Day. These are impressive for parents. |

## SESSION 14: Science Projects with AI

### Session Objectives
- Students use AI to research a science topic deeply
- Students create a science project display (poster + explanation)
- Students learn to verify AI's scientific claims

### Materials Needed
- Projector, speakers, whiteboard
- Lab computers with ChatGPT + Canva + Bing Image Creator
- Science topic list (age-appropriate)
- "Science Project Template" handout
- Chart paper for those who prefer physical display

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|------|----------|----------|---------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "True or False Science"** -- 8 rapid science statements. Students stand for TRUE, sit for FALSE. Include some that AI commonly gets wrong. (1) "Water boils at 100C" -- TRUE. (2) "The Great Wall of China is visible from space" -- FALSE (common myth!). (3) "Lightning never strikes the same place twice" -- FALSE. (4) "Humans only use 10% of their brain" -- FALSE. | After each FALSE answer, say: "AI bhi yeh galat bata sakta hai! Isliye VERIFY karna zaroori hai." This sets up the verification lesson. |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap: "5-Step Problem Solving Review"** -- Quick oral quiz on yesterday's method. Then: "Today we use AI for SCIENCE. Same idea -- AI helps, but YOU verify and create." Show 2 team solutions from yesterday. | Transition from general problem-solving to science-specific application. |
| 0:15-0:40 | 25 min | **Instruction: "AI Science Assistant"** -- Teach science-specific AI use: **(1) Research:** "Explain [topic] for a Class 7 student. Include: what it is, how it works, why it matters, and 1 fun experiment I can describe." **(2) Verify:** "Always check AI's facts with a second source (textbook, Google, another AI)." Demo: Ask ChatGPT about photosynthesis. Check 2 facts against NCERT textbook. **(3) Visualize:** Use Bing Image Creator: "Scientific diagram of photosynthesis, educational style, labeled, colorful." **(4) Display:** Combine in Canva: title, explanation, image, fun fact, experiment description. Full demo on projector with one topic. | Use a topic from NCERT syllabus for maximum relevance. Demo the verification step carefully: "AI ne kaha ki photosynthesis mein oxygen release hota hai. NCERT mein check karo -- HAAN, sahi hai! But what if AI said something wrong? Tab hum NCERT wali information use karenge, AI wali nahi." Show the Bing Image Creator generating a scientific diagram. These look professional and students love them. The Canva display should look like a science fair board. |
| 0:40-1:10 | 30 min | **Guided Practice: "My Science Project" (Phase 1)** -- Each student/pair picks a topic: Solar System, Human Digestive System, Water Cycle, Electricity & Circuits, Plant Life Cycle, Food Chain, States of Matter, Sound & Light. Step 1: Research with AI (10 min) -- get explanation, fun facts, experiment idea. Step 2: Verify at least 2 facts (5 min). Step 3: Generate 2-3 scientific images with Bing (10 min). Step 4: Start Canva layout (5 min). | Walk around during research phase. Check that students are writing in THEIR OWN WORDS, not copying AI. "AI ne explain kiya, ab APNI language mein likho -- jaise tum apne dost ko samjha rahe ho." For verification, if no textbooks available, students can ask a DIFFERENT AI or use Google. "ChatGPT ne kaha, ab Google se check karo." The image generation step is fun -- help students write scientific image prompts. |
| 1:10-1:15 | 5 min | **Break** | Screen shows amazing student science fair projects for inspiration. |
| 1:15-1:50 | 35 min | **Independent Work: "My Science Project" (Phase 2 -- Completion)** -- Students complete their science project display in Canva (or on chart paper with printed images). Must include: (1) Topic title. (2) "What is it?" explanation (own words, 4-5 sentences). (3) "How does it work?" (3-4 steps). (4) AI-generated scientific image. (5) 2 fun facts. (6) Simple experiment idea. Last 10 min: 4-5 students present their project in 1.5 minutes each. | Help students who are behind prioritize: "Pehle explanation aur image lagao, phir fun facts add karo." For students who finish early, challenge them to add a "Did You Know?" section or a quiz question about their topic. The mini-presentations are practice for Showcase Day. Give feedback: "Explain karte waqt apne project ki taraf point karo, just like a real scientist!" |
| 1:50-2:00 | 10 min | **Wrap-Up: "Young Scientist" Award + Preview** -- Award for best science project. Stars of the day. Preview: "Kal hum QUIZ GAMES banayenge -- tum Kahoot jaisa quiz banaoge apne doston ke liye! Aur parson SHOWCASE DAY hai -- parents aa rahe hain!" | Build excitement for both remaining sessions. Remind students to tell parents about Showcase Day. |

## SESSION 15: AI Quiz Games (Kahoot-Style)

### Session Objectives
- Students CREATE quiz questions using AI
- Students build a mini quiz game for classmates
- Students play each other's quizzes in a competition

### Materials Needed
- Projector, speakers, whiteboard
- Lab computers with ChatGPT + Kahoot (or Google Forms for quiz creation)
- Quiz template (10 questions)
- Scoreboard for competition
- Prizes for quiz winners

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|------|----------|----------|---------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "Speed Round"** -- Trainer asks 10 rapid questions from ALL topics covered in the course. Students shout answers. No hands needed. Fastest correct answer gets a point. | This reviews the entire course in 5 minutes. Mix AI knowledge, prompt skills, design rules, safety rules. Get the energy HIGH for today's fun session. |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap: "Science Project Show"** -- Show 3-4 science projects from yesterday. Quick applause for each. Then: "Aaj hum quiz BANAYENGE. Tum teacher banoge aur apne doston ko test karoge!" | Showing science projects keeps yesterday's work alive. Transition to quiz-making with excitement. |
| 0:15-0:40 | 25 min | **Instruction: "How to Make a Great Quiz"** -- Teach quiz design: (1) Good questions are CLEAR and have ONE right answer. (2) Wrong options should be believable (not obviously silly). (3) Mix easy, medium, and hard questions. (4) Add fun facts after each answer. Demo: Use ChatGPT to generate quiz questions. Prompt: "Create 5 multiple choice questions about AI for Class 6 students. Each question should have 4 options with 1 correct answer. Include a fun fact after each answer. Mix difficulty levels." Then show how to enter these into Kahoot (or Google Forms as backup). Edit AI's questions to make them better. | Important lesson: even AI-generated quiz questions need EDITING. Show a question AI made and improve it: "This question is too easy, let's make it trickier." "This wrong option is obviously wrong -- let's make it more believable." Teach students to be QUIZ DESIGNERS, not just copy-pasters. If Kahoot accounts are an issue, use Google Forms quiz mode. Kahoot is more fun for playing but harder to set up. Trainer decides based on available accounts. |
| 0:40-1:10 | 30 min | **Guided Practice: "Build Your Quiz"** -- Each pair creates a 10-question quiz. Topic: ANYTHING they learned in this course (AI basics, prompt writing, design, safety, science). Use ChatGPT to generate initial questions, then EDIT and IMPROVE at least 5 of them. Enter into Kahoot or Google Forms. Step-by-step guidance from trainer. | Walk around checking quiz quality. Common problems: (1) Questions too easy -- "Iska answer obvious hai, thoda mushkil banao." (2) Questions copied directly from AI without editing -- "Apne words mein likho, aur ek question khud se banao." (3) Wrong options too silly -- "Wrong answers bhi real lagne chahiye!" Require each pair to write at least 2 questions entirely from scratch (no AI). This tests their knowledge. |
| 1:10-1:15 | 5 min | **Break** | Screen shows: "Get ready for the ULTIMATE QUIZ BATTLE! Sabse smart kaun hai?" |
| 1:15-1:50 | 35 min | **Independent Work: "Quiz Tournament"** -- GAME TIME! Play 3-4 of the best student-created quizzes on the projector. All students play on their computers/devices. Kahoot-style scoring (speed + accuracy). After each quiz, the pair who created it reveals the answers and shares fun facts. Running scoreboard on the whiteboard. Final round: Trainer's "Grand Finale" quiz (10 questions covering the entire course). | This is the MOST FUN session. Make it feel like a game show. Trainer is the host with energy and commentary: "Ohhh, sirf 5 seconds bache hain! Jaldi jaldi!" "80% ne sahi answer diya -- SHAABAASH!" Play music between rounds. Keep score visible. For the Grand Finale quiz, include tricky questions from all 4 weeks. The competition should be fierce but friendly. Award prizes: "Quiz Champion," "Fastest Finger," "Most Consistent." |
| 1:50-2:00 | 10 min | **Wrap-Up: Quiz Champions + Showcase Day Prep** -- Announce quiz tournament winners. Then: SERIOUS TALK about tomorrow. "Kal Showcase Day hai. Parents aa rahe hain. Tum apna BEST project choose karo -- poster, story, presentation, science project, ya koi bhi. Kal tum 2 minutes mein present karoge." Give students their project checklist for tomorrow. | Distribute Showcase Day checklist: (1) Choose your best project. (2) Practice explaining it in 2 minutes. (3) Be ready to answer questions. (4) Dress nicely. (5) Be proud of your work! Help students who are anxious: "Simple rakhna, bas batao kya banaya aur AI ne kaise help ki." |

## SESSION 16: Showcase Day (Students Present to Parents)

### Session Objectives
- Students present their best AI project to parents and peers
- Parents see the value of the AI Explorer program
- Students receive certificates and celebrate their achievements

### Materials Needed
- Projector, speakers, whiteboard
- All student projects loaded and ready
- Printed certificates ("AI Explorer Junior" for all students)
- Special awards/trophies for category winners
- Snacks/refreshments for parents
- Camera for photos/video
- Parent feedback forms
- Seating arrangement for parents
- Welcome banner/poster

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|------|----------|----------|---------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Welcome & Setup** -- Parents arrive and are seated. Welcome banner displayed. Soft background music. Student projects displayed on screens around the room. Trainer greets parents personally. | Set up 30 min BEFORE session. All projects pre-loaded. Test projector, speakers. Arrange chairs in audience format. Have water/chai ready for parents. Name cards for students at their stations. |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Opening: Trainer Welcome Speech** -- Trainer welcomes parents. Brief overview: "Your children have spent 4 weeks learning AI tools -- responsibly, creatively, and practically. Today they will show you what they've built." Show a 2-minute highlights slideshow (best moments from each week). Introduce the showcase format. | Keep the speech WARM and PROUD. "Aapke bacchon ne 4 hafton mein jo seekha hai, woh bahut se adults bhi nahi jaante. Aaj aap dekhenge." The highlights slideshow should include class photos, project screenshots, funny moments. Parents love seeing their kids engaged and happy. |
| 0:15-0:40 | 25 min | **Student Presentations (Round 1)** -- First 8 students present, 2 minutes each with 1 minute for parent questions. Students present at the projector, showing their project on screen. Format: "Hi, I'm [name]. I made [project] using [AI tool]. It's about [topic]. AI helped me by [specific help]. I learned [key learning]." | Coach students right before they present: "Relax, smile, loud voice." Have a timer visible. Gently cut off at 2.5 minutes if needed. Encourage parents to ask simple questions: "Kaise banaya?" "AI ne kya kiya?" Clap after EVERY presentation. Take photos of each student presenting. If a student freezes, help them with prompts: "Apna project dikhao... yeh kaise banaya tha?" |
| 0:40-1:10 | 30 min | **Student Presentations (Round 2) + Gallery Walk** -- Remaining 7-8 students present (same format). Then 10-minute Gallery Walk: parents walk around the room viewing ALL projects on screens. Students stand by their project and explain to parents who visit. | For the gallery walk, each student should be at their station ready to explain. Some parents will linger at their own child's project -- encourage them to see others too. "Auntie ji, Sahil ka project bhi dekhiye -- bahut creative hai!" This is also a great time for informal parent-trainer conversations. |
| 1:10-1:15 | 5 min | **Break** -- Serve chai/snacks to parents. Students mingle with their families. | Keep this brief but warm. Have helpers serve refreshments. |
| 1:15-1:50 | 35 min | **Awards Ceremony + Certificate Distribution** -- **(1) Category Awards** (10 min): "Prompt Master" (best prompt skills), "AI Artist" (best visual work), "Story Star" (best creative writing), "Young Scientist" (best science project), "Problem Solver" (best solution), "Safety Champion" (best safety awareness), "Design Star" (best design work). **(2) Overall Awards** (5 min): "AI Explorer Champion" (highest total star points), "Most Improved," "Best Team Player." **(3) Certificate Distribution** (15 min): Every student receives their "AI Explorer Junior" certificate. Trainer calls each name, student comes to front, gets certificate, shakes hands, photo taken. **(4) Class Photo** (5 min): Group photo with all students, certificates, and trainer. | Make EVERY student feel special. Even if they didn't win a category award, the certificate is their achievement. Call each name clearly, smile, shake hand firmly. Parent photos are crucial -- wait for parents to take their photos. If any student seems left out, give them a spontaneous "Special Recognition" award. The class photo should include the course banner. |
| 1:50-2:00 | 10 min | **Closing: Thank You + Next Steps** -- Trainer thanks parents for their support. Brief mention of what students can do next (advanced courses, practice at home, TARAhut AI Labs Senior course for when they're older). Hand out parent feedback forms. Students share one final learning: "Go around the room, ek ek line mein batao -- kya seekha?" End with a class cheer. | Collect feedback forms from parents -- CRITICAL for franchise reporting and improvement. Mention the Senior course (Class 8-10) for future enrollment. Share the TARAhut AI Labs social media handles for parents to follow. End on a high note: "Aapke bacche ab AI Explorer hain. They know how to use AI safely, creatively, and smartly. Be proud of them!" Final class cheer: trainer says "AI!" students shout "EXPLORER!" three times. |
