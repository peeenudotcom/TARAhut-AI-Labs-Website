# Week 1: Introduction to AI

## AI Explorer Junior (Class 5-7) -- TARAhut AI Labs

## Week 1 Overview

| Session | Title | Key Outcome |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| Session 1 | What is AI? (Fun Examples) | Students can explain AI in their own words |

## SESSION 1: What is AI? (Fun Examples)

### Session Objectives
- Students can explain "What is AI?" in one sentence
- Students can name 5 examples of AI they already use
- Students feel excited and curious about AI

### Materials Needed
- Projector, speakers, whiteboard
- Printed "AI or Not AI?" cards (20 cards per set, 1 set per pair)
- Star point chart on wall
- Name tags for all students

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|------|----------|----------|---------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "Robot Simon Says"** -- Trainer acts as a "robot" and gives commands. Students must follow ONLY if trainer says "Robot says..." Students who move without the command sit down. Last standing wins 5 star points. | Set high energy from minute one. Speak in a robotic voice. Use Hindi/Punjabi for commands to keep it fun. "Robot kehnda hai... haath upar karo!" This game introduces the idea that robots follow exact instructions. |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Introductions & Expectations** -- Each student says name + "one cool thing about computers." Trainer introduces self, the course, the star point system, and the 5 AI Rules poster. Hand out name tags. | First session -- no recap needed. Write the 5 AI Rules on the board. Go through each rule with a funny example. "AI is a helper, not a cheater -- agar AI tumhara homework kare, toh teacher ko pata chal jayega!" Keep it light. |
| 0:15-0:25 | 10 min | **Video + Discussion: "What is AI?"** -- Play a kid-friendly AI explainer video (3-4 min). Then ask: "So what IS AI? Koi bata sakta hai?" Collect answers on whiteboard. Build a class definition together. | Use YouTube video: search "AI for kids explained" -- pick one under 4 min with animations. Pause at key moments. After video, guide students to a simple definition: "AI is when computers learn to do smart things like humans -- but they need OUR instructions." Write this on board in English + Hindi. |
| 0:25-0:40 | 15 min | **Interactive Demo: "Meet the AIs"** -- Show 5 AI examples live on projector: (1) "OK Google" / Siri voice command, (2) YouTube recommendations, (3) Phone face unlock, (4) Spelling autocorrect, (5) ChatGPT answering a fun question. For each, ask students: "Is this AI? How do you know?" | Make it theatrical. For voice assistant, ask a funny question: "Hey Google, kya tum insaan ho?" For ChatGPT, let a student pick a question to ask. For autocorrect, type a misspelled word and show the correction. Goal: students realize AI is ALREADY in their daily life. |
| 0:40-1:10 | 30 min | **Guided Activity: "AI or Not AI?" Card Game** -- Each pair gets 20 cards with technologies written on them (e.g., "Calculator," "Siri," "Light Switch," "Netflix Recommendations," "Washing Machine," "Self-Driving Car," "Fan," "Google Translate"). Students sort into two piles: AI and Not AI. Then class discusses together. | Walk around and listen to debates. Don't correct immediately -- let them argue. Some cards are deliberately tricky (e.g., "Smart Washing Machine" vs "Regular Washing Machine"). After 15 min sorting, go through each card on projector. Ask pairs to defend their answers. Award star points for good reasoning, not just correct answers. Key teaching moment: AI LEARNS and ADAPTS, regular machines just follow fixed rules. |
| 1:10-1:15 | 5 min | **Break** -- Play upbeat music. Students can stretch, use bathroom, have water/snack. | Put a fun AI fact on the screen during break: "Did you know? AI can write songs, paint pictures, and even make jokes! (But the jokes are not always funny...)" |
| 1:15-1:50 | 35 min | **Independent Activity: "My AI World" Poster** -- Each student (or pair) creates a colorful poster on chart paper: "AI in My Life." They draw/write at least 8 AI tools or uses they encounter daily. Decorate with colors, stickers. Best posters go on the classroom wall. | Give each pair chart paper, colors, sketch pens. Play soft background music. Walk around giving ideas to stuck students: "Tumhare phone mein kya kya AI hai? YouTube pe videos kaise suggest hote hain? Google Maps kaise kaam karta hai?" Encourage creativity -- drawings, comics, mind maps all okay. Keep a visible 20-min countdown timer. Last 10 min: 4-5 students present their posters. Award star points for creativity and completeness. |
| 1:50-2:00 | 10 min | **Wrap-Up: Stars of the Day + Preview** -- Award star points for participation. Announce top 3 point earners. Quick oral quiz: "AI kya hai? Ek example batao." Preview tomorrow: "Kal hum dekhenge ki AI SOCHTA kaise hai -- and we'll play a mind-reading game!" | Build excitement for tomorrow. End on a cliffhanger: "Main kal ek game launga jisme AI tumhara dimaag padh sakta hai... ya shayad nahi. Dekhte hain!" Remind students to look for AI around them at home tonight. |

## SESSION 2: How AI Thinks (Pattern Matching)

### Session Objectives
- Students understand that AI learns by finding patterns in data
- Students can play a pattern-matching game and connect it to AI learning
- Students grasp that AI needs LOTS of examples to learn

### Materials Needed
- Projector, speakers, whiteboard
- Printed pattern cards (shapes, numbers, colors)
- "Guess the Pattern" worksheet (1 per student)
- Small prizes for game winners (stickers/candies)

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|------|----------|----------|---------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "AI Fact or Fiction"** -- Trainer reads 5 statements about AI. Students stand up if they think it's TRUE, sit down if FALSE. (1) "AI can recognize your face" -- TRUE. (2) "AI has feelings" -- FALSE. (3) "AI can write stories" -- TRUE. (4) "AI can taste food" -- FALSE. (5) "AI can predict weather" -- TRUE. | High energy start. Make dramatic pauses before revealing answers. For wrong answers, explain WHY in a fun way. "AI can't taste food... abhi tak toh nahi! But who knows in 2030?" Award 1 star point per correct answer. |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap Quiz: "AI Rapid Fire"** -- Quick oral questions from Session 1: "What is AI? Give me 3 examples. What's the difference between AI and a regular machine? What's AI Rule #1?" Students raise hands to answer. | Don't call only on eager students. Use random name picker (pull names from a cup). Award star points for good answers. If students struggle, hint gently. "Yaad karo, kal humne kya kaha tha -- AI SEEKHTA hai, regular machine sirf..." |
| 0:15-0:30 | 15 min | **Instruction: "How Does AI Learn?"** -- Use the "Teaching a Baby" analogy. Show pictures: baby sees 100 cats, learns what "cat" looks like. AI does the same with data! Show a simplified diagram: DATA goes in -> AI finds PATTERNS -> AI makes PREDICTIONS. Demo: Google Quick Draw (quickdraw.withgoogle.com) -- draw something, AI guesses what it is. Let 3-4 students try. | The baby analogy works perfectly for this age. "Jaise ek chhota baccha 100 billiyaan dekhkar seekhta hai ki billi kaisi dikhti hai, waise AI bhi 100,000 photos dekhkar seekhta hai!" For Quick Draw, let students pick what to draw. When AI guesses correctly, ask: "Isne kaise guess kiya? PATTERN dekha!" When AI gets it wrong: "Isko aur practice chahiye -- jaise tumhe bhi math mein!" |
| 0:30-0:40 | 10 min | **Group Game: "Guess My Pattern"** -- Trainer shows sequences on the board and students guess the next item. Start easy (1,2,3,4,?), get harder (red,blue,red,blue,?), then tricky (cat,cat,dog,cat,cat,dog,?). Then connect to AI: "AI does this with MILLIONS of patterns!" | Start with number patterns, then colors, then shapes, then words. Make it competitive -- teams of 4. First team to shout correct answer gets a point. After 5 rounds, connect back: "You just did what AI does! But AI can find patterns in CRORES of data points in seconds." |
| 0:40-1:10 | 30 min | **Guided Practice: "Train Your Own AI"** -- Use Teachable Machine (teachablemachine.withgoogle.com). Trainer demos on projector: train a model to recognize hand gestures (thumbs up vs thumbs down). Then students (in pairs) train their own model to recognize 3 different poses. Step-by-step on screen. | This is the WOW moment. Walk through EVERY step on projector before students start. Step 1: Open site. Step 2: Click "Image Project." Step 3: Hold up thumbs-up, click record. Step 4: Hold up thumbs-down, click record. Step 5: Click Train. Step 6: TEST! When it works, students will be amazed. Let them experiment with funny poses. "Jitne zyada photos doge, utna better seekhega -- just like YOU!" |
| 1:10-1:15 | 5 min | **Break** | Screen shows: "Fun fact: Google's AI was trained on 10 MILLION YouTube videos to learn how to recognize cats. Dus lakh videos sirf billiyoon ke liye!" |
| 1:15-1:50 | 35 min | **Independent Work: "Pattern Detective" Worksheet + Teachable Machine Exploration** -- First 15 min: complete the "Guess the Pattern" worksheet (15 pattern puzzles, increasing difficulty). Next 20 min: experiment with Teachable Machine -- try training it to recognize different objects (pencil vs eraser vs sharpener). Challenge: Can you get 100% accuracy? | Worksheet has visual patterns (shapes, colors, numbers, and word patterns). Walk around helping stuck students. For Teachable Machine, challenge fast finishers: "Can you train it to tell the difference between your face and your partner's face?" Award bonus stars for anyone who gets 100% accuracy. Keep track of who finishes the worksheet -- good formative assessment. |
| 1:50-2:00 | 10 min | **Wrap-Up: Key Learnings + Preview** -- Ask 3 students: "Aaj kya seekha?" Summarize: "AI learns from patterns in data, just like you learn from examples. More data = better AI." Stars of the day. Preview: "Kal hum AI scavenger hunt khelenge -- puri class mein AI dhundenge!" | Reinforce the key formula: DATA + PATTERNS = AI LEARNING. Write it on the board. "Kal apne ghar mein dhundna -- kitne AI patterns tumhe dikh sakte hain?" |

## SESSION 3: AI in Everyday Life (Scavenger Hunt)

### Session Objectives
- Students identify AI in everyday objects and services
- Students complete an AI scavenger hunt and find 15+ AI examples
- Students categorize AI uses (entertainment, safety, education, health, home)

### Materials Needed
- Projector, speakers, whiteboard
- Printed Scavenger Hunt checklist (1 per team)
- Smartphones/tablets for photo evidence (trainer's devices or lab tablets)
- Prize for winning team
- Large category chart on wall (Entertainment / Safety / Education / Health / Home)

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|------|----------|----------|---------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "AI Charades"** -- 5 volunteers act out AI technologies (voice assistant, self-driving car, robot vacuum, face unlock, autocorrect). Rest of class guesses. | Pick enthusiastic students. Give them the card privately, let them act without speaking. Award star points for good acting AND good guessing. |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap: "Pattern Recap Race"** -- Two teams at the whiteboard. Trainer shows a pattern, first to write the next item wins. 5 rounds. Then: "What did we learn patterns have to do with AI?" | Quick, competitive, gets energy up. Use patterns from yesterday plus new ones. Connect back: "AI finds patterns in everything -- faces, voices, words, shopping habits..." |
| 0:15-0:40 | 25 min | **Instruction: "AI is EVERYWHERE"** -- Interactive slideshow showing AI in 6 categories with real examples kids relate to: (1) Entertainment: Spotify recommendations, YouTube suggestions, game NPCs. (2) Social: Instagram filters, WhatsApp smart replies. (3) Education: Google Translate, Grammarly. (4) Home: Alexa, smart TV, robot vacuum. (5) Transport: Google Maps traffic, Uber ride matching. (6) Health: fitness bands, eye checkup machines. For each, ask students if they've used it. | Make it conversational, not a lecture. "Kitne logon ke ghar mein Alexa hai? Nahin? Okay, kitne logon ke phone mein Google Assistant hai? WAHI TOH AI HAI!" Use lots of images and short video clips. For each category, spend 3-4 min. Let students share their own examples. Write student examples on the board -- they love seeing their ideas acknowledged. |
| 0:40-1:10 | 30 min | **Guided Activity: "AI Scavenger Hunt" -- PLANNING PHASE** -- Divide class into teams of 3-4. Each team gets a Scavenger Hunt checklist with 20 AI items to find (some in the classroom, some require thinking). Examples: "Find something that uses voice recognition," "Find something that recommends content to you," "Find something that auto-corrects your spelling." Teams plan their strategy, then hunt within the lab/classroom for 15 min. Remaining items they write/draw from memory of home/school. | The checklist has items of varying difficulty. Easy: "Find autocorrect" (open a phone). Medium: "Find an AI that recognizes faces" (phone camera). Hard: "Find an AI that predicts something" (weather app). Give teams 5 min to plan, then 15 min to hunt. Trainer circulates, gives hints to stuck teams. "Socho -- agar tumhare phone mein AI hai, toh phone ke andar kya kya AI kar raha hai?" Teams can use the lab computers to search for answers. Each found item = 1 point, with evidence required (show it or explain it). |
| 1:10-1:15 | 5 min | **Break** | Screen shows a funny meme: "AI in 2024: writes essays, creates art, drives cars. Also AI in 2024: still can't fold laundry." |
| 1:15-1:50 | 35 min | **Independent Work: "AI Map of My World" + Scavenger Hunt Scoring** -- First 15 min: Teams present their scavenger hunt findings. Trainer validates and scores. Each correct find = 1 point, bonus points for creative finds not on the list. Next 20 min: Each student creates an "AI Map of My Day" -- a visual timeline from waking up to sleeping showing every AI interaction (alarm, phone unlock, YouTube, Google search, Maps to school, etc.). | For scavenger hunt scoring, make it exciting like a game show. Read each item, teams show evidence. Keep a running score on the board. Award the winning team a prize (stickers, small trophy, extra star points). For the AI Map, provide a template: a timeline from 6 AM to 10 PM with spaces to write/draw AI encounters. Most students will find 10-15 AI interactions in a typical day. "Dekho, subah se raat tak kitni baar AI tumhari help karta hai -- aur tumhe pata bhi nahi chalta!" |
| 1:50-2:00 | 10 min | **Wrap-Up: Scavenger Hunt Winners + Preview** -- Announce winning team, award prizes. Each student shares ONE surprising AI discovery. Stars of the day. Preview: "Kal bahut important class hai -- AI ke saath SAFE kaise rahen. Plus ek secret game hai!" | Build excitement for the safety session (which could be "boring" if not framed right). "Kal ka topic boring lagta hai? Nahi nahi, kal hum ek DETECTIVE game khelenge jisme tumhe AI ke villains dhundne hain!" |

## SESSION 4: Staying Safe Online with AI

### Session Objectives
- Students memorize the 5 AI Safety Rules
- Students can identify unsafe AI interactions in scenarios
- Students understand why personal information must stay private
- Students sign the "AI Safety Pledge"

### Materials Needed
- Projector, speakers, whiteboard
- "AI Safety Detective" scenario cards (10 scenarios per set)
- AI Safety Pledge printout (1 per student)
- AI Safety Rules poster (large, for classroom wall)
- Stickers and markers for pledge decoration

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|------|----------|----------|---------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "Would You Tell a Stranger?"** -- Trainer asks rapid-fire questions. Students shout YES or NO. "Would you tell a stranger your name? Your address? Your favorite color? Your password? Your school name? Your mom's phone number?" Then reveal: "AI tools are also strangers. Let's learn what to share and what NOT to share." | This immediately hooks them into the safety mindset. Start with obviously safe things (favorite color = YES) and escalate to obviously unsafe (password = NO). The gray areas (school name, age) create good discussion. "AI ek stranger hai -- accha stranger, helpful stranger, but still stranger." |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap: "Week 1 So Far" Quiz** -- Kahoot quiz with 10 questions covering Sessions 1-3. Topics: What is AI, how AI learns, AI examples in daily life. Play on projector, students use lab computers or trainer's devices to answer. | Prepare Kahoot in advance. Mix easy and medium questions. Include funny wrong answers. Top 3 scorers get bonus star points. This is also a good assessment checkpoint -- note which topics students struggled with. |
| 0:15-0:40 | 25 min | **Instruction: "The 5 AI Safety Rules" (Deep Dive)** -- Go through each rule with stories, examples, and role-play. **Rule 1: AI is a helper, not a cheater.** Scenario: Riya copies her entire essay from ChatGPT. What happens? **Rule 2: AI can be wrong.** Demo: Ask ChatGPT a tricky question, show it giving a wrong answer. **Rule 3: Never share personal info.** Show what NOT to type into AI. **Rule 4: Be kind with AI.** Show how AI can generate mean content if asked. **Rule 5: AI is not your friend.** Discussion: Can AI feel sad? Can AI care about you? | Spend 4-5 min on each rule. For Rule 1, do a mini role-play: one student is the teacher, one is Riya. Teacher finds out the essay is AI-written. What happens? For Rule 2, deliberately ask ChatGPT "What is the capital of Chandigarh?" or a trick question -- show students it can be confidently wrong. For Rule 3, show a fake chat where someone types their address into AI -- ask: "Yeh safe hai? NAHI!" For Rule 4, don't actually generate mean content -- just discuss why it's wrong. For Rule 5, this is important: "AI tumse pyaar nahi karta. AI tumhara dost nahi hai. AI ek tool hai -- jaise calculator." |
| 0:40-1:10 | 30 min | **Guided Activity: "AI Safety Detective" Game** -- Teams of 3-4 receive 10 scenario cards. Each card describes a situation (e.g., "Arjun asks ChatGPT to write his entire science project," "Priya tells AI her home address to get directions," "Sahil uses AI to check his grammar before submitting"). Teams discuss each scenario and sort into SAFE / UNSAFE / NEEDS DISCUSSION piles. Then class reviews together. | Scenarios should be realistic and relatable. Include gray areas that spark debate. Examples: "Using AI to get IDEAS for a story" (Safe -- as long as you write it yourself). "Asking AI to solve your math homework" (Unsafe -- cheating!). "Using AI to explain a concept you don't understand" (Safe -- that's learning!). "Telling AI your age and class" (Needs Discussion -- minor info but still personal). Let teams debate. Award star points for good reasoning. When class reviews, let the "Needs Discussion" cards generate real conversation. |
| 1:10-1:15 | 5 min | **Break** | Screen shows: "The Internet never forgets. Jo bhi tum online share karte ho, woh hamesha ke liye reh sakta hai. Soch ke share karo!" |
| 1:15-1:50 | 35 min | **Independent Work: "AI Safety Pledge" + "Safety Superhero" Poster** -- First 15 min: Each student reads, understands, and signs the AI Safety Pledge (a simple promise card listing the 5 rules). Students decorate their pledge with colors and stickers. Next 20 min: Each student creates a "Safety Superhero" -- a character they design who protects kids online. Draw the superhero, give them a name, list their powers (e.g., "Password Shield," "Privacy Cloak," "Truth Detector"). | The pledge is a physical card students take home. Make signing it feel important -- like a ceremony. "Yeh tumhara promise hai -- AI ko responsibly use karne ka." For the superhero activity, encourage wild creativity. "Tumhara superhero ka kya power hai? Maybe woh jhooth pakad sakta hai? Maybe woh personal info ko invisible bana deta hai?" Display the best superheroes on the classroom wall. |
| 1:50-2:00 | 10 min | **Wrap-Up: Week 1 Celebration + Preview** -- Announce Week 1 champions (highest star points). Quick chant: trainer calls out each rule number, students shout the rule. Preview Week 2: "Next week, hum ChatGPT ke saath kaam karenge! Hum sikhenge ki AI se SMART questions kaise puchte hain." Collect signed pledges (take photo, return to students). | Make the chant fun and loud. "Rule 1?" "AI IS A HELPER, NOT A CHEATER!" Do it twice -- once normal, once as fast as possible. Take a class photo (with consent) for the WhatsApp parent group. Send parents a Week 1 summary message. |
