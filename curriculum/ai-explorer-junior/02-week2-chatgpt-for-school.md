## SESSION 5: Asking Smart Questions to ChatGPT

### Session Objectives
- Students understand the difference between a good prompt and a bad prompt
- Students can write clear, specific prompts that get useful answers
- Students practice the "Be Specific, Give Context, Say the Format" framework

### Materials Needed
- Projector, speakers, whiteboard
- Lab computers with ChatGPT open (1 per 2 students)
- "Good Prompt vs Bad Prompt" comparison handout
- "Prompt Challenge" worksheet

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|------|----------|----------|---------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "Ask Me Anything (But Badly)"** -- Trainer pretends to be a robot. Students ask questions, but trainer answers LITERALLY. "What's the time?" -> "It's THE time." "Tell me about dogs." -> "Dogs exist." Students learn that vague questions get vague answers. | Be funny and robotic. Exaggerate the literal answers. Students will laugh and naturally start trying to ask better questions. "Dekha? Jab tum vague puchte ho, toh AI bhi vague answer deta hai!" |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap: Quick Safety Rule Check** -- Flash each AI Safety Rule on screen, one word missing. Students fill in the blank. Then: "Aaj se hum ChatGPT use kar rahe hain -- toh konse rules yaad rakhne hain?" Emphasize Rule 1 (helper not cheater) and Rule 3 (no personal info). | This is critical before hands-on ChatGPT use. Make students repeat: "I will NOT share personal information with AI." Ask: "Agar AI galat answer de toh? HAAN -- Rule 2, AI can be wrong! Check karo!" |
| 0:15-0:40 | 25 min | **Instruction: "The 3-Step Prompt Formula"** -- Teach the kid-friendly prompt framework: **(1) Be Specific** -- Don't say "Tell me about animals," say "Tell me 5 interesting facts about dolphins for a Class 6 student." **(2) Give Context** -- Tell AI who you are and why you need this. "I am a Class 7 student working on a science project about..." **(3) Say the Format** -- "Give me a list / Write in simple words / Make it short." Demo live on ChatGPT: show 3 bad prompts, then fix them using the formula. | Do the demos live on projector. Bad prompt: "Tell me about space." Show the long, complicated answer. Good prompt: "Tell me 5 fun facts about space that a 12-year-old would find amazing. Use simple English." Show the much better answer. Do this 3 times with different topics. Let students suggest the "bad" prompt and then help fix it together. Write the formula on the board: SPECIFIC + CONTEXT + FORMAT = GREAT PROMPT. |
| 0:40-1:10 | 30 min | **Guided Practice: "Prompt Makeover"** -- Trainer shows 6 bad prompts on screen one at a time. Students (in pairs) rewrite each one to make it better using the 3-step formula, then type it into ChatGPT and compare results. Bad prompts: (1) "Tell me about India." (2) "Help with math." (3) "What is science?" (4) "Write something." (5) "History." (6) "Explain everything about computers." | Walk around the room. Check that students are actually using the formula. Common mistake: students will still be too vague. Gently push: "You said 'Tell me about India' -- WHAT about India? History? Food? Cricket? For what purpose?" Compare the AI responses side by side on projector. Let 3 pairs share their best prompt makeovers. Award star points for the most improved prompt. |
| 1:10-1:15 | 5 min | **Break** | Screen shows examples of funny AI responses to vague prompts. "Someone asked AI 'Tell me about Mercury' and got an essay about the planet AND the element AND Freddie Mercury." |
| 1:15-1:50 | 35 min | **Independent Work: "Prompt Challenge"** -- Students complete the Prompt Challenge worksheet: 10 scenarios where they must write the perfect prompt. Examples: "You need to find out what causes rainbows, in simple words." "You want 3 easy recipes that a kid can make." "You need help understanding fractions." Students write the prompt on paper, then test their best 3 on ChatGPT. Screenshot or write down the AI response. | The worksheet has space for the "bad" prompt attempt and the "good" prompt using the formula. Students test their top 3 prompts on ChatGPT and rate the responses: Did AI give me what I wanted? If not, what should I change? This teaches ITERATION -- if the first prompt doesn't work, improve it. Award star points for the best prompt (judged by how useful the AI response was). |
| 1:50-2:00 | 10 min | **Wrap-Up: "Prompt of the Day" Award** -- Students share their best prompt. Class votes on the best one. Winner gets "Prompt Master" badge for the day. Recap: "Always be specific, give context, say the format." Preview: "Kal hum dekhenge ki AI tumhara homework mein kaise help kar sakta hai -- CHEATING ke bina!" | Reinforce that good prompts are a SKILL -- the more you practice, the better you get. "Yeh skill tumhare poore life mein kaam aayegi -- AI ke saath aur bina AI ke bhi!" |

## SESSION 6: AI for Homework Help (Math, Science, English)

### Session Objectives
- Students learn to use ChatGPT as a tutor, not an answer machine
- Students practice getting explanations (not just answers) from AI
- Students can use AI for math, science, and English help -- ethically

### Materials Needed
- Projector, speakers, whiteboard
- Lab computers with ChatGPT (1 per 2 students)
- Sample homework problems (math, science, English) -- age-appropriate
- "AI Tutor" prompt templates handout

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|------|----------|----------|---------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "Cheating or Learning?"** -- Trainer reads 6 scenarios. Students hold up GREEN card (learning) or RED card (cheating). (1) "Copy AI's essay word for word" -- RED. (2) "Ask AI to explain a concept you don't understand" -- GREEN. (3) "Ask AI to solve your homework and submit it" -- RED. (4) "Ask AI to check your answer after you've tried" -- GREEN. (5) "Ask AI to give you practice questions" -- GREEN. (6) "Ask AI to write your project report" -- RED. | This is THE most important distinction for this age group. Repeat the mantra: "AI EXPLAINS, you LEARN. AI doesn't DO your homework -- AI HELPS you understand." Make red/green cards beforehand (simple colored paper). |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap: "3-Step Prompt Formula Quiz"** -- 5 prompts on screen. Students identify which step is missing (Specific? Context? Format?). Then fix the prompt. Quick and fast-paced. | Keep this snappy. Show prompt, give 15 seconds to identify the missing step. "Isme kya missing hai? CONTEXT! Humne AI ko nahi bataya ki hum Class 6 ke student hain!" |
| 0:15-0:40 | 25 min | **Instruction: "AI as Your Tutor"** -- Teach 3 tutor-mode prompts: **(1) Math:** "Explain how to solve this step by step: [problem]. I am in Class 6. Don't give me the answer directly, explain the method." **(2) Science:** "Explain [concept] like I'm 12 years old. Use a real-life example. Then give me a quiz question to test myself." **(3) English:** "Check this sentence for grammar mistakes and explain WHY each mistake is wrong: [sentence]." Demo each one live on ChatGPT with real problems from Class 6-7 textbooks. | Use actual problems from NCERT or Punjab State Board textbooks -- this makes it REAL. For math: "Explain how to find the area of a triangle, step by step, for a Class 6 student." For science: "Explain photosynthesis like I'm 12. Use the example of a plant in my garden." For English: "Check this sentence: 'He go to school yesterday.' Explain the mistake." Show students how AI gives great explanations. Then ask: "Ab answer tumne seekh liya -- homework KHUD karo!" |
| 0:40-1:10 | 30 min | **Guided Practice: "Homework Help Station"** -- Set up 3 stations (Math, Science, English). Students rotate through each (10 min per station). At each station, they have 2 real homework problems. They must: (1) Try to solve it themselves first (2 min). (2) Ask AI for help using the tutor prompt (5 min). (3) Write what they learned in their own words (3 min). | Have problems ready at each station. Trainer and assistant circulate. The KEY rule: students must ATTEMPT the problem before asking AI. If they go straight to AI, gently redirect: "Pehle khud try karo, phir AI se explain karwao." Watch for students who just copy AI's answer -- remind them: "AI ne explain kiya, ab APNE words mein likho." This builds the habit of AI-assisted learning, not AI-dependent copying. |
| 1:10-1:15 | 5 min | **Break** | Screen shows: "The SMARTEST students don't use AI to get answers. They use AI to UNDERSTAND the answer." |
| 1:15-1:50 | 35 min | **Independent Work: "My AI Study Session"** -- Each student picks their weakest subject. They use ChatGPT to: (1) Get 5 practice questions on a topic they find difficult. (2) Try to answer each question. (3) Use AI to check their answers and explain mistakes. (4) Write a "What I Learned Today" paragraph. Goal: complete at least 3 of the 5 questions with AI's help. | This simulates a real study session with AI. Students should feel empowered -- "Main apne sabse mushkil topic ko AI se samajh sakta hoon!" Walk around and help students craft good prompts. Common issue: students will ask AI overly broad questions. Help them narrow down: "Don't ask 'Help me with math.' Ask 'Explain how to convert fractions to decimals with 3 examples.'" Collect the "What I Learned" paragraphs -- good assessment data. |
| 1:50-2:00 | 10 min | **Wrap-Up: "Best Learner" Award + Preview** -- 3 students share what they learned using AI today. Award "Best Learner" badge (not for who got the most answers right, but who LEARNED the most). Stars of the day. Preview: "Kal hum AI ke saath KAHANI likhenge -- tum author, AI tumhara assistant!" | Emphasize the learning, not the output. "Sohan ne aaj fractions samajh liye AI ki help se -- yeh hai SMART use of AI!" Get students excited about tomorrow's storytelling session. |

## SESSION 7: Storytelling with AI (Collaborative Story)

### Session Objectives
- Students co-create a story with AI, taking turns adding to the narrative
- Students learn to guide AI's creativity with specific prompts
- Students understand that AI assists creativity but doesn't replace imagination

### Materials Needed
- Projector, speakers, whiteboard
- Lab computers with ChatGPT (1 per 2 students)
- "Story Starter" cards (20 different openings)
- A4 paper for story illustrations

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|------|----------|----------|---------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "One Word Story"** -- Class sits in a circle (or rows). Each student adds ONE word to build a story. Trainer starts: "Once..." Next student: "upon..." Next: "a..." Go around for 2-3 minutes. The resulting story will be hilarious and chaotic. | This game shows that storytelling is collaborative and fun. The chaos of the one-word story leads perfectly into: "Imagine if you had a SMART partner who could help you build a story -- that's what AI can do!" |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap: "Prompt Formula Quick Fire"** -- 5 rapid prompts to fix. Plus: "How should we ask AI for homework help? Tell me the 3 types of tutor prompts we learned." Award star points for correct answers. | Quick and energetic. This keeps the prompt skills fresh. |
| 0:15-0:40 | 25 min | **Instruction: "You + AI = Story Team"** -- Demo the collaborative storytelling process live on ChatGPT. Step 1: Give AI a story starter. "Write the beginning of a mystery story about a kid who finds a magic phone in their school bag. Make it exciting. Write only 3 paragraphs." Step 2: Read what AI wrote. Step 3: Add YOUR part -- write the next paragraph yourself. Step 4: Ask AI to continue from where you left off. Step 5: Keep going back and forth! Show students how to DIRECT the AI: "Now make the character find a clue." "Add a funny sidekick." "Make the ending surprising." | Do this live on projector. Read the story dramatically with voices and expressions. Let students shout suggestions: "Ab kya hona chahiye? Dragon aaye? Villain aaye?" Show them that THEY are the director, AI is the writer. "Tum decide karte ho kahani kahan jayegi -- AI sirf tumhari direction follow karta hai." The key lesson: AI is a tool for YOUR imagination, not a replacement for it. |
| 0:40-1:10 | 30 min | **Guided Practice: "Story Relay Race"** -- Teams of 4. Each team starts with a Story Starter card (random pick). Process: Student 1 writes the opening (their own words, 3-4 sentences). Student 2 gives it to AI and asks AI to continue (1 paragraph). Student 3 reads AI's addition and writes the next part themselves. Student 4 asks AI to write a surprising ending. Each story should be Human-AI-Human-AI. Trainer walks through the first round with one team on projector. | This is the MAIN activity. Walk around and make sure each student gets a turn. Common problem: students will want AI to write everything. Enforce the relay rule: "Human paragraphs must be YOUR words, no AI!" Give teams 5 min per round. After 20 min, teams should have a complete short story. The mix of human creativity and AI assistance creates surprisingly good stories. Keep the energy up: "Timer chal raha hai! Jaldi jaldi!" |
| 1:10-1:15 | 5 min | **Break** | Screen shows: "Did you know? AI has helped write movie scripts, song lyrics, and even jokes. But the BEST stories still come from human imagination + AI help!" |
| 1:15-1:50 | 35 min | **Independent Work: "My AI Story"** -- Each student (or pair) writes their own complete short story using the Human-AI collaborative method. Requirements: (1) At least 1 page long. (2) At least 3 human-written paragraphs and 3 AI-written paragraphs. (3) Must have a beginning, middle, and ending. (4) Student chooses the genre: mystery, adventure, comedy, sci-fi, or fantasy. (5) Bonus: draw an illustration for the story cover. Last 10 min: 3-4 volunteers read their story to the class. | Give students creative freedom. Some will write in Hindi, some in English, some in Punjabi -- all okay! The key rule: they must write AT LEAST half the story themselves. AI assists, not replaces. Walk around reading stories, give specific encouragement: "Yeh plot twist bahut accha hai, Ravi!" For reluctant writers, help them get started: "What's your favorite type of story? Adventure? Okay, let's ask AI for a cool adventure beginning, and then YOU take over." |
| 1:50-2:00 | 10 min | **Wrap-Up: "Story Star" Award + Preview** -- Vote for the best story (peer voting -- each student writes their favorite author's name on a slip). Winner gets "Story Star" badge. Stars of the day. Preview: "Kal hum creative writing ka FINAL project karenge -- poems, letters, speeches -- AI ke saath!" | Peer voting makes students feel valued. Read out a line from the winning story dramatically. "Kal ka session aur bhi creative hoga -- get ready!" |

## SESSION 8: Creative Writing Projects

### Session Objectives
- Students produce a polished creative writing piece using AI assistance
- Students learn to use AI for brainstorming, drafting, and editing
- Students present their work and give/receive feedback

### Materials Needed
- Projector, speakers, whiteboard
- Lab computers with ChatGPT + Google Docs (1 per 2 students)
- "Creative Writing Menu" handout (6 project options)
- Peer feedback forms (simple)
- Printer for final pieces

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|------|----------|----------|---------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "Emoji Story"** -- Trainer puts 5 emojis on the board (e.g., rocket, dog, rain, crown, pizza). Students have 2 minutes to write a 3-sentence story that includes ALL 5 things. Share the funniest ones. | Quick creative exercise to get writing juices flowing. Pick random emojis. The crazier the combination, the funnier the stories. |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap: "Week 2 Review Race"** -- Two teams. Trainer asks questions from Sessions 5-7. Teams alternate answering. Topics: prompt formula, tutor prompts, collaborative storytelling. Plus a live prompt test: "Write a prompt to ask AI for a poem about rain." Best prompt wins. | Make it competitive and fast. This is the mid-course check-in (Session 8 of 16). Note which students are strong and which need extra support in Weeks 3-4. |
| 0:15-0:40 | 25 min | **Instruction: "AI for the WHOLE Writing Process"** -- Teach the 4-step AI writing workflow: **(1) BRAINSTORM:** "Give me 10 ideas for a poem about friendship." **(2) DRAFT:** Write your own first draft. **(3) IMPROVE:** "Read my poem and suggest how to make it better. Don't rewrite it, just give suggestions." **(4) POLISH:** "Check this for spelling and grammar mistakes." Demo the full workflow live with a short poem. Show the "Creative Writing Menu" -- 6 project options students can choose from. | The menu options: (A) A poem (any topic, 12+ lines). (B) A letter to your future self. (C) A short speech about "Why AI is Cool." (D) A diary entry from the perspective of a robot. (E) A news article about a fictional AI event. (F) A comic strip script (4 panels). Demo with option A on projector. Show the brainstorm step (AI gives 10 ideas), pick one, write a rough draft, ask AI for feedback, polish. Emphasize: "YOUR words, AI's suggestions. You are the AUTHOR." |
| 0:40-1:10 | 30 min | **Guided Practice: "Writing Workshop" (Phase 1)** -- Students choose their project from the menu. Phase 1 (this slot): Brainstorming + First Draft. Students use AI to brainstorm ideas, then write their own first draft. Trainer circulates helping students who are stuck. | First 10 min: everyone should be brainstorming with AI. Walk around and check prompts. "Kya tum AI se sirf brainstorm karwa rahe ho ya pura likh raha hai? Yaad rakho, TUMHE likhna hai!" Next 20 min: first drafts. Some students will finish fast, others will struggle. For fast finishers: "Ab AI se feedback lo aur improve karo." For struggling students: help them choose a simpler option or pair them with a buddy. |
| 1:10-1:15 | 5 min | **Break** | Screen shows writing tips: "Good writing tip: Read your writing OUT LOUD. If it sounds weird, fix it!" |
| 1:15-1:50 | 35 min | **Independent Work: "Writing Workshop" (Phase 2 + Peer Review)** -- First 20 min: Students complete their final draft using the 4-step process (improve with AI feedback, polish spelling/grammar). Last 15 min: Peer review -- students swap papers with a partner. Each student gives 2 compliments and 1 suggestion using the feedback form. Then 5 min to make final edits. | The peer review is important for building community. Feedback form has 3 prompts: "I liked..." "The best part was..." "One suggestion is..." NO negative language allowed. Walk around during peer review to make sure feedback is kind and constructive. If time and printer available, print the final pieces. If not, save digitally. |
| 1:50-2:00 | 10 min | **Wrap-Up: "Author's Showcase" + Week 2 Awards** -- 4-5 students read their work aloud (volunteers + trainer picks). Applause after each. Announce Week 2 champion (highest star points). Award special badges: "Prompt Master," "Story Star," "Creative Genius." Preview Week 3: "Next week, hum VISUAL AI tools sikhenge -- pictures, designs, presentations -- it's going to be AMAZING!" | Make this feel special. If possible, display finished pieces on the wall. Take photos for the parent WhatsApp group. Send Week 2 summary to parents. "Aapke bacchon ne AI ke saath stories aur poems likhe -- bahut creative kaam kiya!" |
