## SESSION 9: Canva AI for School Projects

### Session Objectives
- Students navigate Canva and use its AI features
- Students create a visually appealing school project page
- Students understand design basics (colors, fonts, layout)

### Materials Needed
- Projector, speakers, whiteboard
- Lab computers with Canva accounts (pre-created, 1 per 2 students)
- "Design Basics" cheat sheet handout
- Sample school project topics list

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|------|----------|----------|---------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "Ugly vs Pretty" Design Game** -- Show 5 pairs of designs (good vs bad). Students vote on which is better. Discuss WHY the good one looks better (colors, spacing, fonts). | Use real examples: a cluttered poster vs a clean one, a neon-colored slide vs a pleasant one. This builds design awareness before touching tools. "Dekho, yeh kyu accha lagta hai? Space hai, colors match karte hain, font readable hai." |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap: Week 2 Highlights** -- Quick quiz: "What's the 3-step prompt formula? How do we use AI for homework help (ethically)? What's the 4-step writing process?" Award star points. Show 2-3 best creative writing pieces from last week on screen. | Celebrate last week's work. Reading student pieces aloud builds confidence. Keep quiz to 5 questions max. |
| 0:15-0:40 | 25 min | **Instruction: "Canva 101 + AI Magic"** -- Live demo on projector: (1) Log into Canva. (2) Choose a template (school project/poster). (3) Edit text, change fonts, colors. (4) Use Canva's AI features: Magic Write (generate text), Magic Design (auto-layout), Text to Image. (5) Add images from Canva's library. (6) Download as PDF/image. Teach 3 design rules: (A) Maximum 3 colors. (B) Maximum 2 fonts. (C) Leave white space -- don't fill every corner. | Go SLOWLY. These students may not have used Canva before. Click every button deliberately, wait for students to follow. "Pehle main karunga, phir tum karoge." Show the AI features one at a time. Magic Write: "Write a short paragraph about the solar system for a school project." Students will be amazed. Magic Design: show how it auto-arranges content beautifully. Emphasize the 3 design rules repeatedly. |
| 0:40-1:10 | 30 min | **Guided Practice: "My Canva Project Page"** -- Each pair creates a ONE-PAGE school project on a topic of their choice (or from the provided list: Solar System, Water Cycle, Indian Freedom Fighters, Healthy Food, My State). Step-by-step, trainer guides: Step 1: Pick template (5 min). Step 2: Write title and heading (3 min). Step 3: Use Magic Write for content paragraph (5 min). Step 4: Add images (5 min). Step 5: Adjust colors and fonts using the 3 rules (5 min). Step 6: Final touches (7 min). | Walk through each step on projector, then give students time. "Main Step 2 dikhata hoon... ab tum karo. 3 minutes." Keep the timer visible. Pairs should collaborate -- one types, other suggests. Switch roles at Step 4. Check that students are following the 3 design rules. Common mistake: too many colors, too much text. "White space is your friend! Sab jagah mat bharo!" |
| 1:10-1:15 | 5 min | **Break** | Screen shows a gallery of beautiful Canva designs for inspiration. |
| 1:15-1:50 | 35 min | **Independent Work: "Design Challenge"** -- Each pair completes their Canva project page AND creates a second design: a "Fun Fact Card" about their topic (think Instagram-style square post with 1 big fact + 1 image). Challenge: make it so good that a teacher would want to display it. Last 10 min: Gallery walk -- all designs displayed on screens, students walk around and vote for the best with sticky notes. | The Fun Fact Card is a quick second project that reinforces skills. It should be visually punchy -- big text, bold colors, one striking image. Walk around giving design feedback: "Font thoda bada karo," "Yeh color change karo, read nahi ho raha." Gallery walk creates excitement and peer learning. Vote with sticky notes or just "stand behind the computer with the best design." |
| 1:50-2:00 | 10 min | **Wrap-Up: "Design Star" Award + Preview** -- Announce the most-voted design. Award "Design Star" badge. Show 3 best projects on projector. Stars of the day. Preview: "Kal hum AI se ART banayenge -- paintings, drawings, whatever you imagine! AI draw karega, tum direct karoge!" | Save all student Canva projects to a shared folder for the Showcase Day portfolio. Send screenshots to parent WhatsApp group. |

## SESSION 10: AI Art Creation (Bing Image Creator)

### Session Objectives
- Students generate AI art using text prompts
- Students learn to write descriptive art prompts (style, subject, mood, colors)
- Students curate and select the best AI-generated images

### Materials Needed
- Projector, speakers, whiteboard
- Lab computers with Bing Image Creator access (bing.com/create)
- "Art Prompt Formula" handout
- Voting slips for AI Art Gallery

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|------|----------|----------|---------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "Describe This Picture"** -- Show a complex image on screen. Students write a description in 30 seconds. Read 3-4 descriptions aloud. Point out which descriptions are more detailed. "Jitna detail tum describe karte ho, utna better AI art banayega!" | Use a colorful, detailed image (e.g., a fantasy landscape). Compare a vague description ("a mountain") with a detailed one ("a snow-covered mountain at sunset with a river flowing through a green valley"). This directly connects to art prompting. |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap: "Canva Quick Quiz"** -- 5 questions about yesterday: "What are the 3 design rules? What does Magic Write do? How do you add images in Canva?" Plus show 2 more student projects from yesterday. | Quick and fun. Award star points. Showing more projects builds confidence for students who didn't get featured yesterday. |
| 0:15-0:40 | 25 min | **Instruction: "The Art Prompt Formula"** -- Teach the formula: **SUBJECT + STYLE + DETAILS + MOOD**. (1) SUBJECT: What's in the picture? (a cat, a castle, a superhero). (2) STYLE: What art style? (cartoon, realistic, watercolor, pixel art, anime). (3) DETAILS: Colors, setting, time of day, clothing, background. (4) MOOD: Happy, mysterious, epic, peaceful, funny. Demo live on Bing Image Creator with 4 prompts, each getting more detailed. Show how more detail = better results. Also show: what NOT to include (no real people's names, no violent content, nothing inappropriate). | Demo prompts: (1) "A cat" -- boring, generic result. (2) "A cat wearing a crown" -- better. (3) "A fluffy orange cat wearing a golden crown, sitting on a throne, cartoon style" -- much better! (4) "A fluffy orange cat wearing a golden crown, sitting on a velvet throne in a medieval castle, warm candlelight, oil painting style, majestic mood" -- WOW! Show all 4 results side by side. Students will see the dramatic improvement. "Dekha? Detail matters!" Bing Image Creator generates 4 images per prompt -- show how to pick the best one. |
| 0:40-1:10 | 30 min | **Guided Practice: "My First AI Art Collection"** -- Each student creates 5 AI artworks using Bing Image Creator. Trainer assigns 5 themes, one at a time, giving 6 min per artwork: (1) Your dream pet. (2) Your dream house. (3) A scene from your favorite story. (4) An imaginary planet. (5) A superhero version of yourself (no real photos -- describe features). Students must use the full Art Prompt Formula for each. | Walk around checking prompts BEFORE students generate. "Tumhara prompt kya hai? Hmm, 'a dog' -- isme kya missing hai? Style! Details! Mood!" Help students add detail. If Bing Image Creator is slow or has queue, students can write their next prompt while waiting. Save all generated images to a folder. Some students will want to generate 20 images -- limit to 5 quality ones, not 20 random ones. "Quality over quantity! 5 amazing artworks, not 20 okay ones." |
| 1:10-1:15 | 5 min | **Break** | Screen shows a slideshow of amazing AI art from around the world. |
| 1:15-1:50 | 35 min | **Independent Work: "AI Art Gallery"** -- First 15 min: Students select their best 3 artworks out of the 5 and write a title + short description for each (like a museum label). They also write the prompt they used. Next 10 min: Create a "gallery display" -- arrange the 3 artworks in a Canva page or Google Doc with titles and descriptions. Last 10 min: AI Art Gallery Walk -- all screens display their galleries. Each student gets 3 voting slips to place on their favorite artworks (can't vote for own). | The gallery concept makes this feel like a real art exhibition. Encourage creative titles: not "A Cat" but "The Royal Feline of Kingdom Meow." Walk around during the gallery walk. Count votes, announce winners in different categories: "Most Creative," "Most Beautiful," "Funniest," "Most Detailed Prompt." This validates different styles of creativity. |
| 1:50-2:00 | 10 min | **Wrap-Up: "AI Artist" Awards + Preview** -- Announce gallery winners. Award "AI Artist" badges. Key lesson: "AI creates the art, but YOUR imagination and YOUR words guide it. The better your description, the better the art. Tum artist ho, AI tumhara brush hai!" Preview: "Kal hum presentations banayenge -- AI tumhare liye poori presentation design kar dega!" | Save all artwork for Showcase Day. These visual outputs are great for parent marketing too. |

## SESSION 11: Making Presentations with Gamma

### Session Objectives
- Students create a complete presentation using Gamma AI
- Students learn presentation structure (title, sections, conclusion)
- Students practice basic presenting skills

### Materials Needed
- Projector, speakers, whiteboard
- Lab computers with Gamma accounts (gamma.app -- 1 per 2 students)
- Presentation topic list
- "Good Presentation" checklist handout

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|------|----------|----------|---------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "Presentation Fails"** -- Show 3 examples of terrible presentations (too much text, bad colors, boring slides). Students identify what's wrong. Then show 1 great presentation. | Use funny "death by PowerPoint" examples. Students laugh and learn what NOT to do. "Agar tumhari presentation aise dikhti hai, toh log SO jayenge!" |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap: "Art Prompt Challenge"** -- Quick: write the best art prompt you can in 1 minute. Read 3 aloud, class votes on the most descriptive. Then: "What was the Art Prompt Formula?" Award stars. | Keeps the art skills fresh. Quick and competitive. |
| 0:15-0:40 | 25 min | **Instruction: "Gamma -- AI Makes Presentations"** -- Live demo: (1) Go to gamma.app. (2) Click "Create New." (3) Type a topic: "The Solar System -- A Presentation for Class 6." (4) Watch Gamma generate a FULL presentation in seconds. (5) Show how to edit slides, change themes, add images, modify text. (6) Teach presentation structure: Title Slide -> Introduction -> 3-4 Content Slides -> Fun Facts -> Conclusion. Also teach the "Good Presentation" rules: (A) Max 6 lines per slide. (B) Use images, not just text. (C) Keep fonts big and readable. | This is a MIND-BLOWING moment. When Gamma generates a full presentation from one sentence, students' jaws will drop. "Dekha? AI ne 2 second mein 8 slides bana di! But wait -- ab HUM isko better banayenge." Show them that the AI-generated presentation is a STARTING POINT, not the final product. Edit 2-3 slides live: change a heading, replace an image, add a personal touch. "AI ne base diya, tum usko APNA banao." |
| 0:40-1:10 | 30 min | **Guided Practice: "My First AI Presentation"** -- Each pair chooses a topic (from the list or their own -- must be school-appropriate). Step-by-step with trainer: Step 1: Type topic into Gamma (5 min). Step 2: Review AI-generated slides (5 min). Step 3: Edit at least 3 slides -- change text in your own words, add/change images (10 min). Step 4: Add one completely NEW slide with your own content (5 min). Step 5: Choose a theme/color scheme (5 min). | Topics list: My Favorite Scientist, Healthy Eating, Save Water, India's Space Program, Amazing Animals, My Dream Career, Climate Change for Kids, The History of Cricket. Walk through each step on projector. Critical rule: "AI ne jo likha hai, tum edit KARO. Apne words mein likho. Kam se kam 3 slides change karo aur 1 nayi slide add karo." Check that students are actually editing, not just keeping AI's default. |
| 1:10-1:15 | 5 min | **Break** | Screen shows: "Tip: The best presenters don't READ slides. They TALK to the audience and slides are just support!" |
| 1:15-1:50 | 35 min | **Independent Work: "Presentation Polish + Practice"** -- First 20 min: Students finalize their presentations. Add finishing touches: consistent colors, images on every slide, clear headings, conclusion slide. Next 15 min: Practice presenting! Each pair practices presenting to another pair (4 min each). Use the checklist: Eye contact? Speaking clearly? Not reading slides? Smiling? The listening pair gives 1 compliment and 1 tip. | Presentation practice is crucial. Many students have never presented before. Walk around coaching: "Screen ki taraf mat dekho, logon ki taraf dekho." "Thoda loud bolo, peeche walo ko bhi sunna hai." "Smile karo, nervous mat ho!" The peer practice format means everyone presents, not just volunteers. If there's a pair that finishes early, let them present to the whole class as a demo. |
| 1:50-2:00 | 10 min | **Wrap-Up: "Presentation Pro" Award + Preview** -- 2 best pairs do a quick 2-minute presentation to the whole class. Class claps. Award "Presentation Pro" badges. Stars of the day. Preview: "Kal hum POSTER design karenge -- Canva, AI art, sab use karke ek amazing poster!" | The mini-presentations build confidence for the final Showcase Day. Save all Gamma presentations for portfolio. |

## SESSION 12: Designing Posters

### Session Objectives
- Students design a complete poster combining all visual AI skills learned
- Students apply design principles (layout, color, typography, imagery)
- Students create a poster for a real cause or event

### Materials Needed
- Projector, speakers, whiteboard
- Lab computers with Canva + Bing Image Creator
- "Poster Design Brief" handout (6 cause options)
- Poster evaluation rubric (peer + trainer)
- Printer (optional -- for printing best posters)

### Minute-by-Minute Plan

| Time | Duration | Activity | Trainer Notes |
|------|----------|----------|---------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | 5 min | **Warm-Up: "Spot the Design Rules"** -- Show 4 posters on screen. Students identify which follow the 3 design rules and which break them. Quick call-and-response. | Revision of design rules through visual analysis. Use real-world posters (school event, movie, product ad). |
| 0:05-0:15 | 10 min | **Recap: "Week 3 Skills Bingo"** -- Each student has a 3x3 bingo card with skills (Canva template, Magic Write, Art Prompt Formula, Gamma AI, 3 Design Rules, etc.). Trainer calls out descriptions, students mark if they have the skill. First BINGO wins! | Fun assessment tool. Print bingo cards in advance. This reviews ALL Week 3 content so far. |
| 0:15-0:40 | 25 min | **Instruction: "Poster Design Masterclass"** -- Teach poster-specific design: (1) Hierarchy: What should people see FIRST? (big headline). SECOND? (image). THIRD? (details). (2) Call to Action: Every poster needs a clear "do this" message. (3) Combining tools: Use Bing Image Creator for a custom image, then import into Canva for layout + text. Demo live: create a "Save Water" poster. Step 1: Generate a striking water-related image with Bing. Step 2: Open Canva, choose poster template. Step 3: Upload the AI image. Step 4: Add headline, subtext, call to action. Step 5: Apply design rules. | Show the "Poster Design Brief" options: (A) Save Water. (B) Plant Trees. (C) Say No to Bullying. (D) Road Safety. (E) Healthy Eating. (F) Keep Our School Clean. Or students can pick their own cause with trainer approval. The demo should take about 15 min -- show the FULL process from AI image to finished Canva poster. "Aaj hum sab kuch combine karenge -- AI art + Canva design + good prompts = AMAZING poster!" |
| 0:40-1:10 | 30 min | **Guided Practice: "Poster Creation" (Phase 1)** -- Students choose their cause/topic and begin creating. Trainer guides the process: First 10 min: Generate 2-3 AI images using Bing Image Creator with detailed prompts. Select the best one. Next 10 min: Open Canva, choose a poster template, upload the AI image. Next 10 min: Add headline, body text (short!), and call to action. | This is the main production time. Walk around actively. Common issues: (1) Students spend too long on AI image generation -- set a timer. (2) Too much text on poster -- "Poster mein zyada mat likho! Headline + 2 lines + call to action. BAS." (3) Ignoring design rules -- "Kitne colors hain? 3 se zyada? Change karo." Help students who are stuck choose a template. For advanced students, suggest adding multiple AI images or creating a front and back. |
| 1:10-1:15 | 5 min | **Break** | Screen shows a slideshow of award-winning poster designs for inspiration. |
| 1:15-1:50 | 35 min | **Independent Work: "Poster Creation" (Phase 2) + Gallery** -- First 20 min: Finalize posters. Apply finishing touches: check spelling, ensure readability from a distance (trainer stands at back of room and checks), consistent colors, clear hierarchy. Last 15 min: Poster Gallery + Peer Evaluation. All posters displayed. Each student gets an evaluation form and rates 3 classmates' posters on: Visual Appeal (5 pts), Message Clarity (5 pts), Creativity (5 pts). | The "readability test" is important: if the headline can't be read from across the room, it's too small. Walk around doing this test. For the gallery evaluation, students should NOT rate their own poster. Collect evaluation forms -- use scores for assessment. Print the top 3 posters if printer is available. These become classroom decorations AND Showcase Day displays. |
| 1:50-2:00 | 10 min | **Wrap-Up: Week 3 Awards + Preview** -- Announce poster winners (by peer evaluation score). Award Week 3 champion. Special badges: "Design Star," "AI Artist," "Presentation Pro." Preview Week 4: "Next week, hum REAL PROBLEMS solve karenge AI se. Science projects, quiz games, aur SHOWCASE DAY jahan tum apne parents ko dikhaaoge kya seekha!" | Big energy for Week 4 preview. Mention Showcase Day multiple times -- "Parents aa rahe hain! Best projects dikhane hain!" Send Week 3 summary + poster photos to parent WhatsApp. |
