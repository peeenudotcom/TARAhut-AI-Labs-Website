Section 01

Why Contracts Are Non-Negotiable

Horror story: a freelancer did ₹25,000 worth of work. Client said "mujhe pasand nahi aaya" and refused to pay. Why? No contract.

🚨

The ₹25,000 Nightmare

A freelancer created 30 social media posts, 2 videos, and a complete GMB profile for a restaurant. Took 2 weeks of work. Client ghosted after delivery. No contract = no protection = no payment. This will NEVER happen to you because you will always use a contract.

What a Contract Protects

YOU🛡️

Payment Security

50% advance before starting work. Remaining 50% on delivery. Written in the contract = enforceable. No more "sochke batata hoon" after work is done.

SCOPE📏

Scope Clarity

Exactly what is included and what is NOT included. "20 posts per month" prevents the client from saying "ek aur post bana do na" endlessly. Boundaries matter.

CLIENT🤝

Expectation Setting

Client knows exactly what they get, when they get it, and what they need to provide. No surprises on either side. Professional relationships need clear expectations.

REVISIONS🔄

Revision Policy

2 rounds of revisions included. Additional revisions at ₹500/round. This prevents infinite revision loops that eat your time and profit.

"Simple aur clear rakhna hai, waqeel wali language nahi chahiye." Your contract should be readable by a shop owner in Ludhiana, not a lawyer in Delhi. Hindi/English mix is fine. Clarity beats formality.
Contracts protect both sides. They are a sign of professionalism, not distrust.

Section 02

Simple Service Contract Template

7 essential clauses. No legalese. Clear and professional.

CLAUSE 1👥

Parties

Service Provider: [Your Name]. Client: [Business Name, Owner Name]. Clear identification of who is involved in this agreement.

CLAUSE 2📋

Services Described

Exact services: "Social media management including 20 posts/month, GMB updates, WhatsApp broadcast setup." Be specific. Vague = trouble.

CLAUSE 3📦

Deliverables

What the client receives: number of posts, designs, videos, reports. "20 Instagram posts, 4 GMB posts, 2 videos, 1 monthly report."

CLAUSE 4📅

Timeline

Start date, delivery schedule, monthly cycle dates. "Work begins within 48 hours of advance payment. Monthly cycle: 1st to 30th."

CLAUSE 5💰

Payment Terms

Amount, payment schedule, payment method. "₹10,000/month. 50% advance, 50% on 15th. Payment via UPI/bank transfer."

CLAUSE 6🔄

Revision Policy

"2 rounds of revisions included per deliverable. Additional revisions: ₹500 per round. Revision requests within 48 hours of delivery."

CLAUSE 7🚪

Cancellation

"Either party may cancel with 15 days notice. Work completed until cancellation date will be paid in full. Advance is non-refundable after work begins."

Section 05

Role-Play: Full Client Journey

Pair up. One is the client (salon owner). One is the AI service provider. Run the full journey.

The Role-Play Script

STEP 1📋

Present Service Menu

Show the 3-tier service menu. Explain what each tier includes. Let the "client" ask questions. Be confident but not pushy.

STEP 2❓

Handle Questions

"Client" asks questions and raises concerns. "Kitna time lagega?" "Guarantee kya hai?" "Pehle bhi kisi ne try kiya, kaam nahi aaya." Practice responding naturally.

STEP 3🎯

Recommend a Package

Based on the "client's" needs, recommend the Growth Package with clear reasoning. Use the per-day breakdown. Show sample deliverables.

STEP 4✅

"Client" Says Yes

Practice the moment of agreement. "Bahut achha, chaliye shuru karte hain." Stay professional, not overly excited.

STEP 5📜

Walk Through Contract

Show the contract. Walk through each clause. "Yeh hai aapka agreement. Ismein yeh yeh included hai..." Answer contract questions.

STEP 6📝

Send Onboarding Form

Explain the onboarding form. "Yeh form bharna hoga taaki hum kaam shuru kar sakein. Logo, photos, aur business details chahiye."

🎭

Role-Play Tips

For the "client": Act like a real business owner — skeptical, busy, budget-conscious. Ask tough questions. Do not make it easy.
For the "provider": Stay calm, professional, and helpful. Use specific numbers and examples. Show your portfolio samples.
