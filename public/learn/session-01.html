The Simplest Definition Ever

AI is a computer system trained to do tasks that normally require human intelligence — like understanding language, recognising images, making decisions, and generating content. Think of it as a tireless assistant that has read millions of books and can respond instantly.

Misconception vs Reality

🤖

What People Think

AI is a conscious robot that thinks, feels, and will replace all human jobs. Terminator is coming.

✕ Myth
🧩

The Reality

AI is a pattern-finding tool. Incredibly good at specific tasks, but doesn't "think." It assists humans — not replaces them.

✓ Fact

The 3 Types of AI — Only One Exists Today

EXISTS TODAY🎯

Narrow AI (ANI)

AI designed to do one specific thing exceptionally well. ChatGPT writes text. Google Maps finds routes. Spotify curates playlists. Every single AI tool you'll learn in this course is Narrow AI.

IN RESEARCH🧠

General AI (AGI)

AI that can learn and reason across all tasks, just like a human. It doesn't exist yet. OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic are all racing to build it. Scientists estimate it could arrive in 5-15 years.

SCI-FI (FOR NOW)🌌

Super AI (ASI)

AI smarter than all of humanity combined. This is pure science fiction for now — think Terminator or Avengers. We are nowhere close. Don't let movies scare you.

How Does AI in Your Phone Actually Work?

📸

On-Device AI

Your phone has a dedicated AI chip (Apple Neural Engine, Snapdragon's NPU). It runs simple AI locally — portrait mode, face unlock, autocorrect — without needing internet.

☁️

Cloud AI

Complex AI (Google Search, ChatGPT, Gemini) runs on massive servers. Your phone sends a request, the server processes it using powerful GPUs, and sends back the result in milliseconds.

🔄

Learning from You

Apps get smarter about your preferences over time. YouTube refines its understanding of you every video. Spotify gets better at knowing your mood. You are training AI just by using it.
