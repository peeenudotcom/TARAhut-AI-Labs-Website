A logo is the foundation of everything — it goes on the website, social media, and presentations. But a logo alone won't drive inquiries. Priority order: Logo → Website → Social Media → Presentation. Build the logo in 15 minutes with Ideogram, then move immediately to the website which will do the heavy lifting.

✅ Website is the Revenue Driver — But Needs a Logo First

You're right — the website is where inquiries will come from. But build the logo first (15 min) so you have branding for the website. Priority order: Logo → Website → Social Media → Presentation. The website with a proper domain and inquiry form will be the most impactful single deliverable.

⚠️ Social Media is Visible — But Website is Permanent

Social media drives quick visibility, but a website builds long-term credibility. Parents searching for coaching centers want a website to trust. Priority order: Logo → Website → Social Media → Presentation. Build the social posts AFTER the website so you can link to it.

Execution Framework

Tool by tool, prompt by prompt — exactly how to build each deliverable for Punjab Scholars Academy.

Deliverable 1 🎨

Logo — Ideogram AI

Prompt: "Professional logo for 'Punjab Scholars Academy', a coaching institute in Faridkot. Text-based logo with a clean book or mortar board icon. Colors: deep blue and gold. Flat design, modern, trustworthy."

Generate 4 variations. Pick the strongest. Download PNG.

Deliverable 2 🌐

Website — Bolt.new

Prompt: "Build a coaching center website for Punjab Scholars Academy in Faridkot, Punjab. Include: hero with tagline 'Top Results. Real Teachers. Faridkot's Trusted Academy', programs (JEE/NEET/9-12), batch details, why choose us, faculty, contact with WhatsApp button."

Deliverable 3 📱

Social Posts — Canva AI

Use Canva AI templates. Create 5 posts: (1) Welcome launch post, (2) "Why choose us" stats, (3) Batch announcement, (4) Success story/result, (5) Admission open call-to-action. Use the logo colors throughout.

Deliverable 4 📊

Presentation — Gamma

Prompt to Gamma: "Create a 10-slide presentation for Punjab Scholars Academy for parent meetings. Include: about us, programs, faculty, results/success, fee structure, contact. Professional academic style, blue and gold theme."

Bonus Deliverable 🎬

Intro Video — HeyGen

Use HeyGen's AI avatar. Script (write with Claude): "Punjab Scholars Academy — where students from Faridkot crack JEE, NEET, and board exams. Expert teachers. Small batches. Proven results. Admissions open. Call now." 30-60 seconds. Share on WhatsApp status.

All Copy ✍️

All Text Copy — Claude

Use Claude to write all text: website headline, about us paragraph, program descriptions, social post captions, WhatsApp status messages, and the parent meeting pitch. Give Claude the brief and ask for all text in one go.

Key Principle: Consistency

All deliverables must feel like they come from the same brand. Same colors (blue and gold). Same tagline across website, social, and video. Same logo on everything. This is what separates professional work from amateur work. When a parent sees the Instagram post and then visits the website, they should feel "yes, this is the same trustworthy place."

Section 04

Quality Standards

Good vs great. What a client is impressed by vs what makes them hesitate. Know the difference before you submit your work.

Logo — Quality Rubric

✅ Excellent

  • Clean, readable at small sizes
  • Colors reflect trust/academics (blue, gold, green)
  • Academy name clearly legible
  • Works on both light and dark backgrounds
  • Feels professional, not clipart-like

❌ Needs Improvement

  • Too cluttered or hard to read
  • Looks generic or like a template
  • Wrong colors — neon, childish, or clashing
  • Low resolution or pixelated
  • Doesn't match the academic tone

Website — Quality Rubric

✅ Excellent

  • Clear headline that explains who they are
  • Visible phone/WhatsApp CTA above the fold
  • Lists programs with clear info (classes, timing)
  • Loads fast on mobile
  • Contact form or WhatsApp button works
  • Consistent branding with logo colors

❌ Needs Improvement

  • Hero doesn't explain what the business does
  • No clear call-to-action (how to contact?)
  • Broken links or missing sections
  • Doesn't work on mobile
  • Generic placeholder text left in
  • Looks different from the logo/social colors

Social Media Posts — Quality Rubric

✅ Excellent

  • Logo/brand name visible on each post
  • Each post has one clear message
  • Caption has a call-to-action
  • Visual hierarchy — headline is the biggest text
  • Consistent color theme across all 5 posts

❌ Needs Improvement

  • Too much text — post is unreadable
  • No branding or contact info
  • Each post looks completely different
  • Caption is generic ("Check us out!")
  • No mention of location or how to reach them
🎯

The Single Most Important Quality Check

Before you submit ANY deliverable, ask yourself: "If I were a parent in Faridkot looking for a coaching center for my child, would this make me want to call them?" If yes — submit it. If you're not sure — refine it first.

Knowing what great looks like is the first step to producing it consistently.
