Myth Buster🤖

AI Is NOT a Robot

AI is not a sentient being. It is software that recognizes patterns in data and makes predictions. When ChatGPT writes a poem, it is predicting the most likely next word — not "thinking" creatively. "AI sochda nahi — pattern match karda hai."

Core Concept🧠

Pattern Recognition Machine

AI learns from billions of examples. Show it 10 million cat photos and it learns what "cat" looks like. Feed it trillions of words and it learns language patterns. More data = better patterns = better predictions. "Jitna zyada data, utni zyada samajh."

Important⚠️

What AI Cannot Do

AI cannot think, feel, understand, or be creative the way humans do. It has no consciousness, no emotions, no common sense. It can produce amazing outputs but it is fundamentally a very sophisticated pattern-matching machine. "Ih samajhna zaroori hai."

🔴

Common Misconception

"AI will replace all jobs." "AI knows everything." "AI is dangerous and will take over." "AI understands what I say." These are Hollywood myths, not reality.

✕ Myths
🟢

The Reality

"AI is a powerful tool that amplifies human capability." "AI makes mistakes." "AI needs human guidance." "AI recognizes patterns, not meaning." These are the facts.

✓ Facts

The 3 Types of AI You Should Know

Type 1🔎

Narrow AI (What We Have)

AI that is good at ONE specific task. ChatGPT = language. Midjourney = images. Siri = voice commands. All current AI is narrow AI. It cannot do tasks outside its training. "Aaj kal di saari AI ik kaam vich expert hai."

Type 2🧠

General AI (Coming Soon)

AI that can do ANY intellectual task a human can. We are not there yet, but models like GPT-4o and Claude 3.5 are getting closer. Experts estimate 5-15 years away. "Ih AI insaan wangu sab kuch kar sakegi."

Type 3🚀

Super AI (Theoretical)

AI smarter than all humans combined. This is purely theoretical — no one knows if it is even possible. Do not worry about this. Focus on mastering the AI tools available TODAY. "Ih abhi bahut door hai."

"Main technical nahi haan, kya main AI seekh sakda/sakdi haan?" — Absolutely YES. You do not need coding, math, or a tech degree. If you can type and think clearly, you can master AI. This course proves it. 90 days, zero tech background required.

The CRISP Framework — Your First 20 Prompts

CRISP फ्रेमवर्क — आपके पहले 20 प्रॉम्प्ट

CRISP ਫ੍ਰੇਮਵਰਕ — ਤੁਹਾਡੇ ਪਹਿਲੇ 20 ਪ੍ਰੋਮਪਟ

The difference between a bad prompt and a great prompt is structure. CRISP gives you that structure. "CRISP naal tuhade prompts 10x behtar ho jaange."

🌎
C — Context
Background info. Who are you? What is the situation?
🎭
R — Role
Who should AI be? Expert, teacher, advisor?
📝
I — Instruction
What exactly should AI do? Be specific.
📚
S — Specifics
Details: format, length, tone, audience.
⚙️
P — Parameters
Constraints: word count, style, do/don'ts.
👎

Bad Prompt (No CRISP)

"Write me a social media post."

Result: Generic, bland, unusable. AI does not know your business, audience, platform, or tone.

✕ Vague
👍

Great Prompt (CRISP)

"C: I own a sweet shop in Jalandhar. R: Act as a social media expert. I: Write an Instagram caption for Diwali mithai sale. S: Casual Punjabi-English mix, include emojis, mention 30% off. P: Under 150 words, include 5 hashtags."

✓ Specific
💡

The CRISP Golden Rule

The more specific your prompt, the more useful the output. Think of it like ordering food: "Give me food" vs "Give me 2 butter naan, 1 dal makhani, extra butter, medium spice, packed for takeaway." Which order gets you what you actually want? "Jitna specific, utna better result."

Your First 20 Prompts — CRISP in Action

Prompt 1-4💼

Business Prompts

1. "Act as a business advisor. I run a [business] in [city]. Give me 5 ways to increase sales this month using AI tools."
2. "Write a professional WhatsApp message to follow up with a customer who visited my store but didn't buy."
3. "Create a one-week social media plan for a [business type] in Punjab."
4. "Analyze my business idea: [describe idea]. Give honest pros, cons, and suggestions."

Prompt 5-8📝

Writing Prompts

5. "Write a blog post about [topic] for beginners. Use simple language, bullet points, and examples."
6. "Rewrite this text to sound more professional: [paste text]"
7. "Write 5 catchy Instagram captions for a [product/service] business."
8. "Create an email template for [purpose]. Keep it under 100 words."

Prompt 9-12📚

Learning Prompts

9. "Explain [complex topic] like I am a 12-year-old. Use simple examples."
10. "Create a study plan for learning [subject] in 30 days. Include daily tasks."
11. "Quiz me on [topic] with 10 multiple choice questions. Explain wrong answers."
12. "Summarize this article in 5 bullet points: [paste article]"

Prompt 13-16💰

Money-Making Prompts

13. "I have [skill] and [amount] to invest. Give me 5 business ideas in [city]."
14. "Write a freelance proposal for [service] on Fiverr. Make it stand out."
15. "Create a pricing strategy for my [product/service]. Include 3 tiers."
16. "Write a cold outreach message for LinkedIn to get [type of client]."

Prompt 17-20🎯

Creative & Personal Prompts

17. "Plan a 7-day trip to [destination] for [budget]. Include food, stay, activities."
18. "Write a motivational speech about [topic] in Punjabi-English mix."
19. "Create a weekly meal plan for a vegetarian family of 4 in Punjab. Budget: Rs 3000/week."
20. "Help me write my resume for [job role]. I have [X years] experience in [field]."
