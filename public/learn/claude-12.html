Section 1

Claude in Your Workflow

Most people use Claude reactively — when they think of it. Power users build it into their daily routine so it saves time automatically.

Reactive vs Proactive Claude Use Reactive: "Oh, I need to write an email — let me open Claude." Proactive: "Every Monday at 9am, I run my Weekly Briefing Prompt. Every Friday, I run my Client Report Prompt. Every time I get a tricky enquiry, I use my pre-built response template." The second approach saves 5-10 hours per week.

The 3 Levels of Claude Integration

🔰

Level 1: On-Demand

Open Claude when you remember it. Use it for specific tasks. Most people are here.

⚙️

Level 2: Habitual

Pre-built prompts for recurring tasks. Morning briefing. Weekly reports. Response templates. Saves 3-5 hours/week.

🚀

Level 3: Systematic

Claude Projects with full context. Custom instructions. Zapier/Make integrations. Claude as infrastructure, not a tool.

Building the Claude Habit

1

Identify Your Repetitive Tasks

List 5 tasks you do every week that involve writing, analysis, or information processing. These are your automation candidates.

2

Build a Saved Prompt for Each

Write a CRISP prompt for each task. Save it in your notes app. Run it every time instead of starting from scratch.

3

Create a Daily Claude Ritual

Block 15 minutes in the morning for your Claude workflow — morning brief, priority tasks, content plan for the day.

4

Set Up a Claude Project

Upload your business context once. Every conversation starts with Claude already knowing who you are and what you do.

5

Review and Improve Weekly

Every Friday, ask: which Claude interactions were most valuable this week? Improve those prompts for next week.

The 15-Minute Morning Rule The highest-ROI Claude habit: spend 15 minutes with Claude every morning before checking emails. Run your briefing prompt, plan your day, draft any urgent responses. This single habit changes your entire day.
Section 2

Automation Ideas

Four ready-to-use prompt templates for your recurring business tasks.

1. Morning Briefing Prompt

Run Every Morning | 5 minutes
Daily Priority Briefing
Clears your mind, sets your priorities, identifies your most important task.
Morning Briefing Prompt Template
I am: [Your role] at [Your business]. Today is [Day, Date].

My tasks for today:
- [Task 1]
- [Task 2]
- [Task 3]

My current challenges: [Any blockers or decisions pending]

Give me:
1. My top 3 priorities for today (ranked by impact)
2. The ONE thing I must complete no matter what
3. Any task I should delegate or defer
4. A motivating thought for the day (1 line)

Be direct. No fluff. This is my morning briefing.

2. Weekly Report Prompt

Run Every Friday | 20 minutes
Weekly Business Review
Synthesizes your week's work into a structured report for stakeholders or personal records.
Weekly Report Prompt Template
Create a professional weekly report for Week of [Date Range].

Accomplishments this week:
[Paste your bullet points of what you did]

Metrics/Numbers:
- Enquiries received: [X]
- Students enrolled: [X]
- Revenue: [X]
- Content pieces published: [X]

Challenges faced: [List them]
Next week's focus: [Your planned priorities]

Format as: Executive Summary (3 lines) → This Week's Wins → Metrics → Challenges + Resolutions → Next Week Plan. Professional tone. Suitable to share with a team or mentor.

3. Client Response Template

Use For Every Complex Enquiry
Client Communication Generator
Turn raw notes about a client situation into professional, warm, persuasive responses.
Client Response Prompt Template
Situation: [Briefly describe what the client asked or what happened]

Key points I need to communicate:
- [Point 1]
- [Point 2]
- [Point 3]

Tone required: [Professional/warm/empathetic/firm/reassuring]
Channel: [WhatsApp message / Email / In-person script]
Length: [Short 3-4 lines / Medium paragraph / Detailed]

Write the response. Include: greeting, the key message, any next steps, and a warm close. Sound human, not corporate.

4. Meeting Prep Prompt

Run 30 Minutes Before Any Meeting
Meeting Preparation Brief
Enter any meeting fully prepared — questions, talking points, and anticipated objections ready.
Meeting Prep Prompt Template
I have a meeting in 30 minutes. Help me prepare.

Meeting type: [Sales call / Partnership discussion / Student intake / Team review]
With: [Who — their background/context if known]
My goal from this meeting: [What outcome do I want?]
What I know about them: [Any context, previous interactions, their needs]

Give me:
1. 5 smart questions I should ask
2. My key talking points (3-4 bullet points)
3. Likely objections they might raise + my responses
4. The one thing I must NOT forget to say
5. A strong meeting opener (2-3 sentences)
Section 3

Integration Concepts

Claude + Zapier, Claude + Make, Claude + Google Workspace. How automation platforms connect Claude to your existing tools.

What Is Integration? Integrations connect Claude to other tools so Claude can work automatically — without you opening the website. A new form submission triggers Claude to draft a response. A new email arrives and Claude categorizes it. A calendar event is created and Claude preps your briefing. This is the power level above manual use.
⚡
Claude + Zapier
Connect Claude to 6,000+ apps without coding

Zapier is a no-code automation tool. You set up "Zaps" — triggers and actions. When X happens in App A, do Y in App B. With Claude as an action, you can automate content generation, responses, and analysis.

New form submission (Typeform)
→
Zapier trigger
→
Claude drafts welcome email
→
Gmail sends it

Example automation: Student fills registration form → Zapier triggers → Claude writes personalized welcome email with their name and course → Gmail sends automatically.

🔧
Claude + Make (Integromat)
More powerful visual automation builder

Make is similar to Zapier but more powerful for complex workflows. You build visual flowcharts of automation. Ideal for multi-step workflows with conditions (if this, then that, otherwise this other thing).

New Google Sheet row
→
Make scenario
→
Claude analyzes data
→
WhatsApp notification

Example: New lead in Google Sheets → Make triggers → Claude generates lead score and personalized pitch → WhatsApp message sent to your sales team.

📊
Claude + Google Workspace
AI inside Docs, Sheets, and Gmail

Google Workspace integration means Claude can read and write Google Docs and Sheets. Combined with Google Apps Script (which Claude writes for you), you can automate document creation, report generation, and data processing right inside Google's ecosystem.

Google Form submitted
→
Apps Script triggers
→
Claude via API
→
Auto-generated Doc
Start Simple, Scale Later Don't try to integrate everything on Day 1. Start with manual prompts saved in your notes. When you're running the same prompt 3x per week, that's your signal to automate it with Zapier/Make. Integration is the reward for mastering the manual process first.

Your Personal Claude System

Build a Claude setup that knows your business and serves you better every day — using Projects, custom instructions, and your own SOPs.

From Tool to System A tool is something you pick up when needed. A system is infrastructure that works for you continuously. Building your Claude system transforms it from "that AI website" into your most important business asset.

Step 1: Create a Claude Project for Your Work

Project Setup — System Prompt Template
## About My Business
I am [Your Name], [Your Role] at [Business Name].
We [what you do, for whom, in what location].
Our core offering: [Your main products/services]
Our pricing: [Rough range]
Our target audience: [Describe your ideal customer]

## My Communication Style
When helping me write, use: [Professional/warm/direct/conversational]
Our brand voice is: [3 adjectives]
We always avoid: [jargon/certain phrases/excessive formality]

## Key Context
Our main competitors: [List 2-3]
Our differentiator: [What makes us unique]
Current focus: [What you're working on this month]

## How I Use Claude
Main tasks: content creation, client responses, planning, analysis
Always give me: actionable outputs, not just analysis
Format preference: [Bullet points / paragraphs / tables]

Step 2: Upload Your SOPs

📋

What SOPs to Upload

Client onboarding process, response templates, course outlines, pricing sheets, FAQ documents, brand guidelines, team processes.

🧠

Why This Changes Everything

Claude reads your SOPs and follows your actual processes. Response templates match your real templates. Pricing is accurate. Processes are consistent.

Step 3: Build Your Saved Prompt Library

Your Prompt Library Structure

Organize saved prompts in your notes app (Notion, Apple Notes, Google Keep) by category:

CategoryPrompts to Save
Daily OperationsMorning briefing, email responses, meeting prep, task prioritization
Content CreationBlog post, Instagram caption, LinkedIn post, WhatsApp broadcast
Business AnalysisCompetitor review, weekly report, monthly summary, decision analysis
Client CommunicationEnquiry response, follow-up, pricing explanation, objection handling
Team & HRJob descriptions, feedback templates, training outlines, meeting agendas

Step 4: Weekly Prompt Improvement

1

Track What Works

Note which prompts gave you the best outputs this week. Star them in your library.

2

Refine What Doesn't

If a prompt consistently gives mediocre output, update the Instructions or Specifics sections.

3

Add New Use Cases

Every time you use Claude for something new that works well, add it to your library before you forget.

4

Share with Your Team

A prompt library is a team asset. Share effective prompts so everyone benefits from each individual's discoveries.
