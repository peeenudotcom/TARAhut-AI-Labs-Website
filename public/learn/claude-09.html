Section 2

Blog & Article Writing

The iterative 4-step process: Outline → Draft → Edit → SEO Optimize. Never stare at a blank page again.

The Iterative Writing Process Professional writers don't write in one shot. They outline, draft, refine, optimize. Claude makes each step faster and better. The key: treat each step as a separate conversation turn.

Step 1: Generate a Smart Outline

CRISP Prompt — Blog Outline
Context: I run a small business training institute in Kotkapura, Punjab. My readers are local entrepreneurs aged 25-45.
Role: Act as an expert content strategist with deep expertise in small business and digital marketing.
Instructions: Create a detailed blog post outline for a 1200-word article. Include: compelling H1 title, 4-5 H2 sections with bullet points of what to cover, intro hook idea, and conclusion CTA.
Specifics: Topic: "5 Ways Indian Small Businesses Can Use AI to Save 10 Hours a Week." Target keywords: AI for small business, AI productivity India.
Purpose: Build authority and drive enquiries for our AI training course.

Step 2: Write the Full Draft

CRISP Prompt — Full Draft
Now write the full article based on this outline.

Tone: Warm, expert, conversational — like a knowledgeable friend explaining clearly.
Include: Real examples from Indian businesses, 2-3 relatable scenarios, a strong opening hook, and a closing paragraph that invites readers to join our course.
Length: 1200-1400 words. Use subheadings, short paragraphs, occasional bold for key points.
Avoid: Jargon, overly technical language, Western-only examples.

Step 3: Edit & Strengthen

CRISP Prompt — Edit Pass
Review this article draft for:
1. Opening hook strength — is it compelling enough to keep reading?
2. Flow between sections — does each section transition naturally?
3. Weak sentences — flag any that are vague or could be cut.
4. Examples — are they relatable to an Indian small business audience?
5. CTA — does the closing call-to-action feel natural, not pushy?

Provide specific rewrites for any weak areas, not just commentary.

Step 4: SEO Optimization

CRISP Prompt — SEO Pass
Optimize this article for SEO. Provide:
1. SEO title (under 60 characters, includes primary keyword)
2. Meta description (150-160 characters, includes keyword, drives clicks)
3. Suggested URL slug
4. 3-4 related long-tail keywords to naturally weave in
5. 1-2 suggested internal link anchor text ideas
6. Alt text for 2 suggested images that would pair well with this post

Primary keyword: AI for small business India
Secondary keyword: AI productivity tools 2025
Power Move: Repurpose in One Prompt After the article is done, say: "Now create: (1) a LinkedIn post summarizing the key insight, (2) 3 Instagram caption options, (3) an email subject line for sending this to my newsletter." One article becomes a full week of content.

Section 4

Multilingual Content

English → Hindi → Punjabi. Claude doesn't just translate — it culturally adapts your content for authentic regional impact.

Beyond Translation Translation converts words. Cultural adaptation converts meaning. A social media caption that works in English might feel cold in Hindi or unnatural in Punjabi. Claude understands the cultural layer — idioms, formality levels, regional references, and emotional resonance.

Example: Instagram Caption in 3 Languages

English (Original)
Ready to take your business to the next level? Our 15-day AI masterclass will change how you work forever. Limited seats. Real skills. Real results. DM us to enrol today. 🚀
Hindi (Culturally Adapted)
क्या आप भी अपने काम को और बेहतर बनाना चाहते हैं? हमारे 15-दिन के AI कोर्स में सीखें वो सब जो आपके बिज़नेस को नई ऊंचाई पर ले जाए। सीमित सीटें उपलब्ध हैं — आज ही जुड़ें। 💡 DM करें या call करें।
Punjabi (Gurmukhi Script)
ਆਪਣੇ ਕਾਰੋਬਾਰ ਨੂੰ ਅਗਲੇ ਪੱਧਰ 'ਤੇ ਲੈ ਜਾਣਾ ਚਾਹੁੰਦੇ ਹੋ? ਸਾਡੇ 15-ਦਿਨਾਂ AI ਕੋਰਸ ਵਿੱਚ ਸ਼ਾਮਲ ਹੋਵੋ ਅਤੇ ਸਿੱਖੋ ਉਹ ਸਭ ਜੋ ਤੁਹਾਡੇ ਕੰਮ ਨੂੰ ਬਦਲ ਦੇਵੇਗਾ। ਅੱਜ ਹੀ ਸੰਪਰਕ ਕਰੋ। 🙏

Multilingual CRISP Prompt

Multilingual Content Prompt
Context: Creating social media content for a training institute in Kotkapura, Punjab targeting local entrepreneurs.
Role: Expert bilingual copywriter fluent in English, Hindi, and Punjabi with deep understanding of Punjabi business culture.
Instructions: Create this Instagram caption in 3 versions: (1) English, (2) Hindi (Devanagari script), (3) Punjabi (Gurmukhi script). Do NOT just translate — culturally adapt each version to feel native to that language's audience. Include appropriate emojis and a call to action.
Content: [Your English caption here]
Notes: Hindi should feel warm and motivational. Punjabi should feel community-oriented and respectful. Avoid overly formal language in both.

Cultural Adaptation Principles

🇮🇳

Hindi Adaptation Tips

Use respectful second-person (आप). Incorporate motivational phrasing. Blend English business terms naturally (like "business," "course," "AI"). Keep sentences conversational and warm.

🙏

Punjabi Adaptation Tips

Use ਤੁਸੀਂ for respectful address. Include community references. Gurmukhi script shows authenticity. Blend Roman script for technical terms. End with ਧੰਨਵਾਦ (thank you) or ਜੀ for respect.

📊

When to Use Each Language

English: Professional audiences, SEO content, pan-India reach. Hindi: Mass market, emotional stories, motivational content. Punjabi: Local community, personal trust, cultural events.

⚠️

Always Human-Review

Claude's Hindi is excellent. Punjabi is good. Always have a native speaker review before publishing — especially idioms, culturally sensitive references, and formal communications.

The 90% Rule Claude's multilingual content is 90% ready. That final 10% — the idioms, the cultural nuance, the regional slang — needs a human eye. Use Claude to scale, use humans to perfect. This combination beats hiring a full content team.
