Section 01

The 5-Step Blog Pipeline

Most people ask Claude to "write a blog post." That produces mediocre content. The 5-step pipeline produces professional, SEO-optimised articles that rank and convert.

💡

The 5 Steps: Research → Outline → Draft → Edit → SEO

Each step is a separate prompt in the SAME conversation. Step 1 researches the topic deeply. Step 2 creates a detailed outline. Step 3 writes the first draft based on the outline. Step 4 edits for quality (tone, readability, flow). Step 5 optimises for SEO (meta title, description, keywords, headers). The result is 10x better than a single "write me a blog post" prompt.

The key instruction: "NOT translations of each other." Each version should feel originally written in that language. Claude handles this brilliantly.

📈

Why Native > Translated

Translated content feels off. Direct Hindi translation of English marketing sounds like a government notice. Direct Punjabi translation sounds robotic. Native content resonates. English for LinkedIn professionals. Hindi for Facebook's pan-India audience. Punjabi for WhatsApp groups where trust is personal. Each platform, each language, each audience needs its own voice.

The Trilingual Content Strategy

  • English: LinkedIn, Instagram (urban/professional audience), blog, website
  • Hindi: Facebook, Instagram Reels (pan-India reach), YouTube descriptions
  • Punjabi: WhatsApp groups, Facebook local groups, personal messages, voice notes scripts
You can now create native-quality content in 3 languages from a single prompt. That is a 3x content multiplier.

A social media manager in Chandigarh posts only in English. Reaches 20% of the potential audience. You post in 3 languages. Your English post reaches professionals. Your Hindi post reaches pan-India users. Your Punjabi WhatsApp message reaches the uncle who will actually enrol. Same effort, 3x the reach.

You are not managing one social media presence. You are running a trilingual content operation.

Why Scripts Need This Level of Detail

A 60-second video has zero margin for rambling. Every second counts. The hook must stop scrolling in 3 seconds or you lose the viewer forever. Visual cues ensure your editor knows exactly what to show. Energy levels guide your delivery. Word count ensures you do not run over. This prompt produces scripts that professional YouTubers pay ₹2,000-5,000 per minute for.
