Section 02

C-R-I-S-P Deep Dive

Each letter is a force multiplier. Skip one and your output quality drops. Master all 5 and your prompts become unstoppable. "Har letter ik superpower hai."

C — CONTEXT🌎

Background Information

The background AI needs. Without context, AI guesses. With context, AI targets. Example: "Write an email" (no context) vs "Write an email for a B2B SaaS company targeting HR managers in mid-sized Indian companies evaluating HR software." "Context = AI nu dasna ki situation hai."

R — ROLE🎭

Who Should AI Be?

The role determines voice, expertise, and perspective. "Act as a senior marketing strategist with 15 years B2B experience" produces VERY different output than "Act as a college intern." Same task, different quality. "Role badlo = quality badlo."

I — INSTRUCTIONS📝

Exact Steps to Follow

Tell AI exactly WHAT to do and HOW. "Write an email" (vague) vs "Write a 200-word cold outreach. Start with a pain point. Include one stat. End with a soft CTA." Step-by-step = precise output. "Vague instructions = vague output."

S — SPECIFICS📌

Concrete Details

Product name, audience, word count, tone, things to include, things to AVOID. Specifics eliminate ambiguity. "Tone: professional but warm. Avoid: jargon, buzzwords. Include: the phrase 'transform your HR process'." "Specifics = fine-tuning."

P — PARAMETERS⚙️

Output Format

Format: bullet points / paragraph / table / JSON. Length: 100 words / 500 words. Style: formal / casual. Language: English / Hindi / bilingual. Parameters are your delivery instructions. "Format dasna = delivery control karna."

Most Common CRISP Mistakes

1. Skipping Role: The role is the #1 quality lever. Never skip it.
2. Vague Instructions: "Write a good email" — what does "good" mean? Be specific.
3. No Constraints: Always specify what to AVOID, not just what to include.
4. Missing Format: Without format guidance, AI defaults to walls of text.
5. One-shot and done: CRISP prompts improve with iteration. Refine and re-run.
