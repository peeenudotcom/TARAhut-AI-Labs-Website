Step 01 — Project Setup

Create the Gupta Electronics Project

In Claude, click "New Project". Name it "Gupta Electronics — Staff Training". In Project Settings → Custom Instructions, paste the following:

You are a professional HR consultant and business writer creating internal training materials for Gupta Electronics, a consumer electronics retail store in Kotkapura, Punjab. The staff has varying education levels. Always use clear, practical language — no jargon. Write in English but include Hindi phrases where they naturally fit (customer greetings, common product names). Tone: professional, supportive, encouraging. Default output format: well-structured documents with clear headings and numbered steps where applicable. Always prioritize actionability over theory.
Step 02 — Knowledge Upload

Upload Store Context (Create a Quick Reference Doc)

Create a simple text file with this content and upload it to the Project knowledge base:

GUPTA ELECTRONICS — STORE FACTS Location: Near Bus Stand, Kotkapura, Faridkot District, Punjab Founded: 2010 | Owner: Mr. Raj Gupta | Staff Size: 8 (including 4 new hires) Products: Smartphones, Laptops, TVs, Refrigerators, ACs, Kitchen Appliances, Accessories Brands: Samsung, Apple, Realme, OnePlus, LG, Whirlpool, Havells Store Hours: 10am-9pm Mon-Sat, 11am-7pm Sunday Payment: Cash, UPI (GPay, PhonePe, Paytm), EMI available Warranty: Standard manufacturer warranty. Store-level troubleshooting support. Key selling points: Competitive pricing, EMI options, local trust, after-sales support
Step 03 — Generate Main Training Guide

Create the Primary Document Artifact

In your first conversation within the Project, send this prompt:

Create a complete Staff Training Guide for Gupta Electronics as a structured document. Include these 5 sections: 1. Welcome & Culture — Why Gupta Electronics, what we stand for, what good service looks like 2. Product Knowledge Basics — How to quickly learn about a new product category, key specs customers ask about for phones, laptops, and appliances 3. Customer Service Standards — Greeting, consultation process, handling objections, closing the sale professionally 4. Upselling Techniques — How to recommend accessories and extended protection ethically and effectively 5. Store SOPs — Opening/closing checklist, billing process, return/exchange policy, escalation to Mr. Gupta Make this warm, practical, and immediately useful on day one. Use Hindi greetings where natural (e.g., "Namaste, main aapki kaise help kar sakta hoon?"). Include at least 2 real customer scenarios in the Customer Service section.
Step 04 — Generate Supporting Artifacts

Create Companion Documents

After the main guide, create these additional Artifacts in separate conversations within the same Project:

Artifact 2 — Welcome Letter: "Write a warm, professional welcome letter from Mr. Raj Gupta to new staff members. Reference Gupta Electronics' 15-year history. Set expectations clearly. End with an encouraging call to action. Under 250 words." Artifact 3 — Quick Reference Card: "Create an HTML quick-reference card that new staff can keep at the counter. Include: top 5 customer FAQ responses, opening/closing checklist, billing steps, common Hindi phrases, and Mr. Gupta's contact for escalations. Style it clean and printable." Artifact 4 — Product Knowledge Flash Cards: "Create 4 product knowledge cards as HTML — one each for: Smartphones, Laptops, Refrigerators, TVs. Each card: key specs customers ask about, top 3 selling points, common objection + response."
Step 05 — Iterate

Refine Each Artifact Through Targeted Feedback

For each Artifact, do at least one meaningful iteration. Use these prompts:

For the Training Guide: "The Customer Service section is good but needs more depth on handling price objections. Add a 5-step objection handling script specifically for when customers say 'bahut mehenga hai' (it's too expensive)." For the HTML Quick Reference Card: "Make this more visually organized. Add color-coded sections: green for SOP steps, blue for customer interactions, orange for escalation. Add the store address and phone number in the header." For the Product Flash Cards: "Add a 'Don't Say / Do Say' section to each card showing the difference between amateur and professional product descriptions."

What to Show in a Client Presentation

The Project Structure

Open the Claude Project and show the client how it's organized. Custom instructions visible. Documents uploaded. They see that their business context is permanently embedded — not a one-time magic trick.

🎬

The Generation Process

If they're skeptical, show them live generation. Ask a relevant question in the Project and let them watch Claude respond with full business context. This converts skeptics to believers instantly.

📄

The Deliverables

Walk through each Artifact: the main guide, the welcome letter, the HTML card, the product flash cards. Explain what each does and how it's used. Let them interact with the HTML Artifact.

⚡

The Iteration Capability

Show one live iteration: "Mr. Gupta, what would you like to change?" Take their feedback, type it into Claude, and show the update in under 60 seconds. This real-time capability is often the most impressive part.

Presenting Your AI Workflow as a Professional Service

How to Position This Work

Don't say "I used AI to write this." Say: "I built a custom AI workspace for your business that maintains your brand, your products, and your standards across all content."

The former sounds like you took a shortcut. The latter — which is accurate — positions you as a technology-enabled professional who delivers more value faster. That's worth more, not less.
