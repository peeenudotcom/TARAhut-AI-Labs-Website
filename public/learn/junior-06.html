🚫 Common Mistakes Kids Make — Click to See the Fix!

❌ "She don't know the answer." +
✅ "She doesn't know the answer."
💡 With "she", "he", "it" — we use "doesn't", not "don't". "Don't" is for "I", "you", "we", "they".
❌ "I am went to school yesterday." +
✅ "I went to school yesterday."
💡 For past tense, just use "went" — we don't need "am" with past tense verbs. "Am" is for present tense only.
❌ "He is more taller than me." +
✅ "He is taller than me."
💡 "Taller" already means "more tall" — we can't use "more" with words that end in "-er". Just say "taller"!
❌ "I have ate my food." +
✅ "I have eaten my food."
💡 With "have", we use the past participle form: eat → eaten (not "ate"). "Ate" is used alone: "I ate my food."
