Section 4

Cultural Adaptation

Not just translation — adapting tone, references, and examples for Indian audiences. Western prompt vs Indian prompt comparison.

The Adaptation Principle Translation converts words. Cultural adaptation converts meaning. A motivational post about "work-life balance" in English might fall flat in Punjabi where the cultural value is "family first, business for family." The same message, reframed for cultural values, resonates 10x more.

Western Prompt vs Indian Prompt Comparison

WESTERN FRAMING

"Use AI to optimize your personal productivity and achieve your individual success goals through automation of repetitive tasks."

Emphasis: Individual, productivity, optimization, self-achievement

INDIAN/PUNJABI FRAMING

"AI से अपना बिज़नेस बढ़ाओ और परिवार के लिए ज़्यादा समय निकालो" (Grow your business with AI and find more time for your family)

Emphasis: Business growth, family, community, collective benefit

Cultural Adaptation Principles for Indian Content

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

Family-Centered Framing

Connect business success to family wellbeing. "Earn more for your family" resonates more than "achieve personal success" in Indian markets.

🤝

Community Trust Signals

Mention local references — Kotkapura, Punjab, North India. "Other businesses in your area are using AI" creates peer trust more effectively than global statistics.

🙏

Respectful Language

Always use आप (aap) in Hindi, ਤੁਸੀਂ (tuseen) in Punjabi. These are not optional — using informal pronouns in business content is disrespectful in Indian culture.

💰

ROI Over Lifestyle

Indian audiences respond to "save money," "earn more," "grow your business" over lifestyle messaging. Be concrete about financial benefits.

🌾

Local Examples

Use kiryana stores, coaching centers, clinics, farms — not "coffee shops" or "startup founders." Examples must be recognizable to your audience.

📿

Seasonal & Cultural References

Reference Lohri, Diwali, Baisakhi, harvest seasons in your content calendar. These cultural touchpoints create instant resonance.

ADAPTATION RULES:

2. Replace Western examples with Indian equivalents (coffee shop → chai dukan, startup → small business, CEO → business owner)
3. Use family/community framing where relevant (individual success → family benefit)
4. Add local references where natural (mention Punjab/North India context)
5. Use the appropriate respectful pronoun (आप / ਤੁਸੀਂ)
6. Adjust idioms — replace Western idioms with Indian equivalents
7. Concrete financial benefits over abstract lifestyle messaging

Output in: [Devanagari Hindi / Gurmukhi Punjabi]
Tone: [Motivational / Professional / Community / Warm]
