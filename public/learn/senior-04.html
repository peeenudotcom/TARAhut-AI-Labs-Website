🕵️ Fact vs Fiction
AI Can Lie. Seriously.

AI sounds confident even when it's completely wrong. This section teaches you to think like a detective and verify what you read!

🚨

The Scary Truth About AI Hallucinations

In 2023, a lawyer in the USA used ChatGPT to write a legal brief. ChatGPT listed 6 court cases as evidence — every single one was fake. The lawyer didn't check. He submitted it to court. He got in serious trouble. The AI sounded completely confident. This is called an "AI hallucination" — when AI makes up facts with total confidence. 😰

🎮 Fact vs Opinion: Can You Tell the Difference?

Read each statement. Decide if it's a FACT (can be verified) or an OPINION (someone's view). Then reveal the answer! 🔍

"India has 28 states and 8 Union Territories."
"Virat Kohli is the greatest cricket player who ever lived."
"The Eiffel Tower is 330 meters tall."
"Social media is ruining the younger generation."

🔍 The 3-Step Verification Method

🔎

Step 1: Cross-Check

Search the AI's claim on Google. Find 2-3 reliable sources (government sites, Wikipedia, news sites). If they agree → likely true. If they disagree → AI was wrong!

📅

Step 2: Check the Date

AI's training data has a cutoff. Ask AI: "What is your training data cutoff?" For anything from the last 1-2 years, always verify online. News, statistics, and events change!

🎯

Step 3: Ask AI to Prove It

Ask: "What is your source for this?" Good AI will tell you it can't verify sources. That honesty is a good sign! Then you know to verify yourself.

Section 02

⚠️ The Copy-Paste
Problem

Submitting AI-generated homework as your own is a trap. Here's why teachers ALWAYS find out, and why it actually hurts YOUR future.

😬

Real Talk: Teachers Are Smarter Than You Think

If you copy-paste from ChatGPT and submit it as your essay — your teacher will probably know. How?

  • Your normal writing style is completely different from AI's
  • AI uses certain phrases like "It is worth noting that..." and "This underscores the importance of..."
  • AI-detection tools like Turnitin and GPTZero exist and are free to use
  • AI doesn't make the small errors a student normally makes
  • AI's answer is often too perfect — no personal examples, no emotion
🎯

The RIGHT Way: Research vs Copy

WRONG: "Write my essay on water conservation. Copy." Submit. Done. ❌

RIGHT: Use AI to:
• Research: "What are the main arguments about water conservation?"
• Get data: "Give me 3 statistics about water scarcity in India"
• Understand: "Explain the impact of water shortage on agriculture"
• Then: WRITE YOUR OWN ESSAY using these as reference points ✅

💡

Why YOUR Writing Matters More in the AI Era

Here's a plot twist: original writing is MORE valuable now than before AI! AI can generate average text instantly. But YOUR authentic perspective, YOUR personal stories, YOUR original thinking — that's something AI can never replicate. Companies, colleges, and scholarship committees increasingly VALUE original thinkers over people who just use AI. Your voice is your competitive advantage. 🚀

🤔 Class Discussion

Discuss these questions as a group:

  • If you copy an AI essay, whose ideas are you learning? And whose grade do you think you deserve?
  • If everyone uses AI for homework, what skill do you actually have at the end of school?
  • What's the difference between using AI to HELP you and using AI to REPLACE you?
You now understand the line between using AI smartly and using it dishonestly. Big difference! ✅

✍️ Write Like a Pro
The 200-Word Technique

Forget boring essays. Learn a 4-part formula that makes ANY opinion paragraph powerful and impressive! Then write one yourself.

🏗️

The 4-Part Opinion Paragraph Formula

Every great opinion paragraph has exactly 4 parts. Master this structure and you can write about ANY topic in under 10 minutes:

1. Topic Sentence (20 words): State YOUR clear view. Don't be vague.
2. Evidence (50 words): One fact, statistic, or example that supports your view.
3. Personal View (80 words): Why YOU believe this. Include your experience or reasoning.
4. Conclusion (30 words): Wrap up and call to action or final thought.

📝 Example: The 4-Part Formula in Action

Topic: "Should students be taught AI in schools?"

PART 1 — TOPIC SENTENCE

AI education should be made compulsory in all Indian schools starting from Class 8, because it is the most important skill of our generation.

PART 2 — EVIDENCE

According to a 2024 World Economic Forum report, 85 million jobs will be displaced by AI by 2025, while 97 million new roles requiring AI skills will emerge. Indian students who learn AI early will be prepared for both challenges.

PART 3 — PERSONAL VIEW

In my experience at AI Explorer, I have seen how even a few hours of learning changes how we approach problems. Before this course, I thought AI was only for engineers. Now I realise it is a tool anyone can use — for studying, creating, and solving problems. A classmate who previously struggled with English used AI to improve his writing, and his last essay was graded the highest in class. These are the outcomes that textbooks alone cannot achieve.

PART 4 — CONCLUSION

The question is not whether AI will enter classrooms — it already has. The question is whether we will teach students to use it wisely, or leave them to figure it out alone.

🎯 Take-Home Mission
Fact File + Opinion Paragraph

Two parts tonight: a fact file and your best opinion paragraph. Get a parent to read it — their feedback is golden! 🏠

📋

Part 1: Fact File (15 minutes)

Choose ANY topic you find interesting (climate change, IPL salaries, electric vehicles, social media, space exploration). Use AI to research 10 facts. Then verify 3 of them on Google. Note which ones were accurate and which (if any) were wrong. Write it down — this is your fact file! 🔍

✍️

Part 2: Opinion Paragraph (20 minutes)

Write a 200-word opinion paragraph using the 4-part formula on this topic: "Should AI be allowed in board examinations?"
Use AI to help you RESEARCH (find facts, statistics). But write every word yourself. Get a parent or sibling to read it. Ask them: "Does this sound like ME?" Their answer will tell you how authentic your writing is. 📝
