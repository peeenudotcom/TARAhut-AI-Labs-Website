Section 01

Business Emails That Get Replies

The average professional ignores 70% of emails they receive. Here's how to be in the 30% they actually respond to — using AI as your writing partner.

💡

Why Most Business Emails Fail

They're too long, too vague, or too focused on the sender. A great business email answers one question immediately: "What's in it for me?" AI doesn't just write faster — it forces you to think about the recipient's perspective, which is where most people fail.

Template 1: Cold Outreach Email

📧 Cold Outreach — Getting a Meeting or Partnership

High Impact
AI Prompt to Use (in ChatGPT or Claude)
Write a cold outreach email to [Name], the owner of [Business Name] in [City]. I am [Your Name], [Your Role] at [Your Company]. Goal: Get a 15-minute call to discuss [specific mutual benefit]. Context: [1-2 sentences about why you're reaching out to them specifically] Tone: Professional but warm. Concise — under 120 words. Include a clear, low-friction call to action.
Example Output Structure AI Will Generate

Subject: Quick question about [their specific interest]

Hi [Name],

I noticed [specific observation about their business]. I work with [type of businesses] to [specific outcome they care about].

[One sentence about the value you bring — not about you, about them].

Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call this week? I have [Tuesday/Wednesday] available — happy to work around your schedule.

Best,
[Your Name]

Template 2: Follow-Up Email

🔄 Follow-Up — After a Meeting or No Response

Essential Skill
AI Prompt to Use
Write a follow-up email to [Name] after our meeting on [date]. Key points discussed: [2-3 bullet points from the meeting] Next step agreed upon: [what was decided] What I need from them: [specific action] Make it feel warm, not pushy. Reference something specific from our conversation to show I was paying attention. Under 100 words.
Why This Works
🧠

Referencing a specific detail from the conversation ("You mentioned the delivery challenge you're facing") shows you listened. AI can help you phrase this naturally without sounding like you're reciting notes. This single tactic doubles follow-up response rates.

Template 3: Complaint Response

😡 Complaint Response — Turn an Angry Customer into a Loyal One

Reputation Saver
AI Prompt to Use
Write a professional response to this customer complaint: "[Paste the exact complaint here]" My response should: - Acknowledge their frustration genuinely (not robotically) - Take responsibility without admitting legal fault - Explain what we're doing to fix it - Offer [specific resolution: refund/replacement/discount] - End with a genuine commitment to improvement Tone: Empathetic, professional, human. Not corporate-speak.
The AI Advantage Here
💡

When we're emotionally reactive, we write defensive emails. AI stays calm. It helps you respond professionally even when you're frustrated. The result: customers feel heard, and you protect your business reputation. One well-handled complaint often creates your most loyal customers.

60-Second Action

Pick ONE real email you need to write this week. Open Claude or ChatGPT, use one of the prompts above, and send it. Notice the difference in how it feels to write and how people respond.

You now have 3 email templates that professionals pay copywriters thousands to create.

Resume & LinkedIn Optimization

70% of resumes are rejected by automated systems before a human ever reads them. AI helps you write for both robots and humans.

🚨

The Brutal Truth About Resumes

Most resumes are a list of duties, not achievements. Recruiters spend an average of 7 seconds on a resume before deciding. AI doesn't just fix grammar — it transforms "Was responsible for sales" into "Grew monthly sales by 34% in Q2 by implementing a referral system" — the kind of language that gets called for interviews.

Before & After: AI Resume Transformation

❌ Before (Weak)

Work Experience:
• Managed social media accounts
• Created content for Instagram and Facebook
• Helped grow followers
• Worked with marketing team
• Responsible for email campaigns

✅ After (AI-Enhanced)

Work Experience:
• Managed social media presence across Instagram & Facebook, growing combined following by 2,400 (from 800 to 3,200) in 6 months
• Created 45+ content pieces/month; top post reached 18K organic views
• Led email marketing campaigns with 28% avg open rate (industry avg: 21%)
• Collaborated with 4-person marketing team to execute quarterly brand campaigns

Exact Prompts to Transform Your Resume

🌟 Prompt 1: Achievement Transformer

Transform these resume bullet points into achievement-focused statements. Add specific metrics where reasonable. Use action verbs. Keep each under 20 words. [Paste your bullet points here] Context: I work in [industry/role] at [type of company]. My target job is [job title].

🌟 Prompt 2: LinkedIn Summary Writer

Write a LinkedIn "About" section for me. Name: [Your Name] Current Role: [Role] at [Company] Years of experience: [X years] in [field] Key skills: [list 3-5 skills] Career goal: [what you're looking for] One thing I'm proud of: [specific achievement] Personality note: [professional/creative/detail-oriented/etc.] Make it 3 short paragraphs. First-person. Confident but not arrogant. End with what I'm open to.

🌟 Prompt 3: ATS Keyword Optimizer

I'm applying for this job: [Paste the job description] Here is my current resume: [Paste your resume] Identify: 1. Keywords in the job description missing from my resume 2. Which of my experiences are most relevant to highlight 3. Specific changes to make my resume ATS-friendly for this role Do NOT fabricate experience. Only suggest genuine reframing.

A student from our previous batch spent 45 minutes with Claude optimizing her resume for a digital marketing role. She got 3 interview calls in the next 2 weeks — after months of silence. The resume didn't change what she'd done. It changed how it was communicated. That's the power of the right words.

Section 03

Proposals & Research Reports

The documents that win clients and establish credibility. AI can structure and draft them in minutes — you just add your expertise.

Client Proposal Structure (AI-Powered)

Section 1

Executive Summary

Prompt: "Write a 3-sentence executive summary for a proposal to [client type] to help them [specific goal]. We are offering [service] at [price range]."

Section 2

Problem Statement

Prompt: "Write a 'Problem We're Solving' section for a client in [industry]. Focus on [specific pain points]. Make them feel understood, not sold to."

Section 3

Our Solution

Prompt: "Write a 'Our Approach' section explaining how [your service] solves [their problem]. Break it into 3 phases with clear deliverables. Be specific."

Section 4

Timeline & Investment

Prompt: "Create a professional timeline and pricing table for a [X-week] project. Include: Discovery, Execution, Review, Delivery phases."

Section 5

Why Choose Us

Prompt: "Write a 'Why Work With Us' section. Highlight [your differentiators]. Tone: confident, not boastful. Include a brief social proof statement."

Section 6

Next Steps

Prompt: "Write a clear, action-oriented 'Next Steps' section. The client should know exactly what to do to move forward. Reduce friction to a single step."

Research Report with Citations

📋 Research Report Workflow

Use Perplexity + Claude
Step 1: Research (Use Perplexity)
Search on Perplexity: "Latest statistics and data on [your topic] in India 2024-2025" Collect: key numbers, research findings, expert quotes, source links
Step 2: Write the Report (Use Claude)
Write a professional research report on [topic]. Structure: Executive Summary → Context → Key Findings (use the data below) → Analysis → Recommendations → Conclusion Data to include: [Paste your Perplexity research here] Format: Use headers, bullet points where appropriate. 800-1000 words. Professional tone. Include a "Sources" section at the end listing all citations.
Step 3: Format & Polish
Review this report and: 1. Improve the executive summary to be more compelling 2. Ensure all data claims have clear source references 3. Add a "Key Takeaways" box at the top with 3 bullet points 4. Make the recommendations more actionable and specific
