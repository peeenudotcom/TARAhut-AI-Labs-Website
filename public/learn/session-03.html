What is a Prompt?

A prompt is the instruction you give AI. It's the single most important factor determining the quality of what AI produces for you.

💬

The Core Formula

Input (Prompt) → AI Model → Output (Result)

You control the input completely. The AI model is fixed. The output quality is almost entirely determined by how well you construct your prompt. This session gives you the system that makes every AI interaction 10x better.

Why Prompts Matter — A Dramatic Example

❌ Bad Prompt

Write about marketing.
AI Result: A generic 3-paragraph overview of marketing theory. Could apply to any business in any country. Completely useless for your specific needs. You'd delete it immediately.
VS

✅ Good Prompt

Act as a digital marketing expert specializing in small Indian businesses. Write 5 Instagram caption ideas for a clothing shop in Kotkapura, Punjab that sells traditional Punjabi suits. The captions should be in a mix of Hindi and English (Hinglish), friendly in tone, include a call-to-action, and be under 150 characters each.
AI Result: 5 specific, ready-to-post captions in Hinglish, with CTAs, perfectly sized for Instagram. You could use all 5 today.
🔬

What Changed Between Those Two Prompts?

The good prompt told AI: 1) What role to play (marketing expert for Indian businesses) 2) Context (small shop in Kotkapura, Punjabi suits) 3) Exact task (5 Instagram captions) 4) Format requirements (Hinglish, friendly tone, CTA, under 150 chars). That structure — Role, Context, Task, Format — is exactly what you'll learn in Section 2.

⚡

Speed Multiplier

A well-crafted prompt gets you usable output on the first try. A bad prompt requires 5-10 attempts to get something decent. Better prompts = hours saved every week.

🎯

Precision Control

Your prompt is the steering wheel. The more specific your direction, the more accurately AI takes you where you want to go. Vague direction → lost in the wrong city.

💰

Monetisable Skill

Companies pay ₹20K–80K/month for people who consistently get excellent AI outputs. The difference? Systematic prompt engineering. That's what this session teaches.

You now understand something most ChatGPT users will never learn: the quality gap is almost always in the prompt.

Section 02

The RCTF Framework

Role + Context + Task + Format. Four elements that transform every prompt from average to excellent. Once you learn this, you can't unlearn it.

R
ROLE
Tell AI who to be. What expert, what persona, what specialist? The role sets the knowledge domain and communication style.
Examples: "Act as a senior tax consultant..." | "You are an expert Instagram marketer..." | "As a Punjabi-speaking school teacher..."
C
CONTEXT
Give AI the background it needs. Who are you? What's the situation? Who is the audience? What platform is this for?
Examples: "...for a clothing shop in Kotkapura with 500 Instagram followers..." | "...my audience is farmers in Punjab aged 35-55..."
T
TASK
State exactly what you want done. Be specific. Include the number of items, the goal, and any constraints.
Examples: "...write 5 WhatsApp broadcast messages..." | "...create a 7-day content calendar..." | "...list 10 product descriptions..."
F
FORMAT
Specify how you want the output structured. Length, language, tone, bullet points, table, numbered list, JSON, etc.
Examples: "...in bullet points, under 100 words each..." | "...as a table with columns: Day | Topic | Caption..." | "...in Hindi, formal tone..."
🏆

The Full RCTF Template

Act as [ROLE]. [CONTEXT about your situation]. [Specific TASK]. Format the output as [FORMAT specifications].

You don't always need all four — but the more you include, the better the result. Start with all four until it becomes second nature.

Section 03

Common Prompting Mistakes

Most people make the same 4 errors with every single AI prompt they write. Here they are — with before/after fixes you can apply immediately.

🌫️

Mistake 1: Too Vague

❌ "Write something about fitness." ✅ "Act as a certified fitness trainer. Write a 7-day beginner workout plan for a 25-year-old woman in India who works from home and has 30 minutes per day. Include exercises, sets, and reps. Format as a weekly table."

Vague prompts force AI to guess what you want. AI always picks the safest, most generic interpretation — which is rarely what you actually needed.

🧩

Mistake 2: No Context

❌ "Help me market my business." ✅ "I sell handmade clay diyas from my home in Patiala. My customers are middle-class families aged 30-50 who celebrate traditional festivals. I have no budget for paid ads. Suggest 5 free marketing strategies specifically for this situation."

Without context, AI produces advice that could apply to anyone — which means it applies to no one effectively. Context is the difference between generic and genuinely useful.

📋

Mistake 3: No Format Specified

❌ "Tell me about investing in stocks." ✅ "Explain stock market investing for an absolute beginner in India. Give me: 1) A 5-point 'before you start' checklist, 2) A comparison of stocks vs mutual funds in a table, and 3) The 3 biggest mistakes beginners make. Use simple language, under 400 words total."

When you don't specify format, AI writes flowing paragraphs — hard to scan, hard to use. Always state: bullet points, numbered list, table, headings, word count, language, or whatever structure you need.

🎪

Mistake 4: Asking for Too Much at Once

❌ "Write a full business plan, marketing strategy, financial projections, and a pitch deck for my new restaurant." ✅ Start with: "Write a one-page executive summary for a new restaurant in Ludhiana targeting working professionals. Include concept, target market, and unique selling point." Then ask for each section separately.

When you ask for everything at once, AI produces shallow coverage across all areas. Break complex requests into focused stages. Quality deepens dramatically with focused prompts.

💡

The Golden Rule: One Prompt, One Clear Goal

The most effective prompts are focused. They have one role, one clear context, one specific task, and one defined format. If your prompt is trying to accomplish 5 things at once, break it into 5 prompts. Each conversation can be a dialogue — use follow-up messages to go deeper.
