Document Type 01

📊 Executive Summary

CQuarterly performance review for a digital marketing agency. Mixed results: revenue up 18%, client retention down to 82%, team expanding from 8 to 12 people. Board meets Friday. RYou are a senior management consultant who writes executive summaries for board presentations. IWrite an executive summary that presents performance honestly, identifies the key issue (retention decline), frames the team expansion as the strategic response, and ends with a forward-looking recommendation. SBoard members are experienced business people. They appreciate direct, data-led communication. Do not bury the retention problem — address it upfront. P350-400 words. Three sections: Performance Snapshot, Critical Issue, Strategic Response. Professional tone. No passive voice.
Document Type 02

📄 Client Proposal

CProposing a 6-month brand identity and digital presence project to a Ludhiana manufacturing company (₹50Cr annual revenue) that has no digital presence. They've had an initial exploratory meeting and showed strong interest. RYou are a senior brand strategist at a boutique branding agency with B2B manufacturing experience. IWrite a proposal that opens with the client's business problem (invisible to their target buyers), presents the solution (brand identity + digital presence), outlines the 6-month project timeline, and states pricing clearly. SProject value: ₹4.5 Lakh. Decision maker is the MD who is practical and ROI-focused. Avoid creative agency jargon. Include ROI framing: how visibility translates to inquiry volume. P600-700 words. 4 sections: Problem, Solution, Timeline, Investment. Confident, consultative tone. End with a clear next step.
Document Type 03

📋 Standard Operating Procedure

CE-commerce company with 10 customer service staff. Currently no standard process for handling refund requests — each staff member handles differently, causing inconsistency and customer complaints. RYou are an operations manager writing an SOP for the customer service team. IWrite a complete SOP for handling customer refund requests, covering: receiving the request, verifying eligibility, approving or escalating, processing the refund, and following up with the customer. SRefund policy: eligible within 7 days for product defects only. Orders over ₹5,000 require manager approval. All refunds logged in the CRM. Customer communication must be empathetic and within 24 hours. PNumbered steps. Clear, unambiguous language. Include a decision tree for approval escalation. Include template customer communication scripts. Under 500 words main body.
Document Type 04

📈 Full Research Report

CManagement team of a traditional retail chain evaluating whether to launch an online store. Need an internal research report to support or challenge the decision. RYou are a retail industry research analyst who has covered Indian retail for 8 years. IWrite a research report assessing the opportunity, risks, investment requirements, and success factors for a traditional retail chain launching D2C e-commerce in India. SCompany is a 3-store apparel chain in Tier 2 cities. Annual revenue ₹8 Cr. No current digital presence. Focus on realistic assessment — not promotional. Include both optimistic and conservative scenarios. P5 sections: Market Context, Opportunity Assessment, Risk Analysis, Investment Requirements, Recommendation. 800-1000 words. Include specific numbers and percentages wherever possible. Professional, balanced tone.

Four email categories with complete CRISP breakdowns. The most practical section in the business writing module.

Email Category 01

📩 Cold Outreach — B2B Lead Generation

CReaching out cold to the owner of a Chandigarh-based restaurant chain (5 locations) to pitch social media management services. No prior contact. RYou are a digital marketing consultant who has worked with 3 restaurant brands in Punjab. IWrite a cold outreach email that leads with a relevant observation about their social media presence, connects it to a real business outcome (foot traffic, delivery orders), and proposes a 15-minute call. SThe restaurant has 2,300 Instagram followers and posts irregularly. Their competitor across the street has 15,000 followers and runs weekly promotions. Use this comparison without naming the competitor. PUnder 120 words. Subject line included. No filler phrases. End with a specific, low-friction CTA. Professional but personalized tone.
Email Category 02

🔄 Follow-Up — After No Response

CSent a proposal 10 days ago to a manufacturer who was enthusiastic in the meeting. No response since. Third follow-up attempt. RYou are a B2B sales consultant who follows up with respectful persistence and genuine curiosity, never desperation. IWrite a follow-up email that acknowledges the silence without pressure, adds a new piece of value (a relevant insight or mini-resource), and re-opens the conversation naturally. SThe proposal was for a website redesign. Add value with a 2-sentence insight about how industrial B2B websites affect export inquiries. Make them want to respond because you gave them something useful. PUnder 100 words. No guilt-tripping. Subject line: reference of previous email. End with an easy yes/no question, not a long CTA.
Email Category 03

⚠️ Complaint Response — Handling Unhappy Customers

CCustomer received a damaged product and wrote an angry email. The damage occurred during shipping (third-party courier). This is their third order with us — a loyal customer. RYou are a senior customer experience manager who handles escalations with empathy and efficiency. IWrite a complaint response that acknowledges the frustration genuinely (not formulaically), takes clear responsibility without excessive apologizing, and proposes a concrete resolution. SResolution: full replacement product shipped within 3 days, no need to return damaged item. Customer should also receive a 15% discount code for next order as goodwill. Don't blame the courier — own the experience. PUnder 150 words. Warm, direct, no corporate language. Lead with empathy, close with the solution. No passive voice. Subject: "Your replacement is on its way."
Email Category 04

🤝 Negotiation — Price Discussion

CA potential long-term client has asked for a 25% discount on a ₹2 Lakh project. The budget has been presented as firm. You're willing to offer 10% or adjusted scope, not 25%. RYou are a confident, commercially-minded consultant who values long-term relationships but knows your worth. IWrite a negotiation response that respects their budget constraint, offers a genuine alternative (10% discount OR reduced scope), and keeps the relationship warm while firmly communicating your pricing rationale. SThe 10% offer equals ₹1.8L — this is your firm limit. The scope reduction alternative: remove the social media setup phase (save ₹35K) and keep core deliverables. Frame positively — give them a choice, not an ultimatum. PUnder 180 words. Confident but warm tone. No desperation, no over-explaining. Two clear paths forward, one ask for their decision. Professional subject line.
