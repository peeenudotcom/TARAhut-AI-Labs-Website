Why 3 Tiers Work: The Anchoring Effect

Most clients pick the middle tier. The ₹25K package makes ₹10K seem reasonable. The ₹5K package is for hesitant clients who want to "test" — they usually upgrade within 2 months.

3 tiers = professional pricing. You are no longer a "freelancer doing social media." You are a service provider with packages.

Section 02

Value-Based Pricing Psychology

Why you should never charge by the hour. Price based on the VALUE you deliver, not the time you spend.

⏰

Time-Based Pricing

"I charge ₹500/hour. This took me 3 hours = ₹1,500." Client thinks: why so expensive for 3 hours? And you are penalized for being fast.

Loses money
💎

Value-Based Pricing

"Complete social media management for ₹10,000/month. You get 20 posts, GMB, WhatsApp setup." Client thinks: that is reasonable for everything I get.

Wins clients

Demo 1: The Per-Day Breakdown Technique

When a client says "₹10,000 bahut mehnga hai," break it down:

₹10,000 / 30 days = ₹333 per day

"Sir, ₹333 per day. Agar ek bhi extra customer aaye daily — jo ₹500+ spend kare — toh yeh recover ho jaata hai. Baaki sab profit hai."

Demo 2: ROI Calculation for Clients

Show clients the math that makes them say yes:

Click to copy: "You are a business ROI analyst. A restaurant in Ludhiana spends ₹10,000/month on social media marketing. They currently get 50 walk-in customers/day with average order ₹300. Calculate: if social media increases customers by just 10% (5 extra customers/day), what is the monthly revenue increase? Compare this to the ₹10,000 investment. Present as a simple visual comparison the restaurant owner can understand."
💡

The Magic Question

When discussing pricing, always ask: "Ek naya customer aapke liye kitne ka hai?" (What is one new customer worth to you?) A restaurant customer is worth ₹300-500. A salon customer is worth ₹500-2,000. A boutique customer is worth ₹2,000-10,000. Your ₹10K package needs to bring just 5-20 extra customers per month to pay for itself.

Value pricing: you charge for results, not hours. ₹10,000/month is cheap when it brings ₹50,000+ in new business.

Section 03

Writing Professional Proposals

A proposal shows you are serious and professional. It is the document that turns interest into a signed contract.

The 8-Section Proposal Template

SECTION 1📄

Cover Page

Client business name, your name/brand, date, "Prepared by [Your Name], TARAhut AI Labs Certified." Professional first impression.

SECTION 2👤

About Us

Who you are, your training, your expertise. "AI-powered digital marketing professional trained at TARAhut AI Labs with skills in content creation, social media, and local SEO."

SECTION 3🔍

Understanding Your Business

What you learned during assessment. Shows the client you actually understand their business, challenges, and goals. This is where field visit data goes.

SECTION 4🎯

Our Recommendation

Your analysis of what they need and which package tier you recommend. Include reasoning: "Based on your current situation, I recommend the Growth Package because..."

SECTION 5📋

What You Get

Detailed deliverables per tier. List every single thing they receive. The longer this list, the more valuable your package feels.

SECTION 6💰

Investment

All 3 tiers with pricing. Use "investment" not "cost" or "price." Include the per-day breakdown. Highlight the recommended tier.

SECTION 7🚀

Next Steps

"To begin, simply: (1) Choose your package, (2) Sign the service agreement, (3) Pay 50% advance, (4) Fill the onboarding form. We start within 48 hours."

SECTION 8📜

Terms and Conditions

Payment terms, revision policy, timeline, cancellation clause. Keep it simple and clear. Not legalese but professional.

Demo 3: Generate a Proposal with CRISP in 5 Minutes

Click to copy: "[CHARACTER] You are a professional proposal writer who creates winning service proposals for digital marketing agencies serving local businesses in North India. [REQUEST] Create a complete service proposal with all 8 sections: cover page text, about us, understanding their business, recommendation, deliverables, investment (3 tiers: ₹5K, ₹10K, ₹25K), next steps, and terms. [INFORMATION] Client: Lovely Salon, Jalandhar. Women's beauty salon, 5 years old. Currently: no Instagram, dead Facebook page, no Google listing, uses personal WhatsApp. Owner: Priya Sharma, concerned about competition from 2 new salons nearby. Monthly revenue approximately ₹2-3 lakh. Services: haircut, facial, bridal, threading, waxing. [STEPS] Step 1: Write an empathetic assessment showing you understand their challenge. Step 2: Recommend Growth Package (₹10K) with clear reasoning. Step 3: Detail every deliverable. Step 4: Include the ROI math. Step 5: Write clear next steps. [POLISHING] Professional but warm tone. Include Hindi phrases naturally. Format with clear headers. Ready to copy into a Google Doc or Canva."
This single CRISP prompt generates a complete proposal in under 2 minutes. Customize it for each client by changing the Information block.
🎯

Proposals Win Clients, WhatsApp Messages Do Not

Never send pricing over WhatsApp as a text message. Always send a professional proposal document (PDF via Canva or Google Docs). The proposal itself shows you are serious, organized, and worth the investment.
