What's a Prompt? 🗣️

If AI is a genie, a prompt is your wish. And just like with a genie — HOW you say your wish matters EVERYTHING!

🧞

The Genie Rule: Garbage In = Garbage Out

Imagine asking a genie: "Give me something good." The genie is confused. What did you get? Random stuff. Now ask: "Give me a cold Thums Up, a plate of momos, and help with my Maths homework." PERFECT! AI works exactly the same way. Vague prompt = vague answer. Specific prompt = specific answer. 🎯

❌ Bad Prompt

"Write something about cricket."

✅ Good Prompt

"Write a 100-word paragraph about Virat Kohli's batting technique for my Class 9 English assignment. Use simple language."

💡

Why Does This Matter For You?

Two students ask AI for help with the same assignment. One types 5 words. The other types 30 specific words. The second student gets a 10x better answer. Same AI, same question, completely different results. The skill of writing good prompts is called Prompt Engineering — and companies pay lakhs for it! 💰

📋

The 3 Parts of Every Great Prompt

Context: Tell AI who you are and what situation you're in.
Task: Tell AI exactly what you want it to do.
Format: Tell AI how you want the answer — bullet points, paragraph, table, etc.

The 5 Prompt Power-Ups ⚡

Click each power-up to see before & after examples. These 5 techniques will transform your AI results immediately!

1

🎯 Be Specific — Kill the Vagueness

+

The more specific you are, the better the answer. Add: age, subject, word count, level, purpose.

❌ Vague

"Help me with science."

✅ Specific

"Explain Newton's 3rd law for a Class 9 student in 5 bullet points with one real-life example."

2

📝 Give Examples — Show, Don't Just Tell

+

Show AI one example of what you want. It will follow that pattern perfectly.

❌ No Example

"Write a fun caption for my Instagram photo."

✅ With Example

"Write 5 Instagram captions like this one: 'Chasing sunsets and good vibes ✨' — funny and relatable for Indian teens."

3

🎭 Set a Role — Give AI a Character

+

Tell AI to BECOME someone — a teacher, a friend, a scientist. The answers change dramatically!

❌ No Role

"Explain photosynthesis."

✅ With Role

"You are a fun science teacher who loves cricket. Explain photosynthesis using a cricket match analogy for Class 8 students."

4

📊 Choose a Format — Control the Output

+

Tell AI how to structure the answer — bullet points, numbered list, table, story, etc.

❌ No Format

"What are the causes of World War 1?"

✅ With Format

"List the 5 main causes of WW1 in a table with columns: Cause | Brief Explanation | Impact. Keep each row under 20 words."

5

🚧 Add Constraints — Set the Limits

+

Add constraints: word limit, no jargon, no introduction needed, only use given information, etc.

❌ No Constraints

"Write a Bollywood movie review."

✅ Constrained

"Write a 150-word review of Dangal for a school magazine. No spoilers. No complex words. End with a star rating out of 5."

My Top 10 Prompt Recipes

What's a Prompt Recipe?

A prompt recipe is a template you save and reuse. Instead of thinking from scratch every time, you just fill in the blanks. Professional prompt engineers earn lakhs by selling their recipe books! Tonight, you'll create your own personal one. 🚀

📝 Your Prompt Recipe Starter Pack

Here are 5 ready-made recipes. Tonight, create 5 MORE for YOUR subjects and interests!

RECIPE 1 — EXAM PREP

"Act as a Class [X] [Subject] teacher. Create 10 MCQ questions on [Chapter Name] with 4 options each. Mark the correct answer. Difficulty: medium."

RECIPE 2 — NOTE MAKING

"Summarize the chapter '[Chapter Name]' from [Subject] Class [X] in 10 bullet points. Use simple language. Include key terms in bold."

RECIPE 3 — INSTAGRAM CAPTION

"Write 5 Instagram captions for a photo of [describe photo]. Tone: [funny/inspiring/aesthetic]. Audience: Indian teens aged 14-17. Include 3 relevant hashtags."

RECIPE 4 — EXPLAIN ANYTHING

"Explain [concept] to me like I'm [age] years old. Use a [cricket/Bollywood/food] analogy. Keep it under 100 words. No technical jargon."

RECIPE 5 — DEBATE PREP

"Give me 5 strong arguments FOR [topic] and 5 strong arguments AGAINST it. Format as two columns. Class [X] level language."
