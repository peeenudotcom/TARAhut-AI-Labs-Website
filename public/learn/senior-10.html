Section 02

Research & Plan 📋

Big projects feel overwhelming until you break them into small steps. Here's how to plan like a pro.

🧠

The Project Planning Formula

Every successful project follows this pattern:
Choose topic → Research → Outline → Create → Review → Present
Don't skip steps. Rushing to "create" without research is why most projects fail.

1

Define Your Goal Clearly

In one sentence: "My project will [do/show/teach] [what] to [who]." Example: "My project will create an AI awareness poster campaign for students at my school." If you can't say it in one sentence, you haven't decided yet.

2

Research (20% of your time)

Spend time understanding your topic before creating. Use Google, ChatGPT, and YouTube. Take notes. Find 3–5 good sources. This makes your final product much stronger.

3

Choose Your Tools

What will you use to build this? Google Slides/Canva (presentations), Notion/Google Docs (reports), Canva AI/Adobe Express (designs), ChatGPT (writing/ideas). Pick simple tools you know.

4

Set Mini Deadlines

Break into 3 chunks: Today → Outline done. Before Session 11 → First draft done. Session 11 → Peer review. Session 12 → Final presentation. Work backwards from the showcase!

5

Ask for Help When Stuck

Being stuck is normal. When stuck for more than 10 minutes: ask a classmate, ask the teacher, or ask ChatGPT. Never spend 30 minutes spinning on the same problem alone.

💡

AI Prompt for Project Planning

Type this into ChatGPT: "I'm a 14-year-old student working on a [your project type]. Help me break it into 5 clear steps I can complete in 1 week. Be specific."
Use the response as your starter plan — then customise it!

Section 03

Start Building 🔨

Tips for getting into the zone and making progress every single day.

🎯

Use Focus Mode

Phone face down, notifications off, 25-minute Pomodoro timer on. One task at a time. Your brain can't multitask — don't try.

🤖

AI is Your Assistant

Use AI to brainstorm, draft, and suggest — but YOU make the final decisions. AI is a helper, not a replacement. Your voice and ideas must shine through.

📝

Rough Draft First

Don't try to make it perfect on the first attempt. Get SOMETHING on paper/screen first. "Done is better than perfect" — you can edit later.

🙋

Ask for Help

Stuck? Ask. Nobody builds great things alone. Your teacher, classmates, and even AI are resources. Using them is smart, not weak.

⏰

Work in Daily Sessions

30 minutes every day beats 3 hours one night before the deadline. Consistency creates quality. Set a daily project time.

🌟

Think About Your Audience

Who will see this? What do they care about? Design everything for THEM. A project that helps your audience is always better than one that just ticks boxes.

🏗️

Start Right Now

Open a new document/slide/canvas RIGHT NOW. Write your project title, your name, and one sentence describing what you're building. That's your start. Progress beats perfection.

The students who start today will have something to show next session. The ones who wait until "later" will be scrambling. Your future self is watching what you do in the next 10 minutes.

You're not just a student doing homework. You're a creator, building something real. 🏗️

Section 05

Take-Home: Keep Building 🚀

Your project doesn't stop when the session ends. Here's what to do before Session 11!

📋 Between Session 10 and 11

Your mission: Complete at least 60% of your capstone project before Session 11 (Peer Review).

  • Complete your research (at least 3 solid sources)
  • Create a first draft of your main content
  • Build your presentation structure (intro, body, conclusion)
  • Have at least 5 slides or equivalent content ready
  • Bring your laptop or printed version to Session 11 for peer review

📌 Time estimate: 2–3 hours total spread across multiple days. Don't cram it all the night before!

👥

Peer Review is Coming

In Session 11, your classmates will give you feedback on your project. Come prepared! The better your draft, the more useful feedback you'll get. This is your chance to make it great before the final showcase.

🌟 Daily Commitment

Work on your project for at least 30 minutes every day between now and Session 11. Set a daily reminder on your phone. Use this prompt if stuck: "Help me write the next section of my [project type] about [your topic]."
