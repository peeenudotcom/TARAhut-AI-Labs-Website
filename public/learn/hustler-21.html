Every local business NEEDS Google My Business. Most either don't have one, or have an incomplete profile. You walk in, set it up, and charge for it. High-value, low-effort service.

4 steps — claim, verify, optimize, maintain. Most businesses never get past step 1.
STEP 1🔍

Search for Existing Listing

Google the business name + city. Check if a GMB profile already exists. Many businesses have auto-generated listings by Google that they never claimed. Claiming an existing listing is faster than creating new.

STEP 2📝

Create or Claim Profile

Go to business.google.com. Sign in with client's Google account (or create one). Click 'Add your business'. Enter exact business name, category, address, phone, website. Be precise — Google cross-references all data.

STEP 3✅

Verification Methods

Google verifies you own the business. Methods: (1) Postcard — 5-14 days, a physical postcard with PIN code. (2) Phone — instant, if available. (3) Email — instant, for some businesses. (4) Video verification — record your storefront. Phone is fastest.

STEP 4⚙️

Initial Configuration

Set correct business hours (including holidays). Add all service areas. Choose primary + secondary categories carefully. Add attributes: wheelchair accessible, Wi-Fi, parking, etc. Each detail boosts visibility in search.

💰

Service Value: GMB Setup

GMB setup + optimization = ₹3,000-5,000 one-time. Monthly management (posts, reviews, updates) = ₹2,000-3,000/month. This is a recurring revenue service that most businesses desperately need.

The 12-Point GMB Optimization Checklist

BASIC📛

Business Name

Exact legal name — no keyword stuffing. "Sharma Electronics" not "Sharma Electronics Best TV Store Ludhiana." Google penalizes keyword-stuffed names. Keep it clean and accurate.

BASIC📂

Primary Category

Most important ranking factor. Choose the MOST specific category. "Electronics Store" not just "Store." Add secondary categories: "Home Appliance Store", "TV Store", "AC Dealer."

CONTACT📞

Phone Number

Use a local number, not toll-free. Consistency matters — same number across all online listings (website, JustDial, Sulekha, Facebook). This is called NAP consistency.

CONTACT🌐

Website Link

Link to your main website. If no website, create a free one through Google directly from the GMB dashboard. Even a single-page site helps credibility and ranking.

DETAIL🕐

Business Hours

Accurate hours including special holiday hours. Update for Diwali, Holi, Eid closures. Nothing worse than a customer arriving to a closed shop because hours were wrong. Set seasonal hours too.

DETAIL📝

Business Description

750 characters maximum. Include keywords naturally: what you sell, where you are, what makes you special. First 250 characters matter most — they show in search. Write it like a mini sales pitch.

EXTRA🏷️

Products & Services

List every product/service with description and price. Google shows these in your listing. A sweet shop should list: Kaju Barfi, Gulab Jamun, Wedding Trays, etc. Be thorough — each listing is a keyword.

EXTRA💳

Attributes

Wheelchair accessible, accepts cards, has Wi-Fi, has parking, women-owned, delivery available, online appointments. Every attribute you add helps appear in filtered searches.

ADVANCED📍

Service Area

For businesses that serve customers at their location (plumber, electrician, caterer), define service area radius. For storefronts, set the exact pin on the map. Verify the pin drops correctly.

ADVANCED🔗

Booking / Menu Links

Add appointment URLs, menu URLs, or order-ahead links. Restaurants can add menu directly. Service businesses can link to booking systems. Reduces friction from search to sale.

PRO❓

Q&A Section

Seed your own Q&A. Ask common questions from the owner's account and answer them. "Do you deliver?" "Yes, free delivery in Ludhiana." This pre-answers customer questions and adds keywords.

PRO📊

UTM Tracking

Add UTM parameters to your website link to track how many visitors come from GMB. Example: website.com?utm_source=google&utm_medium=gmb. Now you can PROVE your value to the client with data.

Photos & Google Posts Strategy

Businesses with 100+ photos get 520% more calls and 2,717% more direction requests. Photos are the #1 engagement driver on GMB. Plus, Google Posts are free micro-ads that appear right in search results.

The GMB Photo Strategy

ESSENTIAL🏪

Exterior Photos (3-5)

Front of shop from different angles and times of day. Helps customers recognize your business when they arrive. Include the signboard, entrance, and parking. Day and night shots are best.

ESSENTIAL🏠

Interior Photos (3-5)

Clean, well-lit shots of the inside. Show the ambiance: sitting area, display shelves, treatment rooms, kitchen. People want to see what it looks like before visiting. Wide-angle works best.

PRODUCT📸

Product/Service Photos (10+)

Your best products, dishes, services. Close-up, well-lit, appetizing. For food: natural light, no flash. For products: clean background. For services: before/after shots work incredibly well.

TEAM👥

Team Photos (3-5)

Owner, staff, team at work. Humanizes the business. People connect with faces. Chef cooking, dentist with patient (with permission), mechanic working on car. Builds trust immediately.

BONUS🎉

Event/Happy Customer (5+)

Events, celebrations, happy customers (with permission). Birthday celebrations at restaurant, wedding cake delivery, store anniversary. Social proof in visual form.

COVER🖼️

Cover + Logo

Cover photo: best shot of the business (720x420px minimum). Logo: clear, high-resolution. These are the first things people see. Make them count — professional quality only.

Google Posts — Free Micro-Ads

📢

What Are Google Posts?

Free posts that appear directly in Google Search and Maps when someone finds your business. Types: What's New, Events, Offers, Products. They appear for 7 days. Post weekly for maximum visibility. Each post can include image, text, and CTA button.

TYPE 1📰

What's New Post

General updates: new menu item, new service, new staff member. "We just introduced Sugar-Free Barfi! Perfect for health-conscious families." Include an appetizing photo and "Learn More" button.

TYPE 2🎪

Event Post

Events with start/end dates: Diwali Sale, Anniversary Celebration, Live Music Night. "Join our Baisakhi special — 20% off all Punjabi dishes, April 13-14!" Creates urgency.

TYPE 3🏷️

Offer Post

Discounts and deals with expiry: "Flat 15% off AC servicing this summer. Valid till June 30. Use code: COOL15." Coupon code optional. "Redeem Offer" CTA button drives action.

TYPE 4🛍️

Product Post

Highlight specific products: "Our Kaju Katli — made with pure kaju, no artificial sweeteners. ₹800/kg. Order online or visit us." Each product post is a searchable listing.

Review Management & Reputation Building

93% of consumers say online reviews impact their purchase decisions. 4.0+ stars means trust. Below 3.5 means people avoid you. Reviews are the single most important local ranking factor after GMB completeness.

⚠️

Never Fake Reviews

Google can detect fake reviews and will penalize or suspend the listing. Never buy reviews, never write reviews yourself, never ask employees to review. Only ask genuine customers. Teach clients the RIGHT way to get reviews.

The 5-Step Review Generation System

STEP 1🔗

Create Short Review Link

In GMB dashboard > Get more reviews > Share review form. Create a short link using bit.ly. Print on receipts, business cards, WhatsApp quick reply. Make it brain-dead easy for customers to leave a review.

STEP 2⏰

Ask at the Right Moment

Ask for review at the peak of satisfaction: right after a great meal, after successful treatment, after delivery. NOT via cold message days later. The moment matters — strike when they are happiest with the service.

STEP 3📱

WhatsApp Review Request

WhatsApp quick reply: "Thank you for visiting! Aapko hamari service kaisi lagi? Agar achhi lagi to please Google pe review dein — [short link]. Aapka ek review hum jaise chhote business ke liye bahut bada support hai."

STEP 4📋

QR Code at Billing

Print QR code that opens the Google review form. Place at billing counter, on table tent cards, on receipts. Customer scans, opens review form, done. Remove all friction. Physical + digital touchpoints.

STEP 5💬

Respond to EVERY Review

Reply to ALL reviews — positive AND negative. Thank positive reviewers by name. Address negative reviews professionally: acknowledge, apologize, offer to fix offline. Never argue publicly. Your response shows future customers how you handle problems.

Google uses 3 core factors. You can control Relevance and Prominence. Distance depends on the searcher's location.
FACTOR 1🎯

GMB Profile Completeness

Fill every field. Name, categories, description, products, services, attributes, hours, Q&A. Google rewards businesses that provide the most information. This is the foundation of everything.

FACTOR 2⭐

Reviews (Quality + Quantity)

More reviews with higher ratings = higher ranking. Recent reviews matter more. Businesses getting consistent new reviews rank higher than those with old reviews. Response rate matters too.

FACTOR 3📋

Citations (Directory Listings)

Your business listed on JustDial, Sulekha, IndiaMart, Yellow Pages, Practo (doctors), Zomato (restaurants), UrbanClap. Each listing is a "citation." More citations with consistent NAP = higher ranking. Like votes of confidence.

FACTOR 4🔗

Website + On-Page SEO

Website with location-specific content: "Best Dentist in Amritsar" page, embedded Google Map, schema markup, mobile-friendly. Website signals reinforce GMB signals. Together they compound your ranking power.

FACTOR 5📱

Google Posts Activity

Regular Google Posts signal to Google that the business is active. Weekly posts improve ranking. Stale profiles get penalized. Activity = relevance in Google's algorithm. Post consistently.

FACTOR 6📸

Photo Engagement

More photos = more engagement = higher ranking. Photos uploaded by the business AND by customers count. Encourage customer photos with location-specific photo spots or props. Regular photo uploads show activity.
