Present Like a Pro

Your project is ready. Now present it in a way that makes people sit up and pay attention. These tips work for course presentations, client pitches, and business meetings.

🎤

The 10-Minute Presentation Structure

Minutes 1-2: The Problem. What challenge did you solve? Make it relatable. "Every coaching centre in Kotkapura writes fee receipts by hand..."
Minutes 3-7: Your Solution. Walk through what you built. Show the actual output. Explain which Claude techniques you used.
Minutes 7-9: Live Demo. Show something working. Open the HTML file. Display the content. This is the wow moment.
Minutes 9-10: What You Learned. Share your biggest insight from the 15-day journey. End with impact: "This saves 10 hours per week" or "This reaches 3x more customers."

💡

What Makes a Presentation Memorable

Show, don't tell. Don't say "I can build websites." Open a browser and show the website you built.
Use numbers. "This tool saves ₹15,000/month" is 10x more powerful than "This tool saves money."
Tell a story. "When I started Day 1, I didn't know what an API was. Today I understand the entire architecture."
Use local context. Punjab references make your project feel real, not theoretical.
End strong. Your last sentence should be memorable. Practice it.
