What's a Brand Kit?

A brand kit is everything a business needs to look, sound, and feel consistent across every touchpoint. When someone sees it, they instantly recognize the brand.

🌟

The Complete Brand Kit Formula

Logo + Colors + Fonts + Social Templates + Video + Audio = Complete Brand Identity

Without a consistent brand kit, a business looks different every time someone sees it. With a brand kit, every post, every video, every voiceover says: "This is us. This is who we are." That recognition builds trust. Trust builds customers.

Visual🔗

Logo

The visual symbol of the brand. Needs to work in color, black & white, large and small. At minimum: primary logo + icon-only version + wordmark.

Visual🎨

Color Palette

3–5 brand colors: primary (main color), secondary (accent), neutral (background/text). Defined as HEX codes so they're always exactly the same.

Visual📔

Typography

1–2 brand fonts: one for headings (bold, personality), one for body text (readable, clean). Consistent font use across all materials builds recognition.

Visual📱

Social Templates

Pre-designed Instagram post templates, Stories, and Reel thumbnails. Team members fill in content — brand always looks the same.

Motion🎥

Brand Video

60-second introduction video: who the business is, what they do, why to choose them, how to contact. The most powerful marketing asset a business can have.

Sound🎤

Audio Identity

Brand voice (the style of speech), IVR greeting, background music loop, and optionally a brand jingle. The sound of the brand reinforces the visual identity.

Section 03

Step-by-Step Execution Plan

The exact workflow for building the complete Brand Kit. Follow in order. Each step feeds into the next.

⏱

Time Estimate

Total time in-session: 3–4 hours (or split across sessions). Each step below shows the recommended time. With practice, this reduces to 90 minutes per brand kit — meaning you can deliver 2 complete kits per day as a freelancer.

Step 1 — 30 min

Logo Concepts with Ideogram AI

Generate 5–10 logo concepts for your chosen business. Use the business name in quotes for text-in-image. Try different styles: Design, 3D Render, Minimalist, Vintage. Pick the best 3 variations — primary (full color), secondary (dark background version), icon-only. Export as PNG with transparent background.

Tool: ideogram.ai → Style: Design → Aspect Ratio: 1:1 → "Put 'Business Name' in the prompt"
Step 2 — 45 min

Social Graphics with Canva AI

Build the social media template library. Create: 3 Instagram post templates (different content types — promotional, educational, social proof), 2 Story templates, 1 Reel thumbnail template. Use Magic Media for images, Magic Write for copy, and apply a consistent color palette across all. Save the Canva project — this is the client's ongoing template set.

Tool: canva.com → Magic Media (images) + Magic Write (copy) + Brand Kit (colors)
Step 3 — 45 min

Product/Brand Video with HeyGen

Script the 60-second brand introduction with Claude first. Then open HeyGen — choose an avatar that matches the brand personality. Paste the Claude script. Select background (professional for clinic, warm for restaurant). Generate the video. Import into CapCut: add captions, background music, logo watermark, and the logo from Step 1 as a lower-third. Export at 1080p.

Workflow: Claude (script) → HeyGen (avatar video) → CapCut (edit + captions + logo)
Step 4 — 30 min

Brand Audio with ElevenLabs + Suno

Create three audio assets: (1) Brand voiceover — 45-second brand story narration in ElevenLabs. (2) IVR greeting — 10-second welcome message. (3) Brand music loop — 15-30 seconds of background music from Suno AI that matches the brand mood. Blend voiceover + music in CapCut at 70/30 volume ratio. Export as MP3.

Tools: elevenlabs.io (voice) + suno.com (music) + CapCut (mix)
Step 5 — 30 min

Compile the Brand Kit Package

Create a folder: "BrandKit_[BusinessName]_[YourName]". Organize into sub-folders: /Logo (3 variations), /Social Templates (5 Canva files or exported PNGs), /Video (brand video MP4), /Audio (voiceover MP3, music MP3, IVR MP3), /Brand Guide (one-page PDF from Canva with colors, fonts, logo rules). This is your deliverable.

Final deliverable: Organized folder with all assets + 1-page Brand Guide PDF
📄

Creating the 1-Page Brand Guide (Bonus)

In Canva, create one page that contains: Logo in all 3 variations, the 5 brand colors with their HEX codes, the 2 brand fonts with examples, the brand voice description (3 adjectives: e.g., "Warm, Trustworthy, Local"), and the mission statement (written by Claude). This becomes the client's reference document for all future design decisions.

What "Good Enough" Looks Like

Don't overthink it. Your logo doesn't need to be perfect — it needs to be intentional. Your video doesn't need a Hollywood production — it needs to be clear and consistent. The standard is: If a local business owner saw this, would they feel their business was represented professionally? Yes → Submit. No → Fix one thing and check again.

Final Review Before Submission

  • Open your brand kit folder — look at all assets together. Do they feel like they belong to the same brand?
  • Check: Are all logos using the same colors? Does the video font match the social template font?
  • Ask one person (family member, classmate) to look at your kit. What do they think the business does? If they guess correctly — your branding works.
