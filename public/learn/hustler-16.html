The CRISP Framework

Five letters. Five elements. The difference between amateur AI use and professional-grade outputs that clients pay thousands for.

C🎭

Character

Detailed persona with expertise, years of experience, specialization. Not just "you are a marketer" but "You are a 15-year veteran digital marketing consultant who specializes in scaling local businesses in North India from ₹0 to ₹50K monthly revenue using AI tools."

R📋

Request

Multi-layered task with sub-tasks. "Create a complete 30-day launch plan including daily tasks, content calendar, budget allocation, and KPI tracking." Not just "make a plan" but a structured ask with clear components.

I📊

Information

Rich context: business details, target audience, budget, competitors, constraints. The more specific the information, the more tailored and valuable the output. Include numbers, locations, demographics.

S📝

Steps

Explicit step-by-step instructions for the AI to follow. "Step 1: Analyze the business type. Step 2: Identify 3 target segments. Step 3: Create content pillars..." This prevents AI from skipping important parts.

P✨

Polishing

Output format, tone, length, examples to follow, things to avoid. "Format as a professional PDF-ready document. Use bullet points. Tone: confident but approachable. Include Hindi phrases where natural."

⚠️

Common Mistake: Vague Information Block

The #1 reason CRISP prompts fail is a weak Information block. "A restaurant in Punjab" is vague. "Raju Dhaba in Ludhiana, serving North Indian thalis since 2015, 40 seats, ₹150-300 average order, peak time 12-2pm and 7-9pm" is specific. Specificity = quality.

Practice: Grade This CRISP Prompt

Look at this prompt and identify which CRISP element is missing or weak:

"You are a marketing expert. Create a social media plan for a salon. Make it professional and include posts for the month."

  • Character: "marketing expert" — too vague. Needs specialization and experience level.
  • Request: "social media plan" — needs sub-tasks defined.
  • Information: "a salon" — zero specifics. Where? What services? Budget? Target audience?
  • Steps: MISSING entirely. No step-by-step instructions.
  • Polishing: "professional" and "include posts" — needs format, tone, length specifics.

Chain-of-Thought Prompting

Give AI time to think. Just like you make better decisions when you think step-by-step, AI produces dramatically better outputs when you tell it to reason first.

"Produce output"
AI ko sochne ka mauka do, tabhi accha output aayega.

3 Chain-of-Thought Techniques

TECHNIQUE 1🔗

"Think Step by Step"

Add "Think step by step before answering" to any prompt. Forces AI to show its reasoning. Works especially well for analysis, strategy, and problem-solving tasks.

TECHNIQUE 2📊

"Analyze, Then Create"

"First analyze the business situation, then recommend a strategy, then create the deliverable." Separates thinking from doing. Better structured outputs.

TECHNIQUE 3🔀

"Consider 3 Approaches"

"Consider 3 different approaches for this problem. Evaluate pros and cons of each. Pick the best one and explain why." Gets AI to explore multiple paths before committing.

Generic answer

With CoT

Detailed strategy

Then evaluate 3 different social media strategies (content-heavy, influencer-based, offer-driven). Pick the best one with clear reasoning. Finally, create a detailed 2-week content plan with specific post ideas, captions, and posting times."

Demo 3: Chain-of-Thought for Client Analysis

Use CoT to analyze a real business scenario before recommending services:

Click to copy: "I'm going to describe a local business. Think step by step through this analysis: Business: Lovely Boutique, a women's clothing store in Jalandhar. They have an Instagram with 800 followers but post only 2-3 times a month. No Google listing. No WhatsApp Business. Owner says sales have dropped 20% this year. Step 1: Identify the 3 biggest problems. Step 2: Rank them by impact and ease of fixing. Step 3: Recommend which problem to solve first and why. Step 4: Create a specific action plan for the first 30 days."
This produces a consulting-quality analysis that would cost ₹5,000-10,000 from a marketing agency.
💡

Pro Tip: Combine CRISP + Chain-of-Thought

Put chain-of-thought instructions inside the Steps section of your CRISP prompt. "Step 1: Think about the business context. Step 2: Analyze 3 possible approaches. Step 3: Select the best one with reasoning. Step 4: Create the deliverable based on your analysis." This produces the highest quality outputs possible.

Chain-of-thought turns AI from a content generator into a strategic consultant. Same tool, better instructions.

Mega-Prompts — One Prompt, One Package

A well-crafted mega-prompt can produce in 2 minutes what takes 30 minutes with multiple small prompts. Ek prompt, poora package. Yeh hai professional level.

🏭

Your Prompt Factory

Think of mega-prompts as your factory. Input = client brief. Output = deliverable. You build the factory once, then run different client briefs through it. Each run produces a package worth ₹5,000-25,000.

Demo 4: Complete Social Media Strategy Mega-Prompt

This single prompt produces a full client deliverable. Copy and test it:

Click to copy: "[CHARACTER] You are a senior social media strategist with 12 years of experience working with local businesses across North India. You specialize in turning zero-presence businesses into thriving online brands using organic content and WhatsApp marketing. [REQUEST] Create a COMPLETE social media marketing strategy that includes: (a) Target audience analysis, (b) Content pillars and themes, (c) 30-day content calendar with specific post ideas, (d) 5 ready-to-use sample posts with captions and hashtags, (e) Posting schedule with best times, (f) Monthly budget recommendation. [INFORMATION] Client: Sweet Corner, a traditional mithai shop in Amritsar, Punjab. In business for 25 years. Known locally for peda and barfi. Has a WhatsApp number but no WhatsApp Business. No Instagram, no Facebook page, no Google listing. Monthly marketing budget: ₹5,000-8,000. Target customers: local families, NRI connections, wedding/event planners. Competition: 3 other sweet shops within 1km, all with basic Instagram presence. [STEPS] Step 1: Analyze the target audience segments and their social media behavior. Step 2: Define 4 content pillars. Step 3: Create the 30-day calendar. Step 4: Write 5 sample posts. Step 5: Recommend posting times for Punjab audience. Step 6: Create budget breakdown. [POLISHING] Format as a professional strategy document with clear headers and sections. Use bullet points for action items. Include Hindi/Punjabi phrases where natural. Tone: confident, practical, achievable. Length: comprehensive but scannable."
This 500-word mega-prompt produces a 2,000+ word strategy document that would take a marketing agency 2-3 hours to create manually. You do it in 2 minutes.
